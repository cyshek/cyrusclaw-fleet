## 1. Best date windows in October 2026

October sits at the **end of Bali's dry season** — typically dry/sunny mornings, with afternoon showers becoming more common as the month progresses. Late October starts the wet-season ramp. ([Go2Bali October](https://www.go2-bali.com/bali-in/october/) — avg 32°C/24°C, ~57mm rain across ~10 rainy days; [Bali Holiday Secrets](https://www.baliholidaysecrets.com/bali-in-october/))

Singapore is hot/humid year-round with rain possible any day; October is similar to the rest of the year, slightly wetter than the "dry" Feb-Apr window but not meaningfully different from your Bali experience.

**Key US calendar note:** Indigenous Peoples'/Columbus Day is **Mon Oct 12, 2026** — a federal holiday, but most schools and offices stay open. Mild bump in 3-day-weekend domestic travel, minimal effect on transpacific awards.

### Three candidate windows

| Window | Dates | Why it's good | Why it might not be |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Recommended** | **Sat Oct 3 → Sat Oct 17** | Driest Bali half of the month; pre-Columbus-Day so award space tends to be better; you're home before late-Oct rains | Misses any Galungan/Kuningan ceremony depending on Bali calendar |
| **B — Slightly later** | Sat Oct 10 → Sat Oct 24 | Includes the Mon Oct 12 long weekend (one less PTO day if you're working); shoulder-season hotel pricing dipping | Back half overlaps the wetter end of October in Bali; afternoon showers more frequent |
| **C — Earliest** | Sat Sep 26 → Sat Oct 10 | Driest, end of peak dry season; best surf/beach conditions; tail end of Aug-Sep peak so prices easing | Still slightly busier; less margin if you want to push the trip later |

**Pick A unless your work calendar forces B.** Saturday-to-Saturday matches the SQ SEA–SIN nonstop schedule (currently 5x/week, see flight section).

### Quick refresher on Capital One miles

- Capital One miles transfer **1:1** to most airline partners (Aeroplan, LifeMiles, Turkish Miles&Smiles, Singapore KrisFlyer, EVA Infinity MileageLands, Air France/KLM Flying Blue, etc.). ([Capital One transfer partners](https://www.capitalone.com/learn-grow/money-management/venture-miles-transfer-partnerships/))
- **Boring baseline:** redeem as "purchase eraser" at 1 cpp → **$1,300 of travel** covered. Or via the Capital One Travel portal at the same rate.
- **Smart play:** transfer to a partner whose award chart prices your route well below cash. For SEA ↔ Singapore, the savings are big.

### The route: SEA ↔ SIN

- **8,060 miles each way; ~16h20m nonstop.** Singapore Airlines (SQ) is currently the **only nonstop** operator, on the **A350-900 long-haul**, **5 flights/week** SEA–SIN as of the 2026 summer schedule that runs through **Oct 24, 2026**. ([Mainly Miles SIA Apr–Oct 2026 schedule](https://mainlymiles.com/2026/01/23/singapore-airlines-announces-schedule-through-to-october-2026/), [FlightConnections SEA-SIN](https://www.flightconnections.com/flights-from-sea-to-sin))
- Cabins on this aircraft: Economy and Business only (no Premium Economy or First on this route).
- One-stop options: ANA via NRT/HND, EVA via TPE, Korean via ICN, Cathay via HKG, China Airlines via TPE.

### Recommended redemption plans

#### Plan A — One business-class long-haul leg + economy the other way (best use of 130k for one person)

- **Outbound (fresher, longer trip):** EVA Air business SEA→TPE→SIN, **78,000 LifeMiles** + ~$45 taxes. Look for award space ~330 days out; EVA tends to release 2 J seats per flight reliably.
- **Return:** EVA economy DPS→TPE→SEA, **45,000 LifeMiles** + ~$40 taxes.
- **Total:** **123,000 LifeMiles** (= 123k Capital One miles) + ~$85 cash. **Leaves ~7k miles in reserve.**
- **Estimated value:** ~$5,000–6,000 of cash fares for 123k miles → **~4–5 cpp**, well above the 1 cpp baseline.

#### Plan B — All economy, biggest miles cushion (one person)

- EVA economy both ways: **90,000 LifeMiles** + ~$80 taxes.
- Or: KrisFlyer Saver economy on SQ nonstop both ways = **88,000 KrisFlyer miles** (transfer 1:1). Nonstop, no connection. Leaves **42k miles** untouched (= $420 eraser, or save for next trip).
- **This is the conservative pick if you're solo and don't care about lie-flat.**

#### Plan C — Two travelers in economy

- 4 × 45k LifeMiles = **180k miles needed** → **you're 50k short**. Options:
  - Cover 2 of the 4 legs with LifeMiles (90k), pay cash for the other 2 (~$1,100 total) and use the remaining 40k as a $400 eraser. Net out-of-pocket ~$700.
  - Or transfer to KrisFlyer (88k for both economy seats one direction on SQ nonstop) + cash the other direction.
  - Realistically, **130k is not enough for two people round-trip in economy** at sweet-spot rates. You'll need to add cash or use the eraser.

#### Plan D — Fallback if award space is poor

If LifeMiles/Aeroplan/KrisFlyer all show no Saver business space on your dates:
1. **Use the 130k as a 1cpp eraser ($1,300)** against the cash booking. Pair with a paid flexible economy fare on SQ nonstop. Net economy round-trip out-of-pocket: ~$0–200.
2. Or split: book one direction in cash, one in award.

### SIN → DPS (Singapore → Bali)

- Distance: ~1,040 miles, ~2h45m flight.
- **Cash is almost always the right answer.** Typical fares: SGD $90–250 one-way (~US$70–190).
  - **Scoot** (low-cost, SQ-owned): often US$70–120
  - **Jetstar Asia**: US$80–130
  - **AirAsia**: US$70–110, sometimes cheaper
  - **Singapore Airlines** mainline: US$140–220, higher comfort
  - **Garuda Indonesia**: US$130–200
- **Why not points:** KrisFlyer Saver SIN-DPS is 8k miles + ~$20 taxes — okay value but only worth it if you'd otherwise use those miles at <1cpp. Most travelers just buy this leg with cash.

### DPS → SEA (return)

Same partner analysis as outbound. Garuda also flies DPS-NRT/HND with onward connections, but it's not a Capital One transfer partner so you'd be buying that as a positioning ticket. Stick with EVA via TPE or SQ via SIN (you can backtrack DPS→SIN→SEA on SQ for the same KrisFlyer/LifeMiles pricing as your outbound).

## 3. On-the-ground sketch

### Singapore — 3 nights (arrive Sun, depart for Bali Wed)

A 3-day Singapore visit is plenty for a first-timer to hit the highlights without exhausting it.

**Day-by-day idea:**
- **Day 1 (arrival):** Recover. Marina Bay Sands SkyPark sunset, Gardens by the Bay (Supertree Grove light show 7:45 & 8:45 pm — free), hawker dinner at Lau Pa Sat or Maxwell.
- **Day 2:** Chinatown + Tiong Bahru morning, Singapore Botanic Gardens (UNESCO, free), Little India lunch, Clarke Quay or rooftop bar evening.
- **Day 3:** Sentosa half-day OR Pulau Ubin (offbeat), evening at a Peranakan restaurant in Katong; head to Changi early next morning.

**Hotel neighborhoods (pick one):**

- **Marina Bay** — Iconic but priciest. *Marina Bay Sands* (~US$500–700/night Oct), *Mandarin Oriental* (~$450–650), *The Fullerton Bay* (~$550+). Best for the postcard view.
- **Orchard Road** — Shopping, central, business-traveler standard. *Hilton Orchard*, *Mandarin Orchard*, *Pan Pacific Orchard* (~$250–400). Solid mid-range pick.
- **Chinatown / Tanjong Pagar** — Walkable, food-rich, great hawker access. *Six Senses Maxwell* (~$400+), *Mondrian Duxton* (~$350+), *The Scarlet* (~$180–250). My pick for character.
- **Kampong Glam / Bugis** — Hipster, Arab Street, mid-range value. *The Vagabond Club* (~$180–280). Good for budget-conscious culture.

**Capital One Travel hotel angle:** booking through the C1 portal earns 10x miles per dollar on hotels (and 5x on flights) for Venture X holders, plus you can apply your $300 annual travel credit there. ([source](https://www.capitalone.com/credit-cards/venture-x/)) Not life-changing on a 3-night stay but worth checking — for a $300/night × 3 = $900 spend you'd pocket 9,000 miles. **Caveat:** portal prices aren't always the best; compare against direct/Hotels.com.

### Bali — ~9–10 nights

**Recommended split for a first/second-time visitor:**

| Area | Nights | Vibe | Stay |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ubud** | 3 | Jungle, rice terraces, yoga, art, cooler, peaceful | Mid: *Adiwana Resort Jembawan* (~$130), *Komaneka at Bisma* (~$280). High: *Como Shambhala*, *Mandapa Ritz-Carlton Reserve* ($800+). |
| **Canggu or Seminyak** | 3 | Beach, surf, restaurants, café/digital-nomad scene | Canggu mid: *The Slow* (~$250), villas $150-400. Seminyak: *The Legian* (~$400), *W Bali* (~$450). |
| **Uluwatu (Bukit)** | 3 | Cliffs, dramatic beaches, sunset surf, more upscale-resort | Mid: *Suarga Padang Padang* (~$200), *Mu Bali* (~$250). High: *Six Senses Uluwatu* (~$700), *Bulgari Resort Bali* ($1,200+). |
| **Nusa Penida day trip** | 0 (day trip from Sanur or Uluwatu) | Iconic Kelingking cliff, Angel's Billabong; touristy but unmissable views | Book a private driver+boat day or a small-group tour ~US$80–150/pp |

**Suggested order:** Singapore → fly to Bali → **Ubud first** (acclimatize away from the chaos), then **Canggu/Seminyak** (transit + beach culture), then **Uluwatu** (cliff sunsets, easy taxi to DPS airport for departure). Reverse if you'd rather end on the calm note.

**Villa vs. hotel:**
- **Villas** (1–4 BR with private pool) shine in Bali — better $/space than hotels, especially Canggu/Seminyak/Uluwatu. Look on Airbnb, Booking.com, or boutique sites like *The Bali Villas*. Mid-range private-pool 1BR villa: $120–250/night. With 2+ travelers, villas often beat hotels on value.
- **Hotels** win for service, breakfast, daily housekeeping, pool/bar scene, and zero logistics. In Ubud especially, a good resort with a jungle-edge pool is half the experience.

**Rough nightly budgets (per room, mid-range 4-star equivalent):**
- Ubud: $120–250
- Canggu/Seminyak: $150–350
- Uluwatu: $200–500
- Comfort/luxury tier: 1.5–3× those numbers

### Getting around Bali

- **Private driver for the day:** ~US$50–70 for 8–10 hours, hands-down the best way to do longer transfers and day trips. Ask your hotel.
- **Grab/Gojek** for short rides in Canggu/Seminyak/Uluwatu. Avoid in Ubud (turf disputes).
- **Scooter** if you're confident — not recommended for first-timers, traffic is chaotic and accidents are common.

## 5. Open questions for you

Before I refine this:

1. **Solo or with a partner / group?** Changes the points math entirely (130k covers one person comfortably, two people barely).
2. **Cabin class preference:** lie-flat business one direction (Plan A) vs. all-economy with miles to spare (Plan B)?
3. **Tolerance for connections / red-eyes:** willing to do EVA via Taipei (saves miles) vs. SQ nonstop (more KrisFlyer miles, no stop)?
4. **Hotel vs. villa preference in Bali**, and any non-negotiables (private pool, breakfast included, walkable to beach, etc.)?
5. **Budget feel:** are you trying to keep cash spend under a target (e.g., "$3k all-in")?
6. **Dietary needs / allergies?** Affects restaurant picks especially in Ubud.
7. **Activities you actually care about:** surf? yoga/wellness? temples? scuba/snorkel? party scene? hiking (Mt. Batur sunrise)? Helps slot Bali areas to days.
8. **Are you flexible on the 14-day total?** If you can flex by ±2 days, award availability gets much easier.
9. **Do you already have any other airline accounts seeded** (existing KrisFlyer/Aeroplan/LifeMiles balances)? Could change which transfer partner is best.
10. **Are you open to booking flights ~330+ days out** to lock in EVA J space, or do dates need to firm up later?
