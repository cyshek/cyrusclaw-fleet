## What is your experience working in dashboards as a Product Manager?

My most direct dashboard experience came during my first PM internship at Microsoft, where I designed and built a Power BI dashboard tracking launch toil across 140+ teams spanning Engineering, Ops, and GTM. That wasn't a reporting exercise - it was the primary surface leadership used to make roadmap trade-offs and prioritize automation investment. I defined the metrics, worked with engineering on the data pipeline, and iterated on the layout based on stakeholder feedback until it surfaced the right signal at the right time. It directly influenced a multi-quarter roadmap reprioritization.

More broadly, my work at Microsoft has centered on giving operators visibility and control over complex workflows. The internal resilience platform I built as a full-time PM standardized operator scheduling and monitoring workflows, reduced manual toil by 30%, and gave cross-org teams a self-service interface to manage their own execution cycles. That's essentially the same problem a merchant dashboard solves - taking a complicated backend system and presenting it as a clear, actionable control center. I also built an internal AI agent that restructured planning workflows, cutting cycle time by 39%, which gave me hands-on experience with surfacing insights and growth opportunities through a product interface rather than buried in a spreadsheet.

Earlier, at Pro Painters, I redesigned the end-to-end operator workflow for 200+ monthly proposals, which increased job bookings by 26%. That work required me to think carefully about how small business operators actually move through a day - what they need to see, when they need to see it, and where friction kills momentum. That operator empathy is what I'd bring directly to the Merchant Dashboard role at SpotOn.
