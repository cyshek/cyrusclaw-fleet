What draws me to YipitData specifically is the ambition of the product bet. Building an AI-powered interface that fundamentally changes how clients interact with alternative data is a hard problem, and it's the kind of problem I want to work on. Alternative data is inherently messy, biased, and coverage-uneven, and getting an AI product to reason responsibly across those sources requires exactly the kind of methodology ownership and quality discipline I've been building toward. The fact that this role sits at the intersection of data, product, and AI, rather than being siloed into one of those three, is what makes it the right next step for me.
