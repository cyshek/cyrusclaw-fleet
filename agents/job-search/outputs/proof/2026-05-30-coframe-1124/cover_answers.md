## What's the most impressive thing you've ever built?

At Microsoft, I led the 0-to-1 build of Azure's first proactive resilience testing capability, shipping it in 4 months with a 94% recovery rate. Before this existed, validating that Azure could actually recover from regional failures was a manual, 2-person operation. I turned it into a platformized system that now sustains 45+ enterprise drills per year and has driven over $14M in business impact, including a sovereign-cloud network isolation test tied to a $1.5B+ enterprise contract where I was bridge lead.

What made it hard was the breadth. I had to write the product requirements, instrument telemetry pipelines to catch hardware defects mid-experiment, design self-service workflows that cut operational toil 30%, and coordinate execution across 14 cross-org teams under exec visibility. Customers like Databricks, Walmart, SAP, and NetApp run on it now.

The part I'm proudest of is the AI agent I built on top of it for drill planning. It restructured the planning workflow with LLM-powered automation, cut planning cycle time 39%, and grew drill capacity 21%, which is what convinced me that agent-driven execution on real production surfaces is the direction worth betting on.
