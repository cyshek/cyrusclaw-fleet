## What excites you most about working in AI today?

The thing that excites me most is how quickly AI is collapsing the gap between intent and execution. At Microsoft, I built an internal AI agent with LLM-driven planning workflows that cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased drill capacity by 21%. Seeing a team's throughput jump like that, not from more headcount but from giving people a smarter interface to their own work, convinced me this is the most leveraged place to be building right now.

What draws me to Gradial specifically is that you're applying this exact dynamic to marketing and creative ops, where the pain is real and the workflows are messy enough that generic tools break down. The FDE role sits where I do my best work: in front of enterprise customers, mapping nuanced processes into generative systems, then shipping the code that makes it real. That loop, discovery to prototype to production with the customer in the room, is what I want to be doing every day.
