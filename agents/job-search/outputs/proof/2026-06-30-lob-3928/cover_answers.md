# Cover answers — Lob, Product Manager (lob-8027342)

## What originally sparked your curiosity in Product Management, and what is an area of product sense that you are looking to grow?

My curiosity in Product Management started during my first internship at Pro Painters, where I owned end-to-end scoping and invoicing workflows for 200+ monthly proposals. I wasn't a PM by title, but I was doing the work - talking to customers, identifying where the process broke down, building a CRM workflow to fix it, and then running profitability analysis across 1,000+ project records to surface margin leaks and improve pricing. Seeing how a well-scoped product decision could directly change business outcomes got me hooked. From there, each role reinforced it. At Microsoft, I ran Jobs-To-Be-Done interviews, built roadmaps from scratch, and eventually owned the product delivery for a platform driving $14M+ in business impact. The part I kept coming back to was the intersection of customer pain and technical possibility - figuring out what's worth building and why.

The area I'm most focused on growing is pricing and monetization strategy. I've had adjacent exposure - I built dynamic invoicing logic at Pro Painters, and at Microsoft I navigated complex stakeholder trade-offs on platform decisions with real revenue implications. But I haven't yet owned a full usage billing or discount logic product end-to-end from the PM seat. That's exactly why this role at Lob caught my attention. The chance to go deep on usage billing, pricing simulations, and dynamic discount logic - while also developing fluency with accounting workflows and financial tooling - is the kind of focused, high-stakes domain work that I know will sharpen a part of my product sense that's still developing. I want to build that muscle deliberately, not stumble into it later.
