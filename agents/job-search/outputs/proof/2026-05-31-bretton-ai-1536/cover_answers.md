## What excites you about working at Bretton AI?

At Microsoft I spent the last two years deploying and integrating Azure resilience validation across enterprise customers like Databricks, Walmart, and SAP, scaling a 2-person operation into a platformized system that sustains 45+ annual drills and drove $14M+ in business impact. A lot of that work was exactly what an FDE does: travelling into complex regulated environments, working through last-mile integration challenges, partnering with technical and non-technical stakeholders to translate their workflows into how a platform actually behaves, and then turning those one-off solutions into repeatable capabilities. I also built and shipped an internal AI agent powered by LLMs that automated drill planning and cut planning cycle time by 39%, so building agentic systems that solve real operational problems is something I genuinely enjoy.

Bretton sits right at the intersection I care about: shipping LLM-powered agents into high-stakes, regulated financial environments where the bar for correctness and compliance is unforgiving. AML and counter-terrorist financing work is mission-critical and unglamorous in the best way, and getting agents to reflect how a BSA officer or AML analyst actually thinks is a hard, worthwhile problem. The fact that companies like Robinhood, Mercury, and Gusto already trust the platform tells me the product is real and the customers are serious, and a forward deployed role here lets me do the part of the job I like most: sitting with customers, untangling the messy edge cases that only show up in production, and feeding what I learn straight back into the product.
