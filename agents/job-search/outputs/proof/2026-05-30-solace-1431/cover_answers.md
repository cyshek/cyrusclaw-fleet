## Share one example of a Product Experience in healthcare that's good. Why?

One Medical's app and care model is the example I keep coming back to. Booking a same-day appointment, messaging a provider, and seeing your records all live in one clean flow, and the experience is built around the patient's question rather than the system's internal structure. The thing I respect most is that it doesn't dump complexity on you. It hides the messy backend (scheduling, insurance, referrals) behind a calm, consumer-grade interface, and it pairs that software with actual humans who follow up.

That combination is why it works. I've spent my time at Microsoft learning that the hard part of a good product isn't surfacing more data, it's deciding what to hide and when to put a person in the loop. When I migrated documentation to an AI-powered semantic search tool and cut lookup time 83%, the win wasn't the AI, it was that people stopped having to understand the underlying structure to get an answer. One Medical does the same thing for patients, and it's the closest analog I've seen to what Solace is building: software that removes navigation burden and a real advocate behind it.
