## [Optional] What is one feature you would be excited to add or improve?

I would focus on memory continuity across conversations. Right now, one of the biggest breaks in immersion is when a character loses context between sessions - you have to re-establish shared history, and that friction reminds users they are talking to a system rather than someone who actually knows them. Persistent, character-specific memory that surfaces naturally (not as a clunky 'here is what I remember' recap) would deepen emotional engagement meaningfully.

The product challenge is not purely technical. It is about what to remember, what to surface, and how to make retrieval feel like genuine recall rather than a database lookup. At Microsoft, I worked on an AI-powered semantic search tool that improved information retrieval by restructuring how metadata was stored and surfaced, cutting lookup time by 83%. That same design thinking applies here - the architecture of what gets retained and when it gets shown shapes whether the experience feels alive or mechanical.

Done well, persistent memory turns a good conversation into a relationship. That is the kind of feature that moves session depth and retention, not just in aggregate metrics, but in the way users talk about the product to other people.
