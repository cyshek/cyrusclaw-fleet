## Describe a project where you had significant ownership in building a product, feature, or system. Be specific about what you built, the key decisions you made, your level of ownership, and the outcome.

At Microsoft, I owned the 0to1 development of an internal Resilience Automation Platform from discovery through launch and post-launch measurement. The starting point was a manual, 2-person validation effort that didn't scale. I made the call to platformize it rather than keep patching the manual process, which meant writing the product requirements, user stories, and acceptance criteria for a self-service scheduling system that engineering and design could build to. That decision turned a fragile one-off effort into a system sustaining 45+ annual drills and driving $14M+ in business impact across enterprise customers.

One of the key bets I drove was pioneering Azure's first proactive rack-level testing capability. I got there by identifying engagement gaps using qualitative research and behavioral data, then prioritizing this capability over other backlog items. We shipped it in 4 months and hit a 94% recovery rate, which surfaced critical defects early and established a continuous validation pattern teams kept coming back to. I also built an internal AI agent to support planning, defining success metrics with our Data team to instrument the flows. That cut planning cycle time 39% and increased drill capacity 21%.

My level of ownership here was end to end. I defined the problem, scoped the work, ran the cross-functional execution, and stayed accountable for outcomes rather than just shipping features. At one point I was driving 14 active recovery projects under executive visibility, managing scope, timelines, and dependencies, including acting as bridge lead on a sovereign-cloud isolation test tied to a $1.5B+ contract. The throughline was using post-launch data to find issues, measure results, and keep iterating.
