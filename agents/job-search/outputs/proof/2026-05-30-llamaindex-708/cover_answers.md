# Cover answers — Llamaindex, Applied AI Solutions Architect (SF) (llamaindex-84bcd1df-a4da-421d-b325-45684d8b7e4d)

## Which excites you most about working at LlamaIndex?

The chance to sit at the intersection of deep technical work and enterprise GTM is what pulls me in. At Microsoft, I've spent the last two years owning technical relationships with customers like Databricks, Walmart, SAP, and NetApp, running discovery, designing PoCs, and turning skeptical partner engineering teams into champions on a rack-level resilience program that hit a 94% recovery rate and drove $14M+ in business impact. That's exactly the motion this role describes, and I want to do it full-time on AI infrastructure instead of cloud resilience.

Document understanding is the unsexy bottleneck that quietly blocks half of the enterprise AI rollouts I see. When I migrated internal docs to a RAG-style semantic search system as an intern, lookup time dropped 83%, and I watched firsthand how much downstream LLM quality depends on getting retrieval and parsing right. LlamaIndex is making the bet that this layer deserves a serious platform, and I'd rather be the person in the room helping ambitious AI teams architect around accuracy, latency, and cost trade-offs than building yet another demo.

The other thing: I like being close enough to the deal to feel the pressure and close enough to the product to actually shape the roadmap. Feeding customer reality back into Product and Engineering is something I already do at Microsoft, and doing it at an earlier-stage company where that signal moves faster is the part I'm most excited about.
