## Tell us about something you've built/done that you think is genuinely cool, big or small, work or personal.

At Microsoft, I built Azure's first proactive resilience testing program from scratch. Before this existed, resilience validation was reactive and ad hoc. I designed and delivered a rack-level drill in 4 months, hit a 94% recovery rate on the first run, and surfaced real hardware defects that would have otherwise gone undetected until a production incident. That part felt genuinely cool to me because it was a case where the program itself created value that nobody knew was missing.

What made it stick was the governance layer I built around it. Milestone gates, decision logs, change control, cross-org review cadences across 6+ stakeholder organizations. I also built playbooks and templated the whole thing so it could repeat without me holding it together manually. The program scaled from a 2-person operation to 45+ annual drills, and the accumulated impact landed at $14M+ in business value. I also built an AI agent that cut planning cycle time by 39% so engineers could spend their time on actual engineering rather than coordination overhead.

The piece I'm most proud of is that it became a platform, not a one-time project. It runs, it scales, and it doesn't depend on any single person to function. That's the version of program management I find interesting to do.
