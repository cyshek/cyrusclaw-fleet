## How have you incorporated AI tools or technologies into your daily work or projects to improve efficiency or outcomes? Please provide a specific example, including the tool used and the impact it had on your work.

At Microsoft, I built an internal AI agent designed to restructure how my team handled planning workflows for our resilience and integration programs. The agent automated the generation and sequencing of planning artifacts that previously required significant manual coordination across multiple teams and source systems. Using Copilot Studio and custom LLM-powered tooling, I connected it to our internal data sources so it could surface relevant context, draft structured plans, and flag dependency conflicts without requiring constant human input at each step.

The impact was measurable. Planning cycle time dropped by 39%, and our capacity to execute integration drills increased by 21% without adding headcount. That freed my team to focus on higher-value work like defining data mapping standards, refining canonical schemas, and improving the playbooks that helped implementation teams onboard source systems faster.

Beyond the numbers, building that agent changed how I think about AI in a product context. It is most useful when it handles repeatable, structured work so that people can spend their time on judgment calls, not logistics. That is the same instinct I would bring to ecosystem integrations at FloQast, where scaling consistent, reliable data flows across many source systems requires exactly that kind of disciplined automation thinking.
