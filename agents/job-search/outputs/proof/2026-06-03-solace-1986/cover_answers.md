## Why does Solace's mission resonate with you?

The stat that 88% of U.S. adults can't navigate healthcare without help is staggering, and it tracks with what I've seen up close. Healthcare is a maze of red tape, and when people are already sick or scared, expecting them to be expert navigators on top of that is a setup for bad outcomes. Pairing patients with advocates and giving them real tools is exactly the kind of leverage that changes lives, not just metrics.

My own work has been about cutting through complexity and toil so the people on the other end can actually get things done. At Microsoft I took an internal platform from 0 to 1 that cut operational toil 30% by replacing manual, error-prone processes with self-service tools. That instinct, find where the system is breaking people down and remove the friction, is the same instinct Solace is applying to healthcare. I'd much rather point that energy at something that helps patients get better care than at almost anything else.

I also like that Solace is intense and mission-driven and doesn't pretend otherwise. I've done my best work on aggressive timelines where the stakes were real, and I want to keep doing that somewhere it genuinely matters.

## Share one example of a Product Experience in healthcare that's good. Why?

One Medical's app experience stands out to me. Booking is fast, you can message a provider and get a real answer without a phone tree, and your visit notes and follow-ups are all in one clear place. It treats the patient like a consumer who deserves a delightful, low-friction experience instead of a case number stuck in an outdated portal.

What makes it good is that it removes friction at the exact moments people are most stressed. The hard part of healthcare UX is that the user is often anxious or unwell, so every extra step costs more than it would in a normal consumer app. One Medical earns retention by making each touchpoint feel handled, which is the same bar I try to hold in my own work, where I've focused on shipping low-friction experiences that drive adoption and keep people coming back. That patient-as-consumer mindset is exactly what Solace is building toward, and it's why I think advocacy plus good product can move outcomes in a way neither does alone.
