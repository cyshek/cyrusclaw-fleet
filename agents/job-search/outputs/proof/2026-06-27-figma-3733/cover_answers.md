# Cover answers — Figma, Product Manager, CMS (figma-6092802004)

## Why do you want to join Figma?

A lot of my work at Microsoft has been about building systems that let teams operate at scale without needing to manually coordinate every step. When I led development of our internal Resilience Automation Platform, the core challenge was the same one Figma's CMS team is tackling: how do you take something that works for a small team and make it work for hundreds of teams, without losing the flexibility that made it useful in the first place? I also spent time during my 2023 internship improving metadata standards and migrating documentation to a semantic search tool, cutting lookup time by 83% across a large internal content system. That experience gave me a real appreciation for how much friction bad content and asset management creates, and how much value you unlock when you get it right.

Figma sits at an interesting intersection I don't think any other product owns: the design-to-production handoff, where content, assets, and components all have to stay in sync. Most CMS and DAM tools treat that handoff as someone else's problem. Figma has a real shot at solving it natively, and the technical bet on headless content management and open source tooling is exactly the right direction. I want to work on a product that engineers and designers actually rely on every day, and help shape what it means to manage digital content at the scale Figma operates.

I am currently based in Kirkland, WA. I am open to relocating to San Francisco or New York if that is the right fit for the role, and I am also happy to work remotely from my current location if that works for the team. Flexible on the arrangement.
