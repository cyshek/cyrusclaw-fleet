## **About Edia**

Our mission is for every child to have an ***exceptional***** **experience in school.

Edia is a Series A technology startup revolutionizing K-12 education with AI-driven solutions. We develop software to improve math education, tackle chronic absenteeism, and improve intervention processes for school districts. Today, Edia supports 200+ districts across the country (e.g. New York City, Denver, Fulton County, San Francisco, etc.).

## **About the role**

Our customers hold us to a high bar, and it's our responsibility to create powerful, intuitive solutions that feel cohesive. The quality of your product decisions will have a direct impact on the confidence that school administrators have in us to solve their most important needs, especially as we utilize AI in completely new ways to rethink their workflows.

As a product manager at Edia, you'll have complete ownership to scope, define, and ship products 0 to 1. You'll be the CEO of your product—responsible not just for defining what to build, but for driving it to success. The ideal candidate is extremely smart, high-agency, has great taste, and is excited to shape how millions of students across the country experience school.

**What You'll Do**

- Work with the CEO to shape an insanely compelling product vision

- Meet with 100+ prospective clients to deeply understand their problems and validate solutions

- Define product requirements, prioritize ruthlessly, and drive alignment across teams

- Build functional prototypes and front-ends yourself using AI coding tools—you should be able to vibe code your way to a working product

- Refine products with great attention to detail

- Work with marketing to communicate that vision and captivate customers

- Work tightly with engineering to ship high-quality products on time

- Use data and customer feedback to iterate and expand key workflows

- Define success metrics and hold yourself accountable to outcomes

**Qualifications**

- You've built something great, ideally from scratch

- 5+ years of product management / product design experience in software

- Ability to build your own front-ends (or get pretty far) using tools like Claude Code, Cursor, or Replit

- Extremely smart, high-agency, and relentlessly resourceful

- Great taste—you know what good looks and feels like

- You're fast and productive—you get thing done

- Excellent written and verbal communication

- Deep care about improving outcomes for students and educators
