## What interests you about Origin?

My work at Microsoft has been fundamentally about embedding with customers, understanding their operational reality, and building custom solutions that make complex infrastructure actually work for them. I led deployments with Databricks, Walmart, SAP, and NetApp, translating unique constraints into custom validation workflows, and I built an internal AI agent that cut planning cycle time by 39% with field learnings feeding directly back into the platform roadmap. That loop of customer-facing engineering, bespoke integration work, and product feedback is exactly what the Forward Deployed Engineer role at Origin is describing, and it is where I do my best work.

What draws me to Origin specifically is the problem space. AI adoption on endpoints is accelerating fast, and most organizations have no real visibility into what their agents are actually doing, whether the actions are traceable, or whether outcomes match intent. Origin's bet that observability has to come before broad AI deployment is the right framing, and building the platform that answers the hardest questions about how work gets done inside an organization is a genuinely hard, genuinely important problem. Backed by Sequoia and operating at the intersection of endpoint security and AI infrastructure, this feels like exactly the kind of early-stage, high-stakes environment where I want to be building.
