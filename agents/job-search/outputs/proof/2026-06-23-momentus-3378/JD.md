**JOB SUMMARY** 

**Key Responsibilities: **

Product Strategy & Ownership 

- Define and own the product roadmap for 3D-printed spacecraft components  

- Translate customer needs into clear product requirements and specifications  

- Serve as product owner, aligning engineering, manufacturing, and business teams  

- Identify and prioritize adjacent product opportunities beyond the initial propellant tank  

Technical & Performance Definition 

- Establish key performance metrics (KPPs/KPIs) including mass efficiency, pressure capability, reliability, manufacturability, and cost  

- Define performance, cost, and schedule targets aligned with market needs and business goals  

- Partner with engineering to ensure designs meet mission and business requirements  

Business Model & Economics 

- Develop and maintain the product business case, including:  

- Unit economics and cost models  

- Pricing strategy  

- Volume assumptions and scaling scenarios  

- Evaluate make vs. buy, vertical integration, and supply chain strategy  

Go-to-Market & Customer Engagement 

- Develop and execute the go-to-market strategy  

- Work closely with sales to:  

- Identify target customers (satellite OEMs, propulsion providers, primes)  

- Support capture efforts and proposals  

- Engage directly with customers to refine requirements and validate product-market fit  

Differentiation & Market Positioning 

- Define the competitive positioning of the product  

- Identify and articulate differentiators (e.g., performance, cost, lead time, integration, reliability)  

- Monitor competitors and industry trends in additive manufacturing and spacecraft hardware  

Program Execution Support 

- Align cross-functional teams around milestones, deliverables, and priorities  

- Track progress against cost, schedule, and technical goals  

- Support risk identification and mitigation strategies 

Required Skills and Experience 

- Demonstrated ability to work across engineering and business functions  

- Strong analytical skills, including cost modeling and trade studies  

- Experience translating technical systems into customer-facing value propositions 

- Proficient in the use of Microsoft Word, PowerPoint and Excel 

- Ability to work independently.  

- Ability to work in multi-disciplinary teams 

- Excellent technical communications skills. 

- Good written and verbal communication skills. 

Desired Skills and Experience: 

- Experience with additive manufacturing (metal AM strongly preferred)  

- Background in spacecraft or launch vehicle development  

- Familiarity with qualification and certification of aerospace hardware  

- Experience in a startup or fast-paced development environment  

Key Traits for Success 

- Technically credible with engineers; can go deep when needed  

- Commercially minded; understands how products win in the market  

- Comfortable with ambiguity; able to build structure from scratch  

- Strong communicator across technical and non-technical stakeholders  

- Bias toward ownership and execution 

Education & Work Experience: 

- Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering or related field  

- MBA or equivalent business experience (a plus, not required) 

- 5+ years of experience in aerospace, ideally with exposure to:  

- Spacecraft propulsion systems  

Why This Role Matters 

This role will define not just a single product, but the foundation of a scalable product line in 3D-printed spacecraft hardware. You will directly shape how advanced manufacturing translates into real, adopted space systems.
