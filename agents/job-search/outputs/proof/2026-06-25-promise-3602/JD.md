**Company Overview** 

Promise modernizes how government agencies and utilities support people in financial difficulty. We build technology that makes it simple for residents to receive benefits, engage with assistance programs, set up flexible payment plans, and stay on track—while helping agencies increase efficiency, recover revenue, and deliver services with dignity. Our mission is to transform public systems so they work better for everyone, especially the most vulnerable.

Our team includes experts from companies like Palantir, Google, Stripe , and esteemed government leaders. We work hard and believe deeply in what we do. We're looking for excellent people to build innovative, resilient technology.

Backed by over $50 million in funding from top investors – such as Reid Hoffman, Howard Schultz, Michael Seibel, Y Combinator, 8VC, The General Partnership, First Round Capital, Kapor Capital, XYZ Ventures, and Bronze Investments – Promise has been recognized as one of Fast Company's "World's Most Innovative Companies of 2022,” “Forbes Next Billion-Dollar Startups 2024,” and Y Combinator’s #1 GovTech startup.

## **About the Role**

Promise is seeking a Forward Deployed Software Engineer who’s driven by impact, excited by challenge, and motivated by Promise’s mission: helping low-income families catch up on past-due bills and access the relief they deserve.

This is not your typical engineering role. You’ll be on the front lines—working hand-in-hand with utilities and government partners, solving complex integration challenges, and making sure our technology actually delivers where it matters most: in people’s lives. You’ll bring strong technical fundamentals together with empathy, patience, and the kind of customer savvy that turns a deployment into a long-term partnership.

## **Role Impact**

In Washington State, Promise distributed $100M of energy aid in just three months, reaching nearly 700,000 households.

In Virginia and Florida, we helped ensure millions of dollars in water assistance funds reached families who might have otherwise gone without.

Our AI-powered eligibility verification now cuts approval times for programs like SNAP and LIHEAP from weeks to hours.

As a Forward Deployed Software Engineer, you’ll be on the front lines making this kind of impact possible.

## **What You’ll Do**

- Be the bridge: Work directly with utilities and government agencies to integrate Promise’s payment and relief products into their existing systems.

- Problem-solve in the wild: Dive into customer environments, untangling complexity and crafting creative technical solutions that actually work in the real world.

- Level up the product: Improve Promise’s products -- you will have the first hand context of what works and doesn't, if you see something to build, build it!

- Travel occasionally (up to 40% of the time): Join customers on-site to make sure things run smoothly and strengthen our relationships in person. 

## **What Will Help You Succeed**

- Engineering chops: 4+ years of experience building and deploying production software, with fluency in environments that require adaptability and fast learning.

- Customer instincts: Experience in a customer-facing technical role where you balanced empathy with technical rigor.

- Tools of the trade: We use JavaScript / TypeScript, ETL tools, SQL, React – you don’t need to be an expert on any of these! You *do* need to come with a willingness to learn.

- Curiosity & grit: You don’t wait for someone to tell you the answer—you explore, experiment, and document so others can follow.

- Collaboration: This is a cross-functional role where you will be working across engineering, design, product, delivery, and sales. FDEs work across team boundaries to get things done.
