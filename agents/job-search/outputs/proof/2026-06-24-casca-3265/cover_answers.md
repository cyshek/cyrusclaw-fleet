## What's a product you love and why? If you were its PM, what would you change and how would you measure the impact?

I love Notion. It nails the balance between flexibility and usability in a way most productivity tools miss entirely. You can build a simple task list or a full internal wiki, and the mental model stays consistent throughout. For knowledge workers managing complex, nonlinear work, that composability is genuinely rare. What keeps me coming back is how fast you can capture something and connect it to existing context without the tool getting in your way.

If I were its PM, the thing I'd fix is the gap between information living in Notion and information actually being findable when it matters. Search and AI assist are improving, but they still feel bolted on rather than native to how people actually navigate their workspace. I'd invest in a proactive context layer, something that surfaces relevant pages based on what you're actively working on, not just what you explicitly search for. This is less about indexing and more about intent modeling. I got a taste of this problem while improving data retrieval speed by 83% through metadata standards and semantic search tooling at Microsoft, so I understand both the technical lift and the product design challenge of making retrieval feel invisible.

To measure impact, I'd track a few things: reduction in time-to-find for knowledge retrieval tasks (testable via session recordings and explicit user research), change in search abandonment rate, and downstream engagement with surfaced content. The north star would be whether users report spending less time hunting and more time doing. Qualitative signals matter here too, especially for something as subjective as 'this tool thinks the way I do.'
