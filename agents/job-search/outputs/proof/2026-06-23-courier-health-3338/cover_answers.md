# Cover answers — Courierhealth, Product Manager (courier-health-4939593007)

## Why are you interested in the Product Manager role at Courier Health?

At Microsoft, I owned end-to-end product development for Azure's Resilience Automation Platform, driving sprint planning, managing stakeholder communication across 14 cross-org executions, and scaling a 2-person operation into a platformized system that drove $14M+ in impact for enterprise customers like Databricks, Walmart, and SAP. That experience maps directly to what this role asks for: owning feature development, coordinating cross-functionally with engineering and client-facing teams, and prioritizing work based on real business value and customer needs.

What draws me to Courier Health specifically is the problem space. Chronic and rare disease patients face genuinely complex journeys, and the gap between diagnosis and sustained therapy is one of the most consequential places software can make a difference. Building engagement tools for life sciences companies that affect patient outcomes is a more meaningful version of the B2B SaaS work I have been doing, and I want to be working on a product where the impact is visible at that level. The focus on patient support and the intersection of CRM-style tooling with healthcare is exactly the kind of domain I want to go deep in.
