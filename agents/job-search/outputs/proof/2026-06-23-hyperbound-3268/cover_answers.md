I led the 0-to-1 development of an internal platform that changed that, defining the product requirements, building self-service scheduling capabilities, and standardizing the onboarding workflows that now support 45 or more annual resilience drills.

What makes me proud of it is not just the output, it is the approach. I did not just automate a task, I redesigned how a program operated. I ran discovery with internal stakeholders to surface what was actually causing toil, translated that into requirements, and shipped something that reduced operational overhead by 30% and delivered $14M or more in business impact across customers like Databricks, Walmart, SAP, and NetApp. It went from a fragile 2-person process to a scalable, self-service model.

That piece was lightweight tooling I built to eliminate repetitive work, which is something I genuinely enjoy, finding the right place to apply automation so people can focus on higher-leverage work. The whole project is a good example of how I think: understand the problem end-to-end, build something that actually changes behavior, and document it so it scales without you.
