## Why NOX METALS? This is an important question that will highly impact our decision making process.

I have spent the last two years owning products end to end at Microsoft, and the part I keep coming back to is shipping fast for people who do physical work. At Amazon Robotics I owned a legacy OS migration across 1,200+ stations and shipped a phased rollout with zero operational downtime during a 2,000+ unit pilot on a live shop floor. I did that by walking the floor myself, sitting with operators, and watching them use what we shipped instead of guessing from a dashboard. On Azure I pioneered the org's first proactive resilience testing by shipping a rack-level v1 in four months over a perfect future design, and ran the weekly product review and operating cadence end to end. That bias to get a v1 live today and iterate is exactly how you describe the job: idea to floor by the end of the week.

Nox Metals appeals to me because you are treating an old industry like a software problem and actually moving metal faster than the industry thought possible. I want to build for operators, schedulers, and buyers whose work shows up on a real customer's dock, not for abstract users behind a screen. NOX NEST, WAYNE, and Gondor running the company, with a PM expected to write tight specs, code lightly, and walk every feature through the factory, is the kind of high-agency, low-ego, ship-it environment where I do my best work. Rebuilding American manufacturing's supply chain in Detroit is a mission I'd be proud to grind on, including outside normal hours when it matters.
