## Tell us about something you've built/done that you think is genuinely cool, big or small, work or personal.

At Microsoft, I built an AI agent that automated drill planning workflows for Azure's infrastructure resilience program. Before it existed, planning a single resilience drill was a manual, multi-day coordination exercise across engineering, datacenter, and networking teams. I scoped the problem, wrote the product requirements, and worked with engineers to ship it. The result was a 39% reduction in planning cycle time and a 21% increase in validation capacity without adding headcount.

What I find genuinely cool about it is the compounding effect. The agent didn't just save time on one drill - it unlocked the ability to run more validations across more infrastructure, including tests tied to sovereign-cloud deployments with strict constraints. It turned a bottleneck into a scaling lever. That kind of force-multiplier outcome, where a single well-scoped tool changes the throughput ceiling for an entire program, is the thing I keep chasing.

On a smaller scale, I also built a Power BI dashboard during an earlier internship that tracked operational toil across 140+ teams. It sounds mundane, but having a single source of truth for where manual effort was piling up let leadership actually prioritize the right automation investments. Sometimes the cool thing is just making invisible work visible.
