## Why Substack?

At Microsoft, I spent the last two years doing something that maps almost directly to this role: taking bespoke, high-stakes requests from enterprise partners like Databricks, Walmart, and SAP and turning them into reusable platform systems rather than one-off solutions. I built a Resilience Automation Platform from scratch, defined self-service capabilities that cut operational toil by 30%, and drove $14M+ in impact by consistently asking "how do we make this durable" instead of "how do we close this ticket." That exact instinct, translating partner-specific pressure into scalable primitives, is what the Enterprise PM role at Substack is asking for.

What draws me to Substack specifically is the bet the company is making: that the best creators deserve a business model built around their relationship with their audience, not around advertisers. Enterprise creators pushing the edge of what the platform can enable is a genuinely interesting forcing function, and I want to be the person who turns that pressure into leverage for every creator on the network. The intersection of platform thinking, creator empathy, and direct executive alignment is where I do my best work, and Substack is one of the few places where all three matter equally.
