## Describe something impressive you've achieved or built

At Microsoft, I built Azure's first proactive rack-level resilience testing capability from scratch. Before this, Azure had no way to validate recovery at the hardware tier before failures hit production customers. I designed the validation architecture, coordinated across infrastructure teams, and stood up a repeatable testing pipeline that achieved a 94% recovery rate within 4 months. That work surfaced critical hardware defects that would have otherwise gone undetected until they caused customer-facing outages.

Around the same time, I scaled what had been a 2-person recovery validation operation into a platformized system capable of sustaining 45+ annual resilience drills across enterprise customers like Databricks, Walmart, SAP, and NetApp. That meant standardizing deployment playbooks, defining integration patterns, and building self-service execution workflows that cut operational toil by 30% - so teams could run drills without needing dedicated engineering support every time.

What I'm most proud of is that both of these started as blank-page problems. There was no playbook, no reference architecture, no team to hand things off to. I had to figure out what needed to exist, build it, and make it repeatable. That's the kind of work I do best.
