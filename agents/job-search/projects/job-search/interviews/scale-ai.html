SCALE AI — Interview Prep Role: PM (Product Manager), Public Sector GenAI (Generative AI) Test & Evaluation (T&E) Locations: SF / St. Louis / NYC / DC

What Scale AI does: Scale AI builds the data and evaluation infrastructure that frontier AI runs on. They started in 2016 doing data labeling for self-driving cars, but today the bulk of the business is providing high-quality training data, RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback), and evaluations to frontier model labs like OpenAI, Anthropic, and Meta — through a global network of vetted experts plus the tooling and ML (Machine Learning) to run that operation at scale.

Their fastest-growing arm is Public Sector — including Donovan, a GenAI platform for defense and intel analysts, and the T&E (Test & Evaluation) tooling that evaluates whether agentic AI is safe and performant enough for mission-critical government use. Customers include the US Army, Air Force, and DoD (Department of Defense) primes.

Founded by Alex Wang. In May 2026, Meta took a ~$14B / ~49% stake and brought Alex over to lead their superintelligence effort; Jason Droege is now interim CEO.

What this role is: Technical PM owning the T&E stack for Public Sector agentic apps — the infrastructure that lets ML teams measure, improve, and prove agent performance for government use. Cross-org work across Infrastructure, ML, and Public Sector teams; “stalled-to-shipped” pattern explicitly called out.

Why I want to leave Microsoft: Two years at Azure has given me strong cross-org execution muscle, but Microsoft moves slowly — decisions that should take a week take a quarter. I want a faster product loop and more ownership of the “what,” not just the “when.”

Why I want to be a PM: The TPM (Technical Program Manager) work I’ve enjoyed most has been PM-shaped — 0→1 building the Resilience Automation Platform, defining requirements, running discovery. I want full ownership of the product decisions, not just execution.

Why Scale, why this role: Scale sits at the center of how frontier AI gets built and deployed, and evaluating agentic AI for federal use is one of the most important unsolved problems in the space right now. The work — building infrastructure that measures and proves AI performance — is exactly the shape of what I did with Azure’s proactive resilience testing.

Why me — mapping to their bar: - Engineering depth: 3 yrs Azure TPM in technical infra — read code, sit in design reviews, partner deeply with engineering. - Evaluation systems: Built Azure’s first proactive resilience testing infrastructure (94% recovery rate) — same problem shape as GenAI evals, different substrate. - Problem distillation: Turned vague “drills are too manual” into a roadmap with $14M+ impact and 30% toil reduction. - Stalled-to-shipped: Proactive rack-level testing (0→4mo), sovereign-cloud isolation test ($1.5B+ on the line), Resilience Automation Platform (2-person op → platform). - Cross-functional: Drills span Compute / Networking / Storage / Datacenter engineering plus customer engineering at Databricks, Walmart, SAP, NetApp. - Exec reporting: Weekly delivery + blockers to leadership is the rhythm.

Questions I’ll ask: - What does the T&E stack look like today — what’s in production for Public Sector, and what’s the biggest gap I’d own day one? - How do you draw the line between commercial Data Engine evals and Public Sector T&E? - Who are the primary stakeholders on the government side, and how does evaluation signal actually drive their decisions? - Post-Meta investment + Alex’s transition — how is product strategy on the Public Sector side evolving under Jason? - What’s the relationship between this T&E work and Donovan — does T&E gate Donovan releases? - “Stalled to shipped” was explicit in the JD (Job Description) — what’s currently stalled that this role would inherit? - What does “great” look like 6 months in?
