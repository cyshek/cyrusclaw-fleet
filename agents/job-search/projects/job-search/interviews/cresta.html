CRESTA — Interview Prep Tue May 26, 9:30am PDT Role: Platform Product Manager (R-5114374008) — Remote, US Comp: $130K–$190K base + equity

What Cresta does: Cresta builds enterprise AI for the contact center. Their platform does three big things: real-time agent assist (whispering suggestions to reps mid-call), AI agents that handle full conversations end-to-end, and conversation intelligence / analytics that surface insights from millions of calls. They train their own models on contact-center-specific data, which is their main moat against generic LLM (Large Language Model) wrappers.

Born from the Stanford AI lab. Chairman Sebastian Thrun (Google X, Waymo, Udacity). CEO Ping Wu co-founded Google CCAI (Contact Center AI) and Vertex AI. Co-founder Tim Shi was early at OpenAI. Backed by a16z, Greylock, Sequoia. Customers include Intuit, Cox, Hilton, and CarMax.

What this role is: Platform PM (Product Manager) owning Cresta’s foundational platform capabilities, starting with IAM (Identity and Access Management) — authentication, authorization, user management, access control, and auditability. Sits at the center of how the enterprise AI platform scales, supports both human workflows and AI agents, and likely expands to other shared platform capabilities over time. They explicitly say day-one IAM expertise isn’t required; they want product judgment, technical fluency, and bias to action.

Why I want to leave Microsoft: Two years at Azure has given me strong cross-org execution muscle, but Microsoft moves slowly — decisions that should take a week take a quarter. I want a faster product loop and more ownership of the “what,” not just the “when.”

Why I want to be a PM: The TPM (Technical Program Manager) work I’ve enjoyed most has been PM-shaped — 0→1 building the Resilience Automation Platform, defining requirements, running discovery. I want full ownership of the product decisions, not just execution.

Why Cresta, why this role: Cresta is real deployed enterprise AI with paying customers and measurable ROI (Return on Investment), not a demo, and the leadership has actually shipped AI platforms before (Google CCAI, Vertex, OpenAI). Owning IAM for a product where AI agents are first-class users is a frontier problem most companies haven’t had to solve yet, and I want in on that.

Why me — mapping to their bar: - Technical fluency: 3 yrs Azure TPM in technical infra — comfortable in APIs (Application Programming Interfaces), architecture, tradeoffs, and implementation detail with engineering. - Product judgment under ambiguity: 0→1 Resilience Automation Platform and 0→1 proactive rack-level testing program — both systems-level, ambiguous, high-stakes. - Ownership / bias to action: $14M+ business impact, 30% toil reduction, 39% faster planning cycles. - Cross-functional: Drills span Compute / Networking / Storage / Datacenter engineering plus customer engineering at Databricks, Walmart, SAP, NetApp simultaneously. - Enterprise instinct: Have operated in regulated, audit-heavy environments at Azure scale — I know what “enterprise-ready” actually means.

Questions I’ll ask: - IAM is the starting point — what’s the next platform capability you’d hand to this role after IAM is in a good place? - How is Cresta thinking about IAM for AI agents vs. humans? Where do those models diverge? - What’s the biggest open product question on IAM today that you’d hand to whoever takes this seat? - How does the Platform PM team partner with the AI/model teams — where do platform tradeoffs (auth, multi-tenancy, audit) get prioritized vs. AI-feature work? - How “enterprise-ready” is Cresta today vs. where it needs to be in 12 months? - What’s the partnership look like with Security and Solution Architects in practice? - What does “great” look like 6 months in?
