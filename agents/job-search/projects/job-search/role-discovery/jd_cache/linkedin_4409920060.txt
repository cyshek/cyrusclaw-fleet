# Solution Architect

HAVI is a global, privately owned company focused on innovating, optimizing and managing the supply chains of leading brands. Offering services in marketing analytics, packaging, supply chain management and logistics, HAVI partners with companies to address challenges big and small across the supply chain, from commodity to customer. Founded in 1974, HAVI employs more than 10,000 people and serves customers in more than 100 countries. For more information, please visit HAVI.com.
The Solution Architect is a strategic leadership role responsible for the end-to-end design and realization of a comprehensive supply chain solution portfolio. Acting as the critical bridge between complex global planning and analytics challenges and technical implementations, the SA ensures that supply chain systems, shared infrastructure, and technology solutions align with the organization’s long-term "To-Be" vision.
This is a hybrid role based at 345 N Morgan St, Chicago, IL 60607. Candidates must reside in the Chicago metropolitan area. Relocation assistance is not offered at this time.
Education: Bachelor’s degree in computer science, System Analysis, or a related field (master’s degree preferred).
Experience: 8+ years in several IT disciplines (e.g., Application Development, Integration, Middleware, Cloud Architecture, or Data and Analytics).
Data & Analytics: Hands-on experience with data platforms, including warehouses, lakehouses, data transformation pipelines, and MDM.
Integration: Strong knowledge of integration patterns (APIs, event streaming, MFT) and experience with iPaaS/middleware solutions in complex B2B ecosystems.
Cloud: Solid experience across major cloud platforms (Azure, AWS, GCP), designing scalable, cloud-native architectures.
Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs): Expertise in designing for performance, scalability, resilience, security, and cost efficiency.
Supply Planning (preferred not required): Deep understanding of supply chain planning processes and the enterprise platforms that support them.
Forecasting (preferred not required): Experience with statistical forecasting and ML-driven forecasting solutions
The successful candidate will lead the architectural way, leveraging deep experience in supply chain optimization, integrated planning, and technology. You will ensure that solutions are not only technically sound but also financially viable, scalable, and resilient within the enterprise’s governance framework and the evolving demands of the global market.
Responsibilties
Business Strategy & Blueprinting
Architectural Translation: Convert high-level business requirements and value streams into actionable technical blueprints.
Product Lifecycle Alignment: Partner with Product Owners and Solution Owners to maintain a robust architectural runway that supports future feature sets without accumulating excessive technical debt.
Impact Assessment: Evaluate how proposed changes affect business capabilities and current-state processes to ensure targeted business outcomes are met.
Technology Portfolio Orchestration
Portfolio Health: Monitor the solution ecosystem to identify misalignment, aging technologies, or critical deficiencies in legacy systems.
Modernization & Retirement: Lead "Keep vs. Retire" assessments, supporting Product Managers in decommissioning end-of-life systems and transitioning to modern stacks, in partnership with market technology leads.
Innovation & R&D: Analyze emerging market trends (AI/ML, Edge, etc.) and perform Proof-of-Concept (PoC) implementations to determine their practical application to the business model.
Solution Design & Delivery
Cross-Domain Design: Lead the evaluation and design of architecture spanning multiple applications, ensuring consistency across cloud, on-prem, and hybrid environments. Collaborate with solution owners to ensure design consistency across the technology landscape, at varying levels of details.
Strategic Road Mapping: Develop and maintain the evolution roadmap from current-state to future-state architecture.
Agile Integration: Provide high-level consultancy to decentralized Agile teams, ensuring individual product designs roll up into a cohesive enterprise strategy.
Governance & Enterprise Guidance
Standards Leadership: Define principles, guidelines, and reference patterns (UML/ArchiMate) to streamline decision-making, in close collaboration with Enterprise Architecture and the Technical Review Board.
Compliance & Assurance: Direct governance activities to ensure all solution designs meet security, privacy, and regulatory standards.
Responsible AI Governance: Ensure AI‑enabled solutions comply with enterprise standards for security, privacy, data governance, explainability, and ethical use of AI, aligned with internal policies and external regulatory requirements.
Community of Practice (CoP): Foster a culture of architectural excellence by mentoring solution owners and lead developers.
