# Technical Program Manager, Cell Manufacturing Engineering

What To Expect
Tesla’s world-class Cell Engineering Team is looking for a highly motivated Technical Program Manager for an exciting new project. As a Technical Program Manager, you are responsible for managing projects. This program spans across a large scope which includes cell product design, process development, and equipment/automation development and deployment. Your job will be to understand the technical intricacies of each of these areas, remove blockages/dependencies and drive all teams to success. Scope of this role will stretch across multiple phases. Initial phase will begin with early concepts and stretch to a fully validated cell design, capable processes and a very large operating factory.
Technical program managers own driving the scope, schedule, implementation, and cost of the project as well as additional functions such as vendor relations, stakeholder management and report outs. You will communicate and present project status, highlight schedule risk, and develop recovery plans in the event of a project slip. Thus, this is a cross-functional role that requires coordination between many different groups within and outside of Tesla.
What You'll Do
“Concept to completion” management of projects; drive project from concept through handover to production, acting as single point of contact
Manage road-map of projects working within the framework of the overall program. Define and drive manufacturing program plans outlining strategy, scope, schedule, resource requirements, and cost targets. Own report-outs for the projects highlighting status, risks, mitigations and opportunities across all levels of management within Tesla
Define and drive goals, deliverables, risks, and mitigations across all cross-functional stakeholders including design, manufacturing, controls, process, quality, infrastructure, logistics etc. through the various stages of the project: concept development, prototype testing, engineering design, system build, integration, testing, commissioning, and ramp
Prioritize team project work based on schedule, risk, resources, and payback. Track status of projects and action items against the schedule. Identify creative ways to overcome obstacles and meet the schedule despite setbacks. Continuously develop and adapt processes and methodologies to the team’s needs
Drive review, selection, budget, and schedule of external vendors and integrators for delivery of selected manufacturing and materials project scope. Drive vendor partnership working closely with supply chain and purchasing team and ensure alignment on engineering requirements, scope, schedule and cost
What You'll Bring
Bachelor’s Degree in Mechanical, Electrical, Manufacturing Engineering or similar technical discipline, or equivalent experience
1-3 years working in a manufacturing environment, in a mix of engineering and project management roles
Demonstrated experience in the management of engineering projects for mass-produced products
Ability to work cross-functionally and manage large teams
Availability for domestic/international travel as required, sometimes multiple weeks at a time
Knowledge and understanding of Battery Cell technology a plus
Experience with AutoCAD, Solidworks, or other 2D/3D design tools is a plus
