# Forward Deployed Engineer

About the Opportunity
Oscar Faye is partnering with a $30B+ alternative asset manager to hire a Forward Deployed AI Engineer as one of the first members of a brand new AI team. The firm operates across three businesses and is sitting on data and operational complexity that exists nowhere else. The firm has made an explicit, top-down commitment to becoming an AI-first organization. The cloud foundation is in place, early primitives are shipping, and engineers across the firm are already building on them. This is not exploration. This is execution.
The Role
You will embed across the firm's business lines and sit with the actual users — portfolio managers, traders, general counsel, operations teams — to figure out where AI compresses days of work into minutes, then ship the tools end-to-end. You report directly to the Head of AI and operate with genuine startup ownership inside a well-capitalized firm. A dedicated AI platform team is building the runtime, agents, and shared primitives underneath you. Your job is composing those primitives into products people actually adopt. You are not building plumbing. You are translating ambiguous workflows into systems users come back to every day.
What You'll Do
Embed across business lines and sit with non-technical and semi-technical users — portfolio managers, traders, general counsel, operations, legal — to diagnose where AI creates step-change leverage, including opportunities the team itself has not yet surfaced
Ship end-to-end solutions across the full stack: design, build, deploy, and own AI-powered tools that real users adopt and depend on daily
Turn vague descriptions of how someone does their job into structured, automatable workflows that hold up in a regulated financial environment
Compose the AI platform team's primitives, agents, and shared components into products users adopt — feed back what is missing or worth platformizing
Make the right tradeoffs between moving fast on frontier AI tooling and producing work that meets the bar of a regulated firm managing real capital
Document, demo, and hand off each solution so the team can extend it without you — repeat-request rate is the measure of success
What We're Looking For
5+ years building and shipping software real users depend on, regardless of the title on your business card — forward-deployed engineers, product engineers, founding engineers, technical PMs who ship, and analytical generalists who code are all in scope
Strong hands-on engineering with Python as the core language, plus enough TypeScript and frontend fluency to ship a usable interface solo when the work calls for it
Demonstrated track record of shipping LLM features to production users — RAG pipelines, agent workflows, tool use, eval frameworks, prompt engineering applied to real products
Product and UX instincts — you can take a vague description of someone's daily work and design a workflow they will actually use
Genuine curiosity about the frontier of AI tooling paired with the judgment to tell a demo from something that will hold up in production
Self-directed and collaborative — you can structure your own work, identify dependencies, and sequence deliverables without waiting for a ticket
Strong communicator who genuinely enjoys the collaboration side of the role — taking briefs from anyone in the firm, asking the right questions, and translating the answers into systems
What We're Not Looking For
Pure backend or ML engineers who cannot ship a usable frontend solo — a backend-only tool that no one opens is not a shipped tool
Solutions Architects, Sales Engineers, Pre-Sales, or Developer Advocates without a production engineering track record
Engineers who need heavily scoped tickets or defined roadmaps to operate
Pure research or applied scientist profiles with no shipping evidence — model development without product engineering does not fit this seat
Big-tech specialists with narrow slice ownership and no evidence of scoping ambiguous problems autonomously
Engineers who build only for engineers — devtools, infrastructure, internal libraries with no end-user surface area
Candidates with surface-level AI exposure who have not shipped LLM-powered features in production
Why This Role Stands Out
One of the first hires on a net-new AI team — direct line to the Head of AI and the CTO, no layers, no political overhead
Surface area across three distinct businesses in a single seat
300-person firm where you know the colleagues you build for by name and see your work used in their day-to-day immediately
Platform team underneath you handling the plumbing — you focus on translation, product judgment, and adoption
Competitive compensation structure with long-term upside built in — and a retention rate that speaks for itself
