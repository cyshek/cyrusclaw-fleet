# Technical Program Manager, Electric Propulsion Systems

Archer is an aerospace company based in San Jose, California building an all-electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft with a mission to advance the benefits of sustainable air mobility. We are designing, manufacturing, and operating an all-electric aircraft that can carry four passengers while producing minimal noise.
Our sights are set high and our problems are hard, and we believe that diversity in the workplace is what makes us smarter, drives better insights, and will ultimately lift us all to success. We are dedicated to cultivating an equitable and inclusive environment that embraces our differences, and supports and celebrates all of our team members.
About the role:
At Archer, we are scaling production of our cutting-edge eVTOL Aircraft. This team owns execution across assembly, integration, and test, managing detailed sequencing, resolving build challenges, and aligning cross-functional teams to ensure each aircraft meets schedule and readiness milestones.
What You’ll Do:
Own end-to-end execution of manufacturing, fabrication, and assembly programs for electric propulsion systems, ensuring on-time, high-quality delivery
Act as the primary interface across Manufacturing, Engineering, Supply Chain, Quality, and Program Management
Build and manage detailed, task-based schedules (Smartsheet), driving critical path and holding teams accountable to milestones
Embed with manufacturing teams to support day-to-day execution, remove blockers, and drive recovery plans to closure
Lead cross-functional coordination to ensure build readiness, including material, configuration, and production planning alignment
Proactively identify risks, issues, and gaps in execution, and implement solutions to improve throughput and reliability
Communicate program status, risks, and progress clearly to stakeholders and leadership
What You Need:
Bachelor’s degree in Engineering or related technical field
5+ years of TPM or NPI experience delivering complex hardware programs
Strong understanding of how products are built, with preference for hands-on mechanical or manufacturing experience (motors, stators, winding, etc.)
Proven ability to drive execution in manufacturing environments—not just track schedules
Experience managing detailed schedules and dependencies (Smartsheet or similar tools)
Strong cross-functional leadership and ability to influence without authority
Excellent problem-solving, communication, and organizational skills in fast-paced environments
Bonus Qualifications:
Experience in electric propulsion, electric vehicles, or adjacent industries (aerospace experience not required)
Familiarity with tools such as Jira, Teamcenter, and SAP
Experience supporting manufacturing ramp, fabrication, and assembly execution for complex hardware systems
Understanding of production environments and factory operations, including floor support and build readiness
