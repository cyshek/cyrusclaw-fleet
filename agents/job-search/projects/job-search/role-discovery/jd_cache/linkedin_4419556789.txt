# Technical Program Manager, Battery Structures

What To Expect
Technical Program Managers at Tesla Motors drive programs from R&D concept to high volume production. As a member of the HV Battery Structural Design Engineering team, you will manage the product development of our vehicle battery programs. The role requires a highly organized mindset, a passion to launch new technologies, and an unparalleled ability to pull people together to make things happen.
The Battery Engineering team is comprised of various teams, including Structures, Pack, Module, Devices, and Electronics engineering. The unique expertise of our team focuses on the structural design of all Tesla vehicles. We design the components that make up the foundation of the battery, enabling the most efficient and safe EVs on the road. While our team focuses heavily on ensuring occupant safety and controlled environments for the battery cells, our responsibility goes far beyond.
Our designs and schedules push the limits of physical possibility, which requires you to be extremely detail oriented and drive the cross functional teams to execute quickly at high quality. You will drive collaboration with many engineering groups, and ensure our team is aligned with company priorities. A team player mentality is highly valued, as you are the design representative on the ground collaborating with our multiple cross-functional colleagues. Tesla moves quickly, so being self-motivated and disciplined is key to being successful in this role.
What You'll Do
Create, maintain, and communicate schedules and plans for battery programs
Drive action with design engineering and cross functional teams to successfully complete projects within time and resource constraints
Be responsible for understanding and communicating the latest program plan of record, reporting effectively on status, blockers, and changes
Lead the team with confidence through rapid development cycles, changing requirements, and uncertainty
Collaborate with design engineers to resolve issues with part design, manufacturing processes, and validation tests
Coordinate with supply chain and manufacturing to guide design decisions based on material availability, manufacturing constraints, available technologies, and cost
Develop, organize, and track design validation targets and test cases for battery structural requirements, including crash, durability, abuse, and reliability
Own product BOM accuracy and communication of design changes between design, supply chain, NPI (New Product Introduction), and material planning
Identify team priorities and drive resource balancing across program objectives to enable execution of critical tasks
What You'll Bring
Degree in Engineering or equivalent experience and evidence of exceptional ability
Preferred: 2+ years full time employment experience managing and launching hardware products. Minimum: multiple relevant internship positions
Excellent communication skills, both verbal & written
Detail oriented with strong record-keeping & organizational skills
Comfortable reporting status to leadership and communicating in large audiences
Ability to work cross-functionally & manage large teams
Demonstration of mechanical engineering fundamentals (materials, mechanics, analysis, simulation)
Understanding of CAD and BOM management
Desire to own a lot and work hard - we have small teams and fast timelines
Desire to increase the effectiveness of your teammates
