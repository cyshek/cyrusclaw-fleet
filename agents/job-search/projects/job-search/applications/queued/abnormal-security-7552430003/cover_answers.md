# Cover answers — Abnormalsecurity, Product Manager II (abnormal-security-7552430003)

## How are you currently incorporating AI into your workflows?

At Microsoft, I built an internal AI agent and restructured our planning workflows around it, which cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased our drill capacity by 21%. The agent handles a lot of the repetitive synthesis work that used to bottleneck my team, things like pulling context across docs, drafting initial run-of-show plans, and surfacing risks from prior post-mortems. I also led the move from traditional documentation to an AI-powered semantic search tool, which dropped lookup time by 83% across the team.

Day to day, I lean on LLMs heavily for the messy front half of PM work: turning raw customer interview notes into structured problem statements, pressure-testing my own PRDs by having the model argue against them, and generating first drafts of comms for engineering and exec audiences that I then tighten. For analysis, I use it to write SQL and Power BI DAX queries faster than I could alone, then validate the outputs against known baselines.

The principle I try to hold to is using AI to compress the time between a question and a defensible answer, not to skip the thinking. The judgment calls, customer tradeoffs, and final written artifacts still need my fingerprints on them, but the research, drafting, and synthesis layers are where I get the most leverage.
