# Product Manager (YouTube)

**Company:** Google (YouTube)
**Location:** San Bruno, CA, USA (hybrid per Google policy)
**Level:** Mid
**Base salary:** $174,350–$198,000 + bonus + equity + benefits
**Source:** https://www.google.com/about/careers/applications/jobs/results/109318468951515846-product-manager

## Minimum qualifications
- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Engineering, Business Administration, Management Information Systems (MIS) or a related field and 3 years of experience in the job offered or in a Product Management-related occupation. (Alternatively, a Master's degree + 1 year.)
- 1 year of experience in:
  - Managing technical products from conception to launch OR managing product development lifecycle from delivery to sunsetting
  - Synthesizing technical and business options into credible solutions
  - Articulating long-term product vision and value to stakeholders OR narrating product stories to build user evangelist communities
  - Collaborating cross-functionally with engineering, UX/UI, sales, and finance
  - Preparing and delivering technical presentations to senior leadership OR defending business objectives and solutions to high-level audiences

## About the job
At YouTube, we believe that everyone deserves to have a voice, and that the world is a better place when we listen, share, and build community through our stories. We give everyone the power to share their story, explore what they love, and connect with one another. Working at the intersection of cutting-edge technology and boundless creativity, we move at the speed of culture with a shared goal to show people the world.

## Responsibilities
- Define product roadmaps and strategic visions by analyzing market landscapes, user needs, and competitive insights to secure stakeholder buy-in.
- Create comprehensive product requirements documents (PRDs) that prioritize user expectations, security standards, and business goals to guide technical development.
- Collaborate with cross-functional teams including engineering, UX, legal, and marketing to align development efforts with go-to-market plans.
- Prioritize project features and execution schedules in partnership with engineering to resolve technical challenges and manage trade-offs.
- Manage the full product lifecycle from launch to retirement by coordinating approvals, documentation, and compliance across all partner teams.
- Establish quantitative and qualitative success metrics to track product performance, validate opportunities, and drive data-informed iterations.
