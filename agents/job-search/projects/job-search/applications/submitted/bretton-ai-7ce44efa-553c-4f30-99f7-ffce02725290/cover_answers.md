## What excites you about working at a startup?

At Microsoft, I watched how quickly a small, focused team could outmaneuver a large org when the problem was well-scoped and the ownership was clear. Some of my most impactful work, like scaling Azure's recovery validation platform from a 2-person operation into a self-service system that drove $14M+ in documented business impact, happened precisely because there was no bureaucratic buffer between identifying a problem and shipping a solution. That kind of tight feedback loop, where field insight directly shapes the product roadmap, is where I do my best work. Startups compress that loop by default.

What draws me to Bretton specifically is the combination of a genuinely hard problem and a customer base where the stakes are real. Fighting financial crime is not a nice-to-have, and selling AI agents into compliance-sensitive environments like Robinhood or Mercury requires exactly the kind of technical credibility and consultative depth I have been building. I want to be in a role where the feedback I bring from the field actually reshapes the product, not just fills a survey. At a company at Bretton's stage, that influence is structural, not incidental.
