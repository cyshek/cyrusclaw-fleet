## What excites you about working at a startup?

At Microsoft I got to see what it looks like to build something from nothing inside a large org. I led the 0-to-1 development of an internal Resilience Automation Platform, taking a 2-person manual effort and turning it into a platformized system that now sustains 45+ annual drills and drove $14M+ in business impact. I also built and demoed an internal AI agent for drill planning that cut planning cycle time 39% and lifted capacity 21%. The parts I loved most were the early ones: scoping the problem, prototyping fast, putting something rough in front of real users, and letting their feedback reshape the roadmap. A startup is that energy without the layers in between, and that's where I do my best work.

Bretton AI is appealing because the stakes are real and the technical bet is sharp. You're using AI agents to automate AML and counter-terrorist financing investigations, work that actually protects the financial system, and you've earned the trust of companies like Robinhood, Mercury, and Gusto. As a Solutions Engineer I'd get to sit at the bridge between customers and product, translating what CTOs and Chief Compliance Officers need into demos and prototypes, then carrying that field feedback straight back to engineering. That tight loop between customer, product, and a hard problem worth solving is exactly the kind of place I want to be early.
