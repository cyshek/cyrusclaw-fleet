## What is your experience working in dashboards as a Product Manager?

Dashboards have been a concrete part of my PM work, not just a side output. At Microsoft, I built a Power BI dashboard surfacing operational toil metrics across 140+ teams, giving leadership the data-informed visibility they needed to prioritize high-impact automation investments. That required thinking carefully about what metrics actually mattered to operators day-to-day versus what just looked good in a report, and structuring the information architecture so the right signals were front and center rather than buried.

Beyond that specific build, a lot of my product work has centered on the same core challenge the Merchant Dashboard faces: how do you design a surface that serves as a central control point across a complex, multi-part system? On the Resilience Automation Platform at Microsoft, I owned the self-service scheduling requirements and information architecture for a platform used by internal operators managing 45+ annual drills. The design decisions around what operators could configure, monitor, and act on directly shaped whether the platform reduced toil or just created new friction.

At Pro Painters, I also redesigned the end-to-end merchant workflow for 200+ monthly proposals, optimizing the CRM process and scoping lifecycle to reduce friction for operators running daily business tasks. That experience gave me a ground-level perspective on how small business operators actually interact with software tools, which I think is directly relevant to building a dashboard that restaurant operators want to open every morning rather than avoid.
