## What has been your favorite project or proudest accomplishment? Why?

My proudest accomplishment is scaling Azure's recovery validation program from a 2-person operation into a fully platformized system. When I inherited it, the program ran on tribal knowledge and manual coordination. I standardized the end-to-end workflows, led 0-to-1 delivery of an internal Resilience Automation Platform with self-service scheduling, and grew the program to sustain 45+ annual resilience drills with a documented $14M+ business impact across enterprise partners.

What made it meaningful wasn't just the scale - it was that I built structure where there wasn't any. I had to coordinate across Finance, Legal, Engineering, and sovereign-cloud partners simultaneously, including serving as bridge lead on a network isolation test tied to a $1.5B+ enterprise contract. Every dependency had to be mapped, every stakeholder had to be kept informed at the right cadence, and every roadblock had to get cleared before it became a problem.

I'm proud of this project because it required both operational precision and genuine creativity - figuring out how to make something complex feel repeatable and low-friction for the teams depending on it. That balance of rigor and design thinking is something I carry into every program I own.
