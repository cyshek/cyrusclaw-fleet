## What is the most impressive thing you've personally built or automated with AI? Describe exactly what you did, how it worked, and the measurable outcome

The most impactful AI system I've built is an AI agent I designed and deployed at Microsoft to automate drill planning workflows for Azure's resilience testing program. Before this, scheduling and planning each resilience drill was a heavily manual process - coordinating across customer teams, mapping dependencies, validating readiness criteria, and generating documentation. It was a cycle that consumed significant engineering time and capped how many drills we could run.

I built the agent to handle the repeatable, structured portions of that workflow end to end. It ingested inputs about the target environment, dependency graph, and readiness requirements, then generated a complete drill plan - including scheduling recommendations, stakeholder notifications, and pre-drill checklists - without requiring manual assembly at each step. I used LLM-based reasoning for the parts of planning that required interpreting ambiguous inputs or adapting to environment-specific constraints, and layered deterministic logic on top for the outputs that needed to be consistent and auditable.

The measurable outcome was a 39% reduction in planning cycle time and a 21% increase in drill capacity - meaning we could run more drills with the same team, and engineers could redirect the recovered time toward the complex, customer-facing escalations that actually required human judgment. That's the framing I care most about with AI tooling: not automation for its own sake, but freeing high-skill people to do the work only they can do.
