## Why Substack?

At Microsoft, a big part of my work has been turning ambiguous early-stage programs into systems that scale. I built a creator-facing validation platform from scratch, defined the activation funnel instrumentation that made it self-service, and ran discovery with dozens of internal teams to identify where friction was killing adoption. The parallels to creator activation are direct: figuring out what behaviors predict success, removing first-mile friction, and building feedback loops that make progress visible. That's the core of what this role is asking for, and it's where I've spent meaningful time.

What draws me to Substack specifically is the model. Most platforms optimize for engagement on their terms, creator interests come second. Substack is genuinely trying to build an economic engine where the creator wins if their audience wins, not one where the platform extracts value from both. Working on creator activation here means the product improvements I ship have a direct line to whether a writer or podcaster can actually make a living. That kind of leverage is rare, and it's the reason this role stood out.
