## What has been your favorite project or proudest accomplishment? Why?

My proudest accomplishment is building Azure's first proactive resilience testing capability at Microsoft. When I started, recovery validation was a 2-person manual operation. I led the 0-to-1 development of an internal Resilience Automation Platform, synthesizing technical goals from a loose product vision and defining self-service scheduling requirements that cut operational toil by 30%. We shipped a rack-level drill program in 4 months with a 94% recovery rate, which pre-empted critical hardware defects before they reached customers.

What made it my favorite is that it scaled into something durable: a platformized system that now sustains 45+ annual resilience drills and over $14M in business impact. I also got to sit at the center of high-stakes, cross-org work, like serving as bridge lead on a sovereign-cloud network isolation test tied to a $1.5B+ enterprise contract, aligning a room of technical experts under executive visibility.

I'm proud of it because it combined the parts of this work I care about most: getting deep enough technically to earn engineers' trust, removing roadblocks so they could focus on hard problems, and turning a fragile manual process into a repeatable system that outlives any single person running it.
