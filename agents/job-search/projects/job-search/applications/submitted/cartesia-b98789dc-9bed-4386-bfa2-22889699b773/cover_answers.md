## What's the hardest problem you've worked on?

The hardest problem I've worked on was standing up Azure's first proactive rack-level resilience testing capability from scratch, while simultaneously serving as bridge PM on a sovereign-cloud network isolation test tied to a $1.5B+ contract. The two workstreams were running in parallel, and the stakes on the deal side meant I couldn't let the 0-to-1 product work slip.

On the product side, the challenge was technical and organizational at once: there was no existing primitive for rack-level drill execution, no clear API contract, and no established recovery measurement methodology. I had to write PRDs that were specific enough for engineering to build against, while leaving room to learn from the first few drills. We shipped in four months with a 94% recovery rate and surfaced critical hardware defects that hadn't been caught through any prior validation path.

On the deal side, I was running technical reviews with engineering and GTM, translating infrastructure tradeoffs, including decisions around network isolation scope and blast radius containment, into narratives that could hold up in a CTO debrief. The hardest part wasn't any single technical decision; it was holding both contexts simultaneously and making sure the architectural choices we were making on the product side didn't create liabilities in the deal conversation. That forced a level of clarity in my thinking and writing that I don't think I would have developed any other way.
