## Tell us about something you've built/done that you think is genuinely cool, big or small, work or personal.

At Microsoft, I built an AI agent from scratch to automate Azure resilience drill planning workflows. Before it existed, planning a single drill was a manual, time-intensive process involving calendar coordination, dependency mapping, and stakeholder scheduling across multiple orgs. I scoped the problem, built the agent, and deployed it into our actual planning workflow. It cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased our drill capacity by 21%, which meant the team could run more concurrent programs without adding headcount.

What I find genuinely cool about it isn't just the time savings. It's that I built it to solve a real bottleneck I was personally frustrated by, using tools available to me, without being asked to. It freed up TPM bandwidth for the work that actually requires human judgment, like cross-org alignment and escalation decisions, and it made the whole delivery operation more scalable. Small thing in the grand scheme, but it changed how the team worked day to day.
