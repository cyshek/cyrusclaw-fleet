## Why do you want to work at Profound?

At Microsoft, I spent the last two years as the technical and executive-facing lead on Azure's recovery validation platform, serving Fortune 500 clients like Walmart, Databricks, and SAP. I built AI agents to accelerate planning workflows, designed data pipelines that turned raw telemetry into executive dashboards, and identified repeating client integration patterns that I then partnered with Product to systematize. That combination of writing production tools, sitting in C-suite conversations, and feeding field insights back into the roadmap is exactly the loop this role is built around. I know what it feels like to be the person who has to make complex technical systems legible to a boardroom, and I want to do that work at a place that treats it as a first-class engineering function.

Profound is attacking something real. AI search is already reshaping how buyers make decisions, and most brands have no visibility into it and no way to act on it. The fact that 13% of the Fortune 500 is already on the platform tells me the problem is validated and the sales motion is working, which means the forward-deployed role here is about depth and impact, not just getting early customers across the line. Being backed by Lightspeed, Sequoia, Kleiner Perkins, and Khosla at a $1B valuation while still being a lean team means the resources are there but the builder mentality is still the culture. That is the environment where I do my best work.
