# Cover answers — Airtable, Program Manager, Professional Services - East (airtable-8586863002)

## How are you using AI today in your current role? What specific tools are you using? If applicable, show us your last AI experiment.

AI is a core part of how I actually run delivery at Microsoft, not something I bolt on after the fact. The most concrete example: I built and deployed an internal AI agent specifically for drill planning and status synthesis across Azure's recovery validation program. It ingests scheduling data, past execution results, and stakeholder inputs, then surfaces a structured plan and a synthesized status update I can review and send. That single change cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased overall program capacity by 21%, which mattered because we were running 45+ annual resilience drills across customers like Databricks, Walmart, and SAP.

On the tooling side, I use Copilot for day-to-day drafting, risk log synthesis, and turning rough notes from executive check-ins into clean stakeholder updates. I have also worked with RAG-based semantic search, which I implemented to replace a documentation system that required too much manual lookup, cutting stakeholder search time by 83%. My most recent experiment was prompt-chaining a planning workflow where the agent pulls open blockers from our tracking system, cross-references the schedule, and outputs a ranked risk list with suggested owner assignments. It is not fully autonomous yet, but it handles the first-pass triage that used to take me 30-45 minutes each Monday morning.

The honest framing is that I use AI to handle the structured-but-repetitive synthesis work so I can spend my time on the judgment calls: sequencing tradeoffs, partner accountability conversations, and the moments when a plan hits reality and someone needs to make a call. I also have a pretty clear-eyed view of where agentic systems introduce risk, particularly around compliance-sensitive communications and anywhere a hallucinated detail could damage a customer relationship. I keep humans in the loop on anything that touches external stakeholders or contractual scope.
