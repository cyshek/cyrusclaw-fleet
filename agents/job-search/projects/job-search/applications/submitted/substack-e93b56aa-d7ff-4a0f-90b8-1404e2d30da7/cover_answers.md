## Why Substack?

The through-line in my work at Microsoft has been building organic growth flywheels - starting with a 0-to-1 internal platform where I defined product requirements, instrumented funnel metrics, and drove self-service adoption across 140+ partner teams, reducing dependency on central execution and compounding the program's reach without adding headcount. I also built the business case for growth work by translating qualitative discovery into crisp specs that influenced sprint prioritization, and I tracked activation-style metrics across the full lifecycle to distinguish signal from noise in performance data. That combination of product craft, SEO-adjacent discoverability work (including migrating documentation to semantic search and cutting lookup time 83%), and funnel instrumentation maps directly to what this role owns.

Substack specifically appeals to me because the bet it's making - that independent writers and creators can build sustainable businesses through direct reader relationships, outside the advertising model - is one of the more interesting structural bets in media right now. The app becoming the primary destination for engaged readers is a real inflection point, and owning the organic acquisition flywheel at that moment, SEO, ASO, onboarding, the handoff from first click to first subscription, is exactly the kind of compounding, high-leverage work I want to be doing. The intersection of product rigor and organic growth craft, writing PRDs one day and auditing keyword rankings the next, is how I already operate, and doing it for a product that genuinely matters to how culture gets made and distributed is a compelling reason to be here.
