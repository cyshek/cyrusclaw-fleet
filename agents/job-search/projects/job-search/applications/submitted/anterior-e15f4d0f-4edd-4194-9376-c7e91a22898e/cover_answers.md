## Why Anterior?

At Microsoft I owned end-to-end delivery of Azure's enterprise resilience program, coordinating across engineering, product, and customer-facing teams to keep complex integrations on track for customers like Databricks, Walmart, and SAP. I built lightweight processes that worked for a small core team, resolved dependencies before they became blockers, and shipped a 0-to-1 automation platform that made the whole operation repeatable at scale. That work maps directly to what this role is asking for: scoping solutions for enterprise customers, driving cross-functional programs without importing big-company overhead, and building playbooks that hold up as you grow from 5 customers to 50.

Anterior specifically appeals to me because the problem is real and the stakes are high. Healthcare administration is genuinely broken, and the angle here - AI designed by clinicians, for clinicians, to make that overhead invisible - is the right framing. I'm drawn to companies that pair serious technical ambition with a clear, grounded mission, and Anterior checks both. The site reinforces that: the product thinking is sharp, the clinical integration is deliberate, and it's obvious the team cares about getting the fundamentals right rather than moving fast and patching later. That's the kind of environment where I do my best work.
