## Why Anthropic?

The Safeguards role reads almost exactly like the work I've been doing at Microsoft, just pointed at a problem I care about more. I own the operational health of Azure's recovery validation stack, drive the incident-response and post-mortem cadence for 14 cross-org executions under executive visibility, and partner with engineering leads to keep runbooks, ownership, and SLO dashboards accurate during active incidents. I also led a migration from manual runbooks to automated workflows across infra, monitoring, and on-call partners without taking safety-critical pipelines offline, which is the same shape of work as the platform and incident-tooling migrations described here.

Anthropic is one of the few places where reliability of a safety-critical pipeline is treated as a fundamentally different problem than reliability of a product feature, and that distinction is what pulls me in. I want to spend my time making sure classifiers, detection pipelines, and eval infra actually stay up, that post-mortem action items actually close, and that SLOs on safety systems are real numbers someone watches. Doing that for the systems sitting between frontier models and the real world is the version of this job I most want to be doing.
