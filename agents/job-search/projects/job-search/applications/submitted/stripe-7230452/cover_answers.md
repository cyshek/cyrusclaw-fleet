# Cover answers — Stripe, Forward Deployed Engineer, Privy (stripe-7230452)

## Why are you excited about Privy?

What draws me to Privy is the combination of hard infrastructure problems and direct developer impact. At Microsoft, I owned the end-to-end deployment of Azure's Resilience Automation Platform, defining self-service scheduling APIs and configuration workflows that reduced operational toil for enterprise customers like Databricks and Walmart. That work taught me how much the right abstraction layer matters - when you get the API design right, you multiply what every developer building on top of it can accomplish. Privy is doing exactly that for wallet infrastructure and crypto rails, which is a genuinely difficult problem to abstract cleanly.

The mission of making privacy and user ownership the default online is something I find compelling on its own merits. But what makes this role specifically interesting is the forward-deployed model - being the technical owner closest to customers like Ramp and Klarna, diving into their codebases, and turning that friction into product improvements. I have spent the past two years bridging engineering and customer outcomes at scale, and I want to do that in a context where the product itself is the infrastructure layer, not just a process around it. The chance to work at that level, with Stripe's distribution behind it, is rare.
