## Tell us about something you've built/done that you think is genuinely cool, big or small, work or personal.

At Microsoft, I built Azure's first proactive rack-level resilience testing capability from scratch. Before this, resilience validation at that layer basically didn't exist in any structured form. I had four months to design the program, build the methodology, run the drills, and produce something reusable. We hit a 94% recovery rate, surfaced hardware defects that would have caused real production incidents, and the playbook I wrote got adopted as the standard validation model going forward.

What made it genuinely interesting wasn't just the technical complexity - it was the 0-to-1 nature of it. There was no template to follow, no prior art internally to copy. I had to figure out how rack-level failure scenarios should be sequenced, how to get alignment across hardware, software, and network teams who all had different definitions of 'done,' and how to document it in a way that the next person could actually run it without me in the room. Delivering a first-of-a-kind program in four months with a reusable output at the end is the part I'm most proud of.

Smaller but also cool: I built an internal AI agent to restructure our program planning workflows. It cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased drill capacity by 21% without adding headcount. It's the kind of thing that sounds incremental but changed how the team actually operated day to day.
