## Why Anthropic?

My work at Microsoft has been directly in the compute space - standing up rack-level validation programs, building operational visibility into fleet health, and coordinating cross-org execution across infrastructure and systems teams at scale. The TPM role at Anthropic maps closely to what I do day to day: owning programs across the compute lifecycle, translating competing priorities into clear operational plans, and building the processes and tracking frameworks that let engineering teams move fast without losing visibility. I led a sovereign-cloud network isolation test tied to a $1.5B+ enterprise contract and pioneered Azure's first proactive rack-level compute testing capability from scratch - both required exactly the kind of cross-functional coordination and trade-off ownership this role describes.

What draws me specifically to Anthropic is that compute here is not a cost center to be optimized in the abstract - it is the direct substrate for frontier AI research and safety work that actually matters. The technical bet Anthropic is making, building reliable and interpretable AI systems at scale, means compute infrastructure is genuinely mission-critical in a way that is different from most cloud or enterprise contexts. I want to work on a problem where getting the compute layer right has real downstream consequences for the research, and where the TPM role is shaping those outcomes rather than just tracking milestones. That combination of technical depth and high stakes is what I am looking for in the next chapter of my career.
