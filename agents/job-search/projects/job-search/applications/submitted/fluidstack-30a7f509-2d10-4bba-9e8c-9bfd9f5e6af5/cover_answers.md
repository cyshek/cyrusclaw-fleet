## Tell us about something you've built/done that you think is genuinely cool, big or small, work or personal.

The thing I'm most proud of is building Azure's first proactive rack-level drill program from scratch. Before this existed, resilience validation at the rack level simply wasn't happening in a structured way. I took it from concept to go-live in 4 months, defined the acceptance criteria, designed the execution model, and ran it end-to-end. It landed a 94% recovery rate on the first cycle and surfaced real hardware defects that would have otherwise shown up as unplanned outages.

What made it genuinely interesting wasn't just the technical side - it was the constraint. I had no playbook to copy, no prior program to inherit. I had to figure out what 'done' looked like, get cross-functional teams aligned on that definition, and build the operational standard from the ground up. That's the part I find most satisfying: when there's no existing answer and you have to construct one that actually holds up under pressure.

On the side, I also built an internal AI agent to automate drill planning workflows. It cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased program capacity by 21% without adding headcount. That one started as a personal experiment to see if I could remove the most repetitive parts of my own job, and it ended up becoming something the broader team relied on.
