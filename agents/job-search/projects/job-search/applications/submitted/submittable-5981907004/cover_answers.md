## In a fast-paced, growing startup environment, how do you handle changes in priorities or unexpected challenges?

My default is to get oriented fast and keep moving. When Azure's recovery validation program was handed to me as a 2-person operation with no standardized process, I didn't wait for a perfect plan - I mapped the existing workflows, identified the highest-leverage gaps, and started building the platform and process changes in parallel. That meant some things shifted mid-build, and I adjusted scope without losing momentum, ultimately delivering a system that scaled to 45+ annual drills and $14M+ in business impact.

I also had a similar experience at Amazon Robotics, where I inherited a 2,000+ unit OS migration mid-flight with zero downtime tolerance. The key there was rapid dependency mapping and tight cross-functional coordination across IT, Operations, and Engineering - staying flexible on approach while holding firm on the outcome. In both cases, the through-line was the same: treat ambiguity as information, communicate clearly with stakeholders, and make the next best decision with what you know right now rather than waiting for certainty that isn't coming.
