## Why do you want to work at Profound?

At Microsoft, I built and maintained a structured web roadmap and sprint backlog across 6+ engineering stakeholders, ran project cycles that kept delivery on schedule, and defined KPIs to measure platform adoption and conversion to self-service usage. Earlier, at Pro Painters, I directly owned website experience and CTA optimization as a revenue lever - cutting CAC by 13% and lifting site conversions by 2.7% by treating the site as a conversion asset, not a brochure. That combination of roadmap ownership, cross-functional coordination, and obsession over what drives buyers to act is exactly what this role calls for, and it's work I've done in practice, not just in theory.

Profound is building infrastructure for a genuinely new marketing category - one where the question isn't just how brands rank on Google, but how AI systems represent them to buyers who may never visit a search results page at all. That's a consequential problem, and the product sits at the intersection of analytics, AI, and go-to-market in a way I find compelling. Owning the marketing website for a company at that inflection point - where the site itself has to communicate a new category clearly and convert sophisticated buyers - is the kind of high-ownership, high-craft challenge I want. The backing, the customer list, and the pace of the team make it clear this is a serious operation, and I want to be part of building it.
