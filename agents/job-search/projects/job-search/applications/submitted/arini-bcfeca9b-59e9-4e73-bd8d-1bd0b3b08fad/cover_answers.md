## What's the coolest thing you've built?

The thing I'm most proud of is Azure's first proactive resilience testing capability. Before this existed, Azure had no way to validate hardware recovery at the rack level before failures hit production. I built and delivered a rack-level drill program in four months, end to end, with a 94% recovery rate. The program surfaced critical hardware defects that wouldn't have been caught otherwise and established a new continuous-validation model that became the foundation for how Azure approaches proactive resilience testing going forward.

What made it genuinely hard was that it was a 0-to-1 problem with no playbook. I had to define what success looked like, secure cross-org buy-in, design the execution model, and ship it under executive visibility with real stakes. The output wasn't a deck or a roadmap, it was a production-grade capability running live on Azure infrastructure. That combination of ambiguity, cross-functional execution, and shipping something that actually runs in production is what I look for in the problems I take on.

On top of that, I built an AI agent for drill planning that reduced planning cycle time by 39% and increased drill capacity by 21%. That one was satisfying in a different way because it directly expanded how many enterprise customers we could serve without adding headcount. Both projects together are probably the clearest example of the kind of work I do best: find the operational constraint, build the system that removes it, and measure the outcome.
