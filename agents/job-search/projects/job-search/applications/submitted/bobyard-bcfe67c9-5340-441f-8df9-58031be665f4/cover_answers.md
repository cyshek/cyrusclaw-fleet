## What interests you about Bobyard?

A lot of my work at Microsoft has been about taking complex, multi-stakeholder technical workflows and turning them into something scalable and clearly tied to business outcomes. Running discovery with 20+ service teams to map 81 hours of manual toil per region launch, then building a roadmap and POV plan around it, is pretty close to what a Sales Engineer does every day. I also spent time earlier in my career at Pro Painters, where I owned end-to-end scoping and invoicing for 200+ monthly proposals for a trade services business, so contractor workflows are not abstract to me. That combination of technical depth and real familiarity with how trade businesses actually operate feels directly relevant to what Bobyard needs.

What draws me to Bobyard specifically is the problem and the moment. Construction and the trades are genuinely underserved by modern software, and Bobyard is going after that gap with a product built around how contractors actually work. Being the first SE hire means I would be building the function from scratch, defining the demo playbook, the POV framework, the trade-specific materials, and I find that kind of zero-to-one work more interesting than inheriting a finished process. The chance to have direct input on product and roadmap while helping close real deals is exactly the kind of role I want to be in right now.
