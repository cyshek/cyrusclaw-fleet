# Cover answers — Courierhealth, Customer Engineer (courier-health-4859485007)

## Part of this role is customer facing. Please explain your experience working with non technical stakeholders or customers.

At Microsoft, a significant part of my role as a Program Manager involved working directly with technical and non-technical stakeholders at enterprise clients like Databricks, Walmart, SAP, and NetApp. For our resilience drill program, I regularly had to translate complex infrastructure concepts, things like rack-level failure scenarios or recovery validation logic, into plain language that business stakeholders and executive sponsors could act on. I served as the bridge between engineering teams and client-side decision makers, especially on a network isolation test tied to a $1.5B+ enterprise contract where clear communication across both sides was critical to keeping the work on track.

I also conducted 20+ discovery sessions with internal service teams at Microsoft to surface workflow pain points and manual effort, then translated those findings into product roadmaps that engineering could actually build against. That process required a lot of back-and-forth with non-technical program owners who cared about outcomes, not architecture. Similarly, at Amazon Robotics, I coordinated across IT, Operations, and Engineering during a 2,000+ unit OS migration, which meant keeping non-technical operations stakeholders informed and aligned throughout a high-stakes transition. I find that the ability to listen carefully, ask the right clarifying questions, and reframe technical constraints in terms of business impact is what makes those relationships work.
