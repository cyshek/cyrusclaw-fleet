## What is the most impressive thing you've personally built or automated with AI? Describe exactly what you did, how it worked, and the measurable outcome

At Microsoft, I built an internal AI agent to automate drill planning workflows for Azure's recovery validation program. The problem was that planning each resilience drill required significant coordination overhead - pulling together schedules, aligning cross-functional stakeholders, mapping dependencies, and generating execution artifacts manually across a growing portfolio of 45+ annual drills. The process was repetitive, time-consuming, and bottlenecked on me.

I designed and built an agent that ingested program context - team calendars, dependency maps, past drill records, and execution templates - and automated the generation of planning artifacts, stakeholder notifications, and scheduling proposals. It used an LLM backbone to handle the natural language and reasoning components, with structured outputs that fed directly into our existing coordination workflows. I iterated on it based on feedback from the engineering and operations leads who were the downstream consumers of its outputs.

The measurable outcome: planning cycle time dropped 39% and program capacity increased 21% without adding headcount. That last part matters - the team was able to absorb more drills and more complexity without scaling the coordination burden linearly. It also freed me to focus on the higher-judgment work of dependency mapping and risk management rather than logistics assembly.
