## What interests you about Bobyard?

A lot of my work at Microsoft has been about taking complex technical workflows and translating them into something that makes sense to the people who depend on them, whether that's an enterprise customer trying to understand their resilience posture or a stakeholder needing a clear readout before signing off on a $1.5B contract. That's essentially what a Sales Engineer does, and I've been doing it in a high-stakes, cross-functional environment for the past two years. I also spent time early in my career at a painting contractor, Pro Painters, where I built out their CRM process, improved scoping workflows, and helped the sales team close more jobs with a repeatable playbook. That combination, technical depth plus direct exposure to how trade businesses actually operate, feels directly relevant to what Bobyard is building.

What draws me to Bobyard specifically is the bet you're making on the trades. Construction and trade businesses are underserved by software, and the complexity of their workflows makes the technical sales motion genuinely interesting. This isn't a commodity SaaS sale. It requires someone who can earn trust with contractors, understand their day-to-day, and show them exactly where the value lands. The fact that this is a founding SE role means I'd be building the playbook from scratch, which is the kind of work I find most engaging. I want to be in a place where what I ship actually shapes the company, and this is clearly that.
