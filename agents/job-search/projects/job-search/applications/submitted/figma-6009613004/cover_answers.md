# Cover answers — Figma, Technical Program Manager, Data Science (figma-6009613004)

## Why do you want to join Figma?

The work I've been doing at Microsoft maps almost directly onto what this role is asking for. I built a self-serve Resilience Automation Platform that shifted teams from manual, request-based execution to a scalable, self-service model, reducing operational toil by 30%. I deployed an AI agent that cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased capacity by 21%. And I ran a change management motion during my 2023 internship where I drove adoption of AI-powered workflows across 14 teams through enablement sessions and champion demos, saving 37 engineering hours monthly. The throughline in all of that is the same mission this role is centered on: moving people from filing requests to getting answers themselves.

Figma's bet here is compelling to me specifically. The combination of semantic layer expansion, a Slack-native data assistant, and a proper eval framework is a serious, thoughtful approach to data democratization, not just a BI tool refresh. Figma sits at the intersection of design, collaboration, and developer tooling, and data self-serve at that scale means serving an unusually diverse set of stakeholders, from Sales to Research to Engineering. That cross-functional complexity is exactly the kind of program I want to own. The fact that this is Figma's first dedicated DS TPM role also means there's real org-building work alongside the program delivery, and that combination is what I find most interesting.

## From where do you intend to work?

I'm currently based in Kirkland, WA, just outside Seattle. I'm open to working remotely from there or relocating to one of Figma's hub offices if that's the better fit for the team. Either arrangement works for me.
