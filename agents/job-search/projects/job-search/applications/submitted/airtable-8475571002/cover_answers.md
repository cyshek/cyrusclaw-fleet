# Cover answers — Airtable, Product Manager, Funnel & Activation (airtable-8475571002)

## How are you using AI today in your current role? If applicable, show us your last AI experiment.

At Microsoft, I built an internal AI agent to automate planning workflows across our resilience drill program. The problem was straightforward but painful: each drill cycle required manually aggregating scheduling inputs, stakeholder availability, and execution parameters from across the org, which ate up a disproportionate chunk of my time and the team's. I scoped and shipped an agent using Copilot Studio that ingested those inputs, generated structured planning artifacts, and flagged conflicts before they became blockers. The result was a 39% reduction in planning cycle time and a 21% increase in drill capacity, which directly compressed our learning loop and let us run more experiments per quarter.

The more interesting lesson was about where AI actually added leverage versus where it created noise. Early on I over-indexed on automating outputs that still required human judgment to validate, which added a review step that partially offset the gains. I pulled back, narrowed the agent's scope to the deterministic parts of the workflow, and let it do those reliably rather than trying to cover the ambiguous cases. That tradeoff, between broad coverage and trustworthy precision, is something I think about a lot now when evaluating where AI fits into a product experience versus where it gets in the user's way. It's directly relevant to how I'd approach AI-powered onboarding: the goal isn't to automate everything, it's to remove the friction that actually blocks users from reaching their first real workflow.
