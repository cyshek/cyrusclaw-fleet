## Why Anthropic?

My work at Microsoft has been fundamentally about building safety into systems before things go wrong - not patching after the fact. I defined upstream detection requirements and downstream intervention logic for Azure's resilience surface, built eval frameworks for a rack-level drill program that surfaced critical hardware defects, and designed blindspot dashboards that fed directly into risk prioritization. When I led the sovereign-cloud network isolation test tied to a $1.5B+ contract, the core challenge was exactly what this role describes: aligning policy, engineering, and cross-functional stakeholders on risk tradeoffs under pressure, with no clear playbook. That's the kind of work I want to do at greater scale and higher stakes.

What draws me to Anthropic specifically is that the Safeguards problem here is genuinely hard and genuinely matters. Most trust-and-safety work is reactive - detect bad content, remove it, iterate. The rare harms problem is different: low-frequency, high-consequence events where you can't rely on volume signals, where adversaries are determined, and where the cost of a false negative is asymmetric. Anthropic is the team actually thinking rigorously about this at the frontier model level, building evals and interventions as first-class research problems rather than afterthoughts. That combination of technical seriousness and real-world deployment pressure is exactly where I want to be.
