## Describe a one-off customer solution you built that you later turned into something reusable for the team or the product. What was the original problem, what did you build the first time, and what did the repeatable version look like?

At Microsoft, I was asked to support a sovereign-cloud network isolation test tied to a $1.5B+ enterprise contract. The ask was urgent and scoped narrowly: get through that one drill with clean execution and strong stakeholder communication. I mapped the dependencies, owned end-to-end technical delivery, and we got through it. But doing it made the gaps obvious - there was no standardized way to plan, schedule, or document what we had just done. Every drill was being re-invented from scratch by whoever happened to own it that quarter.

So I took what I had built for that one engagement and turned it into a platform. I defined the product requirements and self-service scheduling capabilities for what became Azure's internal Resilience Automation Platform - something that didn't exist before. That shifted execution from a manual, person-dependent process into a repeatable, scalable model. The result was a 30% reduction in operational toil and a system that could sustain 45+ annual resilience drills across enterprise customers like Databricks, Walmart, SAP, and NetApp without requiring the same heroics each time.

The pattern I follow is the same one this role is asking for: solve the real problem in front of you, then look at what you built and ask what the repeatable version looks like. That instinct is what I'd bring to customer integrations at Firecrawl - shipping the fix fast, then turning the one-off into a playbook the whole team can use.
