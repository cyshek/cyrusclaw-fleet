## Please write a short note on a project you're proud of:

The project I'm most proud of is building Azure's Resilience Automation Platform from scratch. When I started, resilience testing was a largely manual, ad-hoc process run by a small team. I defined the execution framework from the ground up - how tasks decompose into subtasks, how failures get detected and retried, and how the system recovers when something goes wrong. That work scaled to 45+ annual drills, reduced operational toil by 30%, and ultimately drove $14M+ in measurable business impact across customers like Databricks, Walmart, and SAP.

What makes me most proud isn't the scale - it's that I also built an internal AI agent on top of that platform to handle drill planning. I redesigned the task-decomposition and planning workflows specifically to work with the agent's capabilities and limitations, which cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased drill throughput by 21%. I learned a lot about where agents succeed, where they get stuck, and how the harness around them determines almost everything about reliability and user trust.

The benchmarking work that came out of it was also meaningful. I defined evaluation criteria, measured 94% recovery rates across scenarios, and surfaced hardware failure modes that hadn't been on anyone's radar. Turning those empirical results into engineering priorities felt like exactly the right way to build - and it's the kind of work I want to keep doing at a much larger scale.
