## Share one example of a Product Experience in healthcare that's good. Why?

One example that stands out to me is how Epic's MyChart handles test result delivery. A few years ago, getting lab results meant waiting days for a callback or decoding a cryptic voicemail. MyChart changed that by surfacing results directly to the patient, often with plain-language explanations attached, and then routing follow-up actions clearly, whether that's scheduling, messaging your provider, or understanding what a value means.

What makes it work is the sequencing. It doesn't just dump data on you. It contextualizes it, tells you if something is flagged, and gives you a next step. That's good product thinking, reducing anxiety through clarity and making the patient feel like a participant rather than a bystander. It's not perfect, plenty of results still come without enough context, but the core design decision to default toward transparency and action is the right one.

That principle, meeting users where they are and reducing the cognitive burden of navigating complexity, maps directly to what Solace is trying to do at a much larger scale across the entire healthcare journey.
