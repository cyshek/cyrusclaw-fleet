## Please describe a product you helped build that you are most proud of.

The product I'm most proud of is the Resilience Automation Platform I built at Microsoft. When I joined the team, the recovery validation program was a 2-person manual operation with no scalable infrastructure. I owned the product end-to-end, from writing requirements to shipping self-service scheduling capabilities, and grew it into a platformized ecosystem that now supports 45+ annual resilience drills and has driven $14M+ in impact across enterprise customers.

What made this especially meaningful was the 0-to-1 nature of it. There was no playbook. I had to balance quantitative pipeline data with qualitative feedback from partner teams like Databricks, Walmart, and SAP to figure out what to build and in what order. One of the biggest wins was reducing operational toil by 30% through the self-service capabilities, which let the team shift from manual execution to a scalable model. I also shipped Azure's first rack-level proactive resilience drill in 4 months, hitting a 94% recovery rate and surfacing real hardware defects that wouldn't have been caught otherwise.

Late in the build, I layered in an AI agent to automate planning workflows, which cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased drill throughput by 21%. That combination of building the core product, the surrounding process, and then improving it with AI is what makes this one stick with me. It wasn't just shipping features. It was building something that teams actually relied on and that measurably changed how the organization operated.
