## Please describe a product you helped build that you are most proud of.

The product I'm most proud of is the Resilience Automation Platform I built at Microsoft for Azure. When I started, recovery validation was a manual, 2-person operation with no standardized workflows, no self-service capabilities, and no real path to scale. I owned it from 0 to 1 - defining the product requirements, designing self-service scheduling capabilities, and building the processes around it so small operator teams could actually use it without needing me in the room for every execution. By the end, the platform was sustaining 45+ annual resilience drills, had reduced operational toil by 30%, and drove $14M+ in business impact across enterprise customers like Databricks, Walmart, SAP, and NetApp.

What makes me most proud isn't just the numbers - it's that I built it under genuine ambiguity. There was no playbook, limited historical data, and constant pressure from executive-visibility executions. I also incorporated an AI agent into the drill planning workflows, which cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased drill capacity by 21%. I balanced quantitative metrics with qualitative feedback from internal operator teams, running discovery sessions to surface workflow pain points and using those inputs to drive roadmap decisions.

It's the kind of product where I had to earn alignment across engineering, operations, and go-to-market partners, ship fast under tight timelines, and then hand off something that runs without me. That combination of 0-to-1 building, measurable business impact, and durable process design is what I look for in the work I do - and it's exactly the kind of ownership I want to bring to Tarro.
