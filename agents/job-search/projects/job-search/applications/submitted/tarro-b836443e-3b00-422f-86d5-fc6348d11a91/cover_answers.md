## Please describe a product you helped build that you are most proud of.

The product I'm proudest of is the Resilience Automation Platform I built at Microsoft. I owned a new product vertical end-to-end, scaling Azure's recovery validation from a 2-person operation into a platformized ecosystem that now sustains 45+ annual resilience drills and drove $14M+ in business impact across enterprise customers like Databricks, Walmart, SAP, and NetApp. It started as a manual, toil-heavy effort and I had to build both the product and the processes around it so teams would actually adopt it.

What makes me proud is that it was genuinely 0 to 1. I defined the product requirements and a self-service scheduling model backed by both quantitative analysis and real user interviews with partner service teams, which cut operational toil by 30% and got adopted across engineering teams. I also pioneered Azure's first proactive resilience testing capability, delivering a rack-level drill program in 4 months at a 94% recovery rate that surfaced critical hardware defects nobody had caught before.

The part I learned the most from was balancing data with anecdotes. The dashboards told me where the problems were, but sitting with the engineers running these drills told me why. Later I built an internal AI agent and restructured planning workflows, which cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased drill capacity by 21%. Watching something I shipped become the default way a whole org operates is the kind of impact I want to keep chasing.
