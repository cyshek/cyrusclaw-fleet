## Please describe a product you helped build that you are most proud of.

The product I'm most proud of is the Resilience Automation Platform I built at Microsoft, which I took from 0 to 1 as the PM. The core problem was that Azure's recovery validation process was manual, slow, and didn't scale - we were running drills for customers like Databricks, Walmart, SAP, and NetApp, but execution depended on a small team and tribal knowledge. I defined the self-service scheduling requirements, designed feedback loops that made the system trustworthy over time, and drove cross-functional alignment to ship it. The result was a 30% reduction in operational toil and a platform that could sustain 45+ annual drills without adding headcount.

What makes me proud of it isn't just the outcome - it's the kind of problem it was. The value was earned through data quality and customer trust, not just features. I had to partner with data science to build extraction and normalization pipelines that converted raw telemetry into drill intelligence customers could actually act on in real time. I also had to care deeply about explainability and auditability, especially on a sovereign-cloud test tied to a $1.5B+ enterprise contract where every decision had to be traceable. That combination - building a trust-earning data product under high-stakes conditions - is exactly the muscle I want to keep developing at Ramp.
