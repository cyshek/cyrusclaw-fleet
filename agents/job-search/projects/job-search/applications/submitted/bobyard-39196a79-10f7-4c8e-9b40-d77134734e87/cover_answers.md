## What interests you about Bobyard?

The Forward Deployed Engineer model maps almost exactly to how I've worked best. At Microsoft, I embedded directly with enterprise customers like Databricks and Walmart to run resilience drills, then turned what I learned in the field into a platform capability that eventually handled 45+ annual exercises. I also built an internal AI agent for drill planning that cut cycle time by 39% and got adopted as a platform-wide feature. That loop of discovery, rapid build, and converting one-off solutions into durable infrastructure is exactly what this role describes, and it's the work I find most energizing.

Bobyard's focus on general contractors is a space where the tooling is genuinely underdeveloped relative to the complexity of the workflows, and that gap means the software you ship has immediate, measurable impact on how real projects run. I'm drawn to the idea of being close enough to the customer to feel that impact directly, and then owning the full stack from data model to deployment to make sure the solution actually holds up in production. Building in that environment, where patterns you establish become core product, is the kind of ownership I'm looking for.
