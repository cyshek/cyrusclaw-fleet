# Cover answers — Gomotive, GTM/Sales Onboarding Program Manager (motive-8604981002)

## What about Motive makes it an appealing place to work?

What draws me to Motive is the direct connection between the platform and real-world operations. I've spent the last couple of years at Microsoft building systems that help enterprise teams execute more reliably at scale, and what I found most motivating in that work was when the output had a tangible effect on how people actually did their jobs. Motive is doing that for an entire category of workers who don't sit at desks. That's a genuinely compelling problem space.

The specific GTM enablement challenge here also resonates with me. Motive serves nearly 100,000 customers across a wide range of industries, which means the revenue teams need to deeply understand diverse buyer contexts and move fast. Building an onboarding program that actually prepares reps for that complexity, rather than just checking boxes, is the kind of systems-thinking work I find energizing. The fact that Motive is at the intersection of AI, fleet operations, and enterprise sales puts it in a category where the enablement function actually has to keep pace with a fast-moving product. That's the environment I want to be in.

## This role is an individual contributor role (not a managing a team). If you are currently managing a team please share why you'd be open to an IC role?

I'm currently an individual contributor at Microsoft, so this isn't a step down in scope for me. My work as a Program Manager has always been about driving outcomes through influence rather than direct reports. I coordinate across six or more teams, run executive-visible programs, and own end-to-end delivery without managing anyone directly. That's the model I know and enjoy.

What I find most satisfying is doing the craft work well: designing a system, facilitating a session, building a feedback loop, and seeing it actually change how people perform. An IC enablement role at Motive lets me go deep on that instead of spreading attention across personnel management. I'm not looking for a management title. I'm looking for a high-impact program to own and make better, and this role is exactly that.
