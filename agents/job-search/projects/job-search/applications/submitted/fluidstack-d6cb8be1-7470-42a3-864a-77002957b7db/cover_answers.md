## Tell us about something you've built/done that you think is genuinely cool, big or small, work or personal.

At Microsoft, I built an AI agent from scratch to restructure how our team planned infrastructure recovery drills. Before it existed, drill planning was a manual, coordination-heavy process that bottlenecked capacity. The agent automated the scheduling logic, conflict resolution, and documentation prep that used to eat up hours of engineer time per cycle. The result was a 39% reduction in planning cycle time and a 21% increase in drill capacity - which directly compressed mean time to resolution for real infrastructure incidents, not just exercises.

What I find genuinely cool about it is that it started as a small internal bet with no formal headcount or budget behind it. I scoped it, pushed it through, and it ended up changing how the whole operation ran. It also fed into a broader platform shift - scaling recovery validation from a 2-person manual workflow into a system sustaining 45+ annual drills with $14M+ in measurable business impact. The AI piece was maybe 10% of the total work, but it was the part that unlocked the throughput ceiling everything else was hitting.
