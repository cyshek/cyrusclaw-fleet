## What are the most interesting aspects of Perplexity that you are excited to work on?

The area I'm most excited about is the intersection of Perplexity Computer and enterprise knowledge work, specifically in the cloud infrastructure and developer tooling space. At Microsoft, I built an internal AI agent for drill planning that cut planning cycle time by 39% and increased drill capacity by 21%. That experience taught me how much latent productivity is locked up in fragmented workflows, and how powerful it is when an AI system can actually take action, not just surface information. Perplexity Computer is the first product I've seen that feels like it was designed from the ground up to close that gap at scale.

What pulls me toward Perplexity specifically is the conviction behind the 'knowledge into action' bet. Most productivity tools stop at retrieval or summarization. The leap to agentic execution, where the system actually completes work on behalf of the user, is the hard problem, and it's the one worth solving. I want to help define what that looks like for enterprise users who are drowning in operational toil today, the same population I've spent the last two years building for. The mission to power curiosity resonates with me because the people I've built for at Microsoft are exactly that: engineers and operators who want to spend less time on process and more time on the hard, interesting problems.
