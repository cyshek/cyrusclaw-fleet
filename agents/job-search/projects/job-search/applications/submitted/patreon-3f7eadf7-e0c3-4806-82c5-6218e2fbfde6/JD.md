Patreon is a media and community platform where over 300,000 creators give their biggest fans access to exclusive work and experiences. We offer creators a variety of ways to engage with their fans and build a lasting business including: paid memberships, free memberships, community chats, live video, and selling to fans directly with one-time purchases.

Ultimately our goal is simple: fund the creative class. And we're leaders in that space, with:

- $10 billion+ generated by creators since Patreon's inception

- 100 million+ free memberships for fans who may not be ready to pay just yet, and

- 25 million+ paid memberships on Patreon today.

We're continuing to invest heavily in building the best creator platform with the best team in the creator economy and are looking for a **Scaled Program Manager (Contract)** to support our mission.

*This role is based in **New York or San Francisco** and open to those who are able to be in-office 2 days per week on a hybrid work model.*

## **About the Team**

The Scaled Creator team is part of Creator Success and exists for one reason: to help every small and medium sized creator on Patreon feel supported, and grow their business on Patreon. We're a small team managing over 300,000 creators, so everything we do is high touch at scale. We work across everything from scaled communications to 1:1 gold glove relationship management, meeting every creator where they are in their journey.

## **About the Role**

We're looking for a Scaled Program Manager (Contract) to own the programming layer for the top of the Mid-Market creator portfolio. This is a new role built for someone who wants to design a playbook and series of programs from scratch.

You'll build and run scaled, higher-touch programs for top mid-market creators, serving as a touchpoint for ~600 creators. You'll design email series, run office hours, publish newsletters, and build 1:many programming touchpoints that feel personal at scale. And when a creator in your cohort needs more (i.e. a growth conversation, a product walkthrough, a real human in their corner) you'll know when to step in.

## **What you'll need**

- 5 or more years of experience in creator success, partnerships, community management, or a related creator-economy role, or equivalent experience

- Demonstrated experience building or running scaled programs, lifecycle email campaigns, or creator programming

- Written communication skills demonstrated at scale

- Fluency with CRM and outreach tools (Salesforce, Airtable, or equivalent) and the ability to use data signals to prioritize work across a large creator cohort

- A track record of building repeatable processes and playbooks

- Able to define your own structure in an early-stage role - this program doesn't exist yet, and you'll be building the approach from the ground up

## **Bonus if you have**

- Active or former creator yourself

- Experience at a creator platform, MCN, talent management firm, or creator-focused startup
