## Why Anthropic?

The work I find most meaningful at Microsoft sits exactly at the intersection this role targets: I built an AI agent to automate drill planning workflows, which cut planning cycle time by 39% and scaled engineer throughput, and I led the 0-to-1 build of a Resilience Automation Platform where the core challenge was defining behavioral defaults, triaging ambiguous feedback from 140+ teams, and translating that into a prioritized roadmap. That pattern, taking fuzzy signals about how a system behaves and turning them into clear, shippable improvements, is what I want to do at scale on a product that actually matters to how people interact with AI every day.

What draws me specifically to Anthropic is the seriousness with which the company treats the question of what a model should and should not do. Most organizations treat behavior as a constraint to route around. Anthropic treats it as the core research and product problem, which means the Model Behaviors PM role is not a compliance function tacked onto a real product job. It is the product job. The alignment finetuning work, the focus on steerability and character, the emphasis on navigating tradeoffs where there is no clean right answer, those are exactly the kinds of problems I want to spend my time on, and I do not think many places are working on them with this level of rigor.
