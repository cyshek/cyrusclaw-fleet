## What are you looking for in your next role?

I want a role where I can own the full arc from customer conversation to shipped solution. At Microsoft I've spent the last year deploying production-critical workflows for enterprise customers like Databricks, Walmart, and SAP, running discovery sessions, translating field requirements into engineering specs, and building the integrations that make things actually work in production. That work has been meaningful, but it lives inside a large org with a lot of abstraction between me and the outcome. I want to close that gap.

Specifically, I'm drawn to roles where the technical work and the customer relationship are the same job, not handed off between teams. Building integrations, diagnosing data and logic issues, configuring workflows for specific customer setups, then feeding those field insights back into the product - that's the kind of tight loop I want to operate in. I also want to work on a problem that's genuinely hard and underserved. Insurance claims is a massive, complex domain that hasn't seen real technical reinvention, and I think AI has a real shot at changing that in a practical, near-term way.

Beyond the work itself, I want to be somewhere small enough that what I build matters visibly, and where I can grow quickly by being close to decisions rather than several layers removed from them.
