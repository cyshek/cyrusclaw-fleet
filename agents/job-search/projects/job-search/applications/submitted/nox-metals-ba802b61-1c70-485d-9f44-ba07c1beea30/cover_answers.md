## Why NOX METALS? This is an important question that will highly impact our decision making process.

My entire PM career has been about shortening the path from idea to running in production. At Microsoft I owned end-to-end delivery for Azure's resilience platform, writing specs engineers could build from without clarification meetings, walking operators through each rollout personally, and shipping Azure's first rack-level drill in 4 months with a 94% recovery rate. At Amazon Robotics I owned a legacy OS migration across 1,200+ floor stations, mapped every dependency, and achieved zero operational downtime across a 2,000+ unit pilot by sitting with operators through each phase. That combination, tight specs plus floor-level accountability, is exactly what Nox Metals is describing with NOX NEST, WAYNE, and Gondor.

What pulls me specifically to Nox Metals is the bet you are making: that software and automation can move metal to American factories faster than the industry thought possible. That is not a incremental improvement on an old supply chain, it is a re-architecture of how physical operations get supplied, and the product surface is real, operators, schedulers, buyers, and customers all touching the same system. I want to own that product end to end in an environment where a feature I ship on Monday shows up on a customer's dock by Friday. Detroit, in person, building something that matters for American manufacturing, that is the kind of work I am looking to commit to long term.
