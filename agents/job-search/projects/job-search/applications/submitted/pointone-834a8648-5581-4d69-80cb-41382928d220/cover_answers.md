## Why do you want to work at PointOne?

My work at Microsoft has been largely about taking messy, manual workflows and replacing them with systems that deliver hard, measurable ROI - whether that was building an internal AI agent that cut planning cycle time by 39%, or pioneering a RAG-style semantic search migration that reduced documentation lookup time by 83% for engineering teams. That work maps directly to what PointOne is doing: automating the tedious parts of professional workflows and proving the value in concrete numbers. I also spent real time as a forward-deployed partner, sitting alongside enterprise customers at companies like Databricks and Walmart to translate their operational needs into prioritized technical solutions. That combination of building AI pipelines and working directly with customers is exactly what this role asks for.

What draws me to PointOne specifically is that the problem is hard and the stakes are real. Law firms are notoriously resistant to new tooling, so the fact that PointOne is already one of the stickiest products in that market tells me the team has figured out something genuine about product-market fit. I want to be part of a company where the engineering decisions I make directly shape what customers experience, and where I can see the impact - not just in dashboards, but in how attorneys actually work. The technical stack, Go microservices on a serverless AWS backend with custom RAG pipelines, is close to patterns I have already worked within, so I can contribute quickly without a long ramp.
