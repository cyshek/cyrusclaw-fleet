## Why Higharc?

Most of my work at Microsoft has been about exactly what this role calls for: sitting down with people, mapping how they actually operate today, and translating those real-world workflows into a data-driven vision they can get excited about. I led discovery sessions with enterprise customers like Databricks, Walmart, and SAP to uncover current-state pain points, then shaped solution strategy that bridged how they worked to a more tech-forward future, scaling Azure's recovery validation from a 2-person operation into a platformized system. I also directed cross-org executions under executive visibility, including a test tied to a $1.5B+ enterprise contract, where I had to tailor playbacks and business cases for both leadership and the day-to-day operators. That mix of discovery, demos, ROI narratives, and executive-to-end-user storytelling is the core of being a Solutions Engineer.

Higharc appeals to me because you're tackling an industry that runs on fragmented, manual processes and betting that a single data-driven platform can connect Design, Sales, Purchasing, and Estimating. I've spent my career being the person who walks into an operation built on tribal knowledge and spreadsheets and helps a team see what's possible with a unified system, so the challenge of teaching homebuilders a new perspective fits how I like to work. The fact that you've got a founding team that shipped at Autodesk, EA, Nike, and Apple, plus strong strategic backing from people inside construction and building products, tells me this is a serious bet on changing a real, stubborn problem. I'd genuinely enjoy being on the field side of that, turning customer feedback into sharper messaging and helping move deals forward.
