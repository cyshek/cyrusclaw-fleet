## Please write a short note on a project you're proud of:

At Microsoft, I led the 0-to-1 build of an internal Resilience Automation Platform for Azure. When I joined, the team was running 45+ annual drills almost entirely by hand across a 2-person operation. I defined the provisioning requirements, designed self-service scheduling capabilities, and restructured the orchestration workflows so that drills could run in parallel without constant human coordination. The platform reduced operational toil by 30% and ultimately delivered $14M+ in impact across major enterprise customers including Databricks, Walmart, SAP, and NetApp.

What made it hard - and interesting - was that I was simultaneously building the measurement layer from scratch. There were no reliable metrics on task completion rate, time-to-value, or cost-per-execution when I started. I instrumented all of that, which ended up being what actually guided where we invested next. I also defined the artifact and review layer: standardizing logs, error traces, and recovery timelines so engineering leads could verify drill outcomes in minutes rather than digging through raw infrastructure telemetry. Getting the review experience right felt just as important as getting the automation right, and I think about that tradeoff a lot.
