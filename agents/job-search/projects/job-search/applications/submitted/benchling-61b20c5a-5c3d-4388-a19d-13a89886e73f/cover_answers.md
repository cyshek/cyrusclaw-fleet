## What AI tools are you currently using today and how are you using them?

I use AI tools daily across both product work and personal productivity. At Microsoft, I built and iterated on an AI agent for drill planning using LLM-powered automation, which cut planning cycle time by 39% and expanded drill capacity by 21%. That project pushed me to get hands-on with prompt design, workflow orchestration, and evaluating where LLM outputs are reliable enough to act on versus where a human needs to stay in the loop.

For day-to-day PM work, I use Copilot Studio to prototype automation workflows and test how AI-assisted tooling changes the developer experience before committing engineering resources. I also use LLM-based tools for drafting and sharpening product specs, synthesizing interview notes, and stress-testing requirement logic, basically as a fast second opinion on whether my thinking holds up. During my intern rotation I led the migration of internal documentation to an AI-powered semantic search system, so I have practical experience on the infrastructure side of making AI useful for developer onboarding.

More broadly, I think about AI tools through the lens of workflow redesign rather than feature addition. The question I keep asking is whether the AI is actually changing the shape of the work or just automating a step that was already fast. That framing has shaped how I scope agentic features and how I evaluate whether a developer platform primitive is worth building.
