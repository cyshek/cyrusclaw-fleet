**Who we are **

**About the role**

We're looking for a Technical Program Manager (TPM) to drive the Data Quality program, a critical initiative to build a scalable, governed, and reliable data platform across the company. You'll partner with Data Engineering, Data Science, Infrastructure, Product, and Business teams to eliminate customer-facing data errors, reduce manual QA, and establish a single source of truth for metrics.

 

In this role, you'll lead the Data Governance Council (DGC), a cross-functional body responsible for setting and enforcing data governance standards. The ideal candidate is a systems thinker who can translate technical complexity into clear, actionable plans and knows when to drive alignment versus when to let teams execute.

 

**What you’ll do***
*

- Own and drive complex, cross-functional technical programs from concept to launch, bringing clarity to ambiguous problem spaces.

- Steer program direction by proactively identifying risks and dependencies, driving alignment through strong stakeholder relationships.

- Drive the Data Quality program end-to-end, aligning initiatives across engineering, data, and product toward strategic goals.

- Lead the Data Governance Council (DGC), driving policy decisions on certification, contract standards, and metric ownership.

- Guide key workstreams including data contracts, pipeline observability, testing, lineage tracking, and freshness SLA compliance.

- Facilitate program OKRs, roadmaps, and execution plans while ensuring delivery through prioritization, dependency tracking, and blocker resolution.

- Champion data quality across the organization by enforcing metric definitions, SLAs, and clear ownership standards.

 

**What we’re looking for**

- A Bachelor’s degree in a related field is required

- 3+ years experience as a technical program manager or related discipline

- Proven track record of delivering complex technical programs in a matrixed organization

- Strong experience with data-related programs centered around quality, governance, or platform infrastructure

- Ability to influence outcomes across teams without direct authority through trust, data, and clear communication

- Technical fluency with data systems (comfortable reading code, writing SQL, understanding pipeline architecture)

- Comfort with ambiguity, shifting priorities, and high-pressure environments
