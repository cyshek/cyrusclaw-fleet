## Why are you interested in Optimism?

At Microsoft, I spent the last two years sitting exactly at the intersection this role describes: translating complex infrastructure requirements into actionable engineering specs, running production-readiness testing at scale, and serving as the technical bridge between enterprise partners and internal product and engineering teams. The sovereign-cloud network isolation test I led for a $1.5B+ contract is a direct analog to the compliance-heavy, multi-stakeholder diligence work this role calls for. Building the Resilience Automation Platform from scratch, defining architecture requirements, and enabling 45+ annual drills across partners like Databricks, Walmart, and SAP gave me a clear feel for what it takes to move institutional partners from evaluation to production. The 0-to-1 and scale-to-many motions are both things I have done in practice, not just in theory.

Optimism specifically appeals to me because the OP Stack bet - that most of Layer 2 activity can run on shared, open infrastructure rather than fragmented proprietary chains - is the kind of architectural conviction I find genuinely compelling. The fact that enterprises like Coinbase, Sony, and Kraken are already building on it means the institutional adoption curve is real and accelerating, not hypothetical. I want to work on the layer of the stack that actually matters for global financial infrastructure, and OP Labs is the clearest place to do that right now.
