## How have you incorporated AI tools or technologies into your daily work or projects to improve efficiency or outcomes? Please provide a specific example, including the tool used and the impact it had on your work.

At Microsoft, I built an internal AI agent specifically to streamline drill planning and integration workflow management across our resilience validation platform. The agent automated a previously manual process of scheduling, configuration lookup, and cross-team coordination that was eating up significant PM and engineering time each cycle. The impact was concrete: planning cycle time dropped by 39% and drill capacity increased by 21%, which meant the team could run more recovery executions without adding headcount or sacrificing quality.

Beyond that specific tool, I also led a project to migrate internal documentation to an AI-powered semantic search platform, implementing metadata standards that made cross-team data validation faster and more accurate. That effort cut lookup time by 83%, which sounds like a small quality-of-life win but had real downstream effects on how quickly engineers could find integration specs and schema documentation during time-sensitive incidents.

The pattern across both examples is the same: I look for workflows where manual effort is the bottleneck, identify where an AI layer can absorb that toil, and then define the requirements clearly enough that the tool actually gets built and adopted. That approach has been more effective for me than treating AI as a general-purpose assistant - scoping it tightly to a specific workflow problem is what makes the efficiency gains stick.
