## 7) Honest interpretation — real-but-faint edge, tie-back to the two prior same-class kills

**Where momentum lands relative to today's two survivorship mirages:**

| lane | L/S spread (full) | beats EW-104 OOS? | lane_honesty | disposition |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Fundamentals-PIT value/quality (#1) | **−52%** (decisively neg) | ❌ no | **FAIL** | CLOSE (pure survivorship beta) |
| BAB betting-against-beta (#2) | **−50%** (decisively neg) | ❌ no | **FAIL** | CLOSE (pure survivorship beta + low-beta drag) |
| **XSEC momentum (#3, this lane)** | **+29%** (mildly pos) | ✅ **yes (+193pp)** | **PASS** | **SHELF (real-but-faint, sub-bar, crash-prone)** |

- **Momentum breaks the pattern of the prior two — but not enough to promote.** Fundamentals and BAB were *pure* survivorship beta: their L/S spreads were decisively negative, so the entire long-only "win" was the survivor universe. Momentum is **qualitatively different** — its L/S spread is genuinely positive and it is the only one of the three to beat the dumb EW-104 control OOS. There IS a real cross-sectional momentum tilt in this universe.
- **But "real" ≠ "promotable."** The harvestable, survivorship-neutral spread (the L/S) is too weak (OOS Sharpe 0.38 < 0.50, full 0.16) and too crash-prone (−60% maxDD, −43% in the 2009 junk-rally) to stand alone. The gaudy long-only Sharpe (1.13 OOS) is ~half survivor beta (EW-104 alone is 0.87 OOS) and ~half a modest momentum tilt you cannot cleanly isolate market-neutral.
- **Pathology overlap with the priors:** same survivor-universe beta inflating the long-only headline; same lesson that the L/S spread is the only honest test. **Divergence from the priors:** the underlying factor is not dead here — it's just weak and crash-exposed on this small modern-survivor universe. Cost is not the killer in any of the three (12-1 breakeven 50bps); the **signal strength** is.
- **Why the spread is weak on THIS universe specifically:** 104 mega/large-cap survivors is a *terrible* universe for momentum. Academic J-T momentum lives largely in the **cross-section of smaller, more dispersed names** and in the **short leg** (shorting genuine losers/junk). A 104-name all-survivor large-cap set has (a) compressed cross-sectional dispersion and (b) a short leg full of names that *survived and mean-reverted up* (survivorship literally poisons the short leg — the biggest losers you'd short are exactly the ones that came back). That structurally caps the L/S spread and amplifies the crash risk.

**Bottom line:** Momentum passes the survivorship honesty gate that killed value and BAB, confirming a faint real cross-sectional edge — but on this 104-survivor large-cap universe the tradable market-neutral spread is sub-bar and crash-prone. **SHELF, do not promote standalone.** It is the most promising of the three same-class lanes and the one most worth revisiting on better data.
