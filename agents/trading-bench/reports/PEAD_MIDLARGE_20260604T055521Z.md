## 1. DATA FEASIBILITY (the first gate) — FEASIBLE, lookahead-clean

The prior feasibility memo (`reports/BACKTEST_PEAD_DATA_FEASIBILITY_20260530T171453Z.md`) found **no free, point-in-time analyst-consensus SUE feed** — serving today's restated estimates against historical dates would be a silent lookahead leak. That conclusion still holds. This lane **does not need that paid data**, because:

| Component | Source | Point-in-time? | Lookahead risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Earnings **dates** | SEC EDGAR Form 8-K Item 2.02 `filingDate` | ✅ Yes — filings are timestamped and immutable once filed | None |
| **Surprise** signal | Price-reaction proxy: `(reaction_close − pre_event_close)/pre_event_close` | ✅ Computed only from bars at/before the reaction bar | None — entry fills at `reaction_bar+1` (harness-enforced, pinned by `test_entry_fills_after_reaction_bar`) so the surprise is fully in the past at fill time |
| Bars | Alpaca IEX daily (`bars_cache`), ~2yr | ✅ | None |

**Why this is honest and not a leaky backtest:** the only place PEAD normally leaks is via restated/consensus SUE. We sidestep it entirely with the free price-reaction proxy. **No paid data was bought or needed.** The disclosed cost of the proxy: it collapses beat/miss direction into the price move, so ~10–20% of events (sell-the-news beats) get mislabeled vs the academic SUE measure. That is a *power* limitation, disclosed — not a lookahead leak.

**Coverage (verified):** 55 liquid S&P-500-tier names, all with deep EDGAR Item 2.02 history (13–57 lifetime events each). 1,599 distinct events; **437 genuinely in-window** over the ~2yr bar span; **90 trades** fired at the fixed +3% threshold (healthy sample, not low-n).

**Conclusion: this lane is NOT data-blocked.** Free, point-in-time, lookahead-clean. It earns a real backtest, reported below.

## 6. Honest discussion

**The good news.** This is the first PEAD lane that produced a *real, robust, near-front-door* signal: n=90 clean trades, a Sharpe plateau straddling 1.0, positive ann-on-deployed (+18%), shallow drawdown (−7%), trivial cost drag ($3.63 on $9k deployed). The liquid universe is fillable (the small-cap lane's fatal flaw is gone). The data path is free, point-in-time, and lookahead-clean. Drift in liquid large-caps is *not* zero — the proxy captures a genuine post-positive-surprise continuation, strongest at a 21–42-bar hold and higher surprise thresholds (extreme-decile concentration, exactly as Bernard-Thomas predict).

**The disqualifier.** Two hard binds. (1) The pre-committed config clears 0.926, not 1.0 — and I will not slide to the 3.5%/42 = 1.22 cell post-hoc, because that is precisely the multiple-comparisons goalpost-move the gate exists to stop. (2) More fundamentally, the strategy **never beats buy-and-hold SPX** on any cell: it earns ~13–15% while SPX earned 39% over the same span. The ~1.0 Sharpe comes from *low volatility* (the book is event-sparse and often partly in cash), not from out-returning the index. A strategy that delivers a third of SPX's return at a still-positive-but-mediocre Sharpe is not a promotable edge — it's a low-vol drag on a strong-bull window. The survivorship bias (small here — over 2yr essentially none of these 55 delisted, but direction is still positive) makes even this an upper bound.

**Recommendation.** **REJECT; stays in `strategies_candidates/`. Do NOT buy SUE data on the strength of this** — the binding failure is not signal-weakness fixable by better surprise ranking, it's that liquid-cap drift, even captured well, doesn't out-return the index it's drawn from. Paid SUE might lift the Sharpe a few tenths but cannot fix the no-beat-BH structural fact. The lane is **explored and closed**: small-cap PEAD = unfillable, liquid PEAD = fillable-but-can't-beat-SPX. PEAD-via-free-proxy is a documented null/near-miss across the size spectrum. Next dollar of research effort goes to a *different* archetype, not to PEAD parameter hunting (data-snooping at the margin).
