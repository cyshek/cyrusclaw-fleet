## 1. Data source

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Where do earnings dates come from? | **SEC EDGAR Form 8-K Item 2.02** filings (`https://data.sec.gov/submissions/CIK<10digit>.json`). Item 2.02 = "Results of Operations and Financial Condition" — the regulatory wrapper around every earnings press release. |
| Where does "surprise" come from? | **Price-reaction proxy.** Two modes supported in `pead_lib/strategy.py`: `o_to_c` (announcement day open→close) and `prev_close_to_close` (prior close→announcement-day close, captures overnight gap). Default = `prev_close_to_close` after `o_to_c` was shown to miss ~80% of true reactions because earnings drop after-hours and the bulk of the move happens in the open-gap. **No analyst-consensus SUE data is used** — that would require a paid feed (FactSet/IBES). |
| Cost / rate limit? | Free; SEC allows 10 req/s per IP with a descriptive `User-Agent`. Our universe = 10 GETs, well inside the limit. Per-symbol JSON cached to `.cache/earnings/<TICKER>.json`. |
| Coverage we got | Tested 10 mega-cap CIKs. Of those, **8 had ≥8 Item 2.02 events** in the 2024-06→2026-05 window (META 9 lifetime, JPM 4 lifetime — both dropped). See `_lib/earnings_edgar.py:TICKER_CIK`. |

## 7. Verdict

**REJECTED for promotion to `strategies/`.** Strategy stays in `strategies_candidates/`. Headline reasons:

1. **Trade count is structural-low.** 18 round-trips over 2 years from 8 symbols is what PEAD's event nature gives us. Bar A's 30-trade minimum is unreachable without either (a) a 10x wider universe, (b) a much longer history (we max out at ~2 yrs of Alpaca IEX free-tier daily bars in a single fetch), or (c) a multi-symbol harness that can deploy across the universe in one continuous backtest.
2. **Walk-forward per-regime medians are mostly ≤ 0.** Bear regimes split, chop is the worst regime (one zero-trade window, one losing window), bull regimes mostly flat-to-slightly-positive. This is consistent with PEAD being a weak effect in mega-caps (per Bernard-Thomas the effect is strongest in small caps with low analyst coverage), and our 8 names being too few to overcome event-randomness.
3. **The held-out window (2026-recent bull) produced 0 trades** — the gate cannot even be evaluated honestly there. That's not "the strategy failed the holdout"; it's "the strategy had nothing to say."

## 8. Honest discussion (2 paragraphs)

**On the data and the proxy.** The price-reaction proxy is the right call given budget constraints, but it costs us in two ways. First, only ~12% of EDGAR events crossed the +3% prev-close→close threshold (9 of 72 in the long span), so most of the "PEAD-eligible" events are filtered out as quiet reactions. The threshold is tunable downward, but at +1.5% you'd be entering on routine post-earnings noise and the academic literature is clear that the drift effect concentrates in the *extreme* surprise deciles. Second, mega-caps are wall-to-wall covered by analysts; consensus estimates are accurate; the unexpected component of earnings is small. Bernard-Thomas 1989 themselves showed the effect is ~3x stronger in the smallest size quintile vs the largest. So we're testing PEAD on the universe where the effect is weakest. A real PEAD strategy would (a) expand to small-mid caps where the inattention edge is real, (b) use SUE-based ranking (paid data), and (c) deploy across hundreds of names simultaneously so the law-of-large-numbers smooths out individual outliers.

**On the harness gap.** The single-symbol time-series harness in `runner/backtest.py` is fundamentally a mismatch for event-driven strategies. PEAD wants to deploy $100 on every positive-surprise event regardless of which symbol fires, then carry up to N concurrent positions, with cash flowing through a single shared account. Our harness gives each symbol its own $1000 starting equity and runs them in isolation; you can sum the dollar P&L (which I did) but you cannot compute a portfolio-level Sharpe, drawdown, or true win-rate. That's why the Bar A.3 row above is marked "undetermined." The clean fix is a multi-symbol event-driven harness with shared cash — it would also unlock #4 (mean-reversion), #1 (cross-sectional momentum), and any other strategy that wants a universe. Without that infra, PEAD as a standalone candidate in our tournament is structurally capped. I would **not recommend further parameter tuning on this candidate** — the next dollar of effort goes into the multi-symbol harness, not into hunting for a magic surprise_threshold or holding_bars combination that makes 18 trades look better than they are. Promoting this would be data-snooping at n=18.
