## 1. Why the result is what it is — the structural reason (the real finding)

The active cost model (`CostModel.alpaca_stocks()`) is **`spread_bps=2.0, fee_bps=0.0`** — a pure *basis-point* spread on traded notional, **with no fixed per-trade dollar floor** and **fractional-share fills** (`buy_qty = a_notional / fill_px`, no integer rounding). Verified by reading `runner/backtest.py` (lines 174–197, 482–521).

Consequence: scaling notional $100 → $1000 scales **both** gross PnL **and** total cost by exactly 10× (confirmed: dollar lead-lag total cost $0.3257 → $3.2567, a clean 10.00×). The **net return % and the Sharpe are therefore scale-invariant.** The "$100 cost-strangle" language in the prior reversal report refers to the *bps-on-notional drag being fixed at 4bps round-trip* — and that drag is **independent of notional size** in this harness. Raising notional does NOT dilute the bps cost; only a *lower-bps venue* or a *bigger gross edge per trade* would.

So the bug (WF defaulting to $100) was a real **consistency** defect worth fixing, but for these bps-only/fractional-fill lanes it was **never numerically binding** on the pass/fail decision. The tiny non-zero deltas (+0.01/+0.03) are sub-penny cost-rounding/path noise, not recovered edge — and they push the wrong way to matter (a +0.03 lift on a 0.27-wide gap is irrelevant).
