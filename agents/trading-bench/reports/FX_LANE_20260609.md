**One-line verdict:** **No no-leverage FX strategy beats SPX raw** (SPX +472% vs best FX +6.5% over 2006–2026) — *as expected*. **But there is a real, small, genuinely-uncorrelated edge:** the trend and cross-sectional-momentum baskets run at ~6–7% vol with **negative correlation to SPX (−0.14 / −0.10)** and are **positive in both equity crises in-sample (2008 GFC, 2020 COVID)**. Worth keeping as a **diversifier / crisis hedge**, not as a standalone return engine. ### Carry source — REAL rate differentials (not a proxy)
- `strategies_candidates/fx_lane/fx_rates.py` pulls **3-month interbank rates** for all 7 currencies from the **keyed FRED API** (`IR3TIB01<CC>M156N`, OECD MEI) via `runner.fred_cache` (never fredgraph.csv). All 7 verified reachable, monthly, 2003→2026.
- Carry differential for being long a pair = `r(base) − r(quote)`, forward-filled lookahead-safe (monthly stamp treated as availability date; `vintage='latest'` — interbank rates are near-market and barely revised; residual revision is a documented small assumption, not modelled).
- **The carry strategy used REAL rates (`used_proxy=False`).** The momentum-as-carry proxy fallback exists and is clearly labelled, but was **not** needed.
- Sanity (carry signs as of 2026-04-15, economically correct): USDCHF +3.8%, USDJPY +2.5%, USDCAD +1.5% (long USD earns carry); EURUSD −1.7% (long EUR pays carry ⇒ strategy shorts it).

### Cost model — `FxCostModel`
- One-way half-spread charged on |Δweight| of notional turned over. **Baseline 0.8 bp** one-way (realistic blended major); sensitivity grid **{0.5, 0.8, 1.0, 2.0} bp**. No commission / no financing (carry P&L modelled explicitly in the strategy, not smuggled into cost). FX majors are the cheapest liquid asset on earth — this is ~400× cheaper than the crypto model.
- **Cost monotonicity verified** (test-locked): higher bp ⇒ strictly lower net return, and impact scales with turnover (Carry 35 rebalances → cost barely moves it; XSMom 1253 rebalances → very cost-sensitive).

## 4. The real-but-small edge: uncorrelated crisis diversifier

Even though none beat SPX *raw*, the regime detail and correlations reveal a genuine, defensible property:

- **Negative / near-zero correlation to SPX (daily, full period):** Trend **−0.14**, XSMom **−0.10**, Carry +0.09. These are **uncorrelated low-vol return streams** (6–7% vol vs SPX 16.8%).
- **Positive in BOTH in-sample equity crises** (the whole point of a hedge):
  - **2008 GFC** (SPX −39.5%): Trend **+4.6%**, XSMom **+10.2%**, Carry −14.6% — all three *beat* SPX; trend/XSMom were outright positive while equities halved.
  - **2020 COVID** (SPX +15.3% full-year but a −34% intra-year crash): Trend **+5.6% (Sharpe 1.33)**, XSMom **+4.8% (Sharpe 1.13)** — high risk-adjusted returns in the panic.
- **Carry behaves exactly as theory predicts** (validating the real-rate implementation): crushed risk-off (2008 −14.6%, 2020 −10.4%), earns risk-on (2021-22 **+14.3%, Sharpe 0.94**). It's a clean risk-on/risk-off *factor*, not an edge over this window — the post-GFC rate-differential compression (the classic "carry is dead" decade) is the structural drag.

**Interpretation:** Trend and XSMom are textbook **diversifiers** — small standalone return, but negatively correlated and crisis-positive. In a multi-strategy book dominated by long-equity/long-vol-of-the-bull exposure, a 5–10% sleeve of FX trend would have *added* return precisely when the equity sleeve bled (2008, 2020). That is a real, if modest, portfolio-construction value — not nothing, but also not a money-printer.

## 5. Honest caveats

- **Near-efficient, low-vol, leverage-forbidden ⇒ small edges.** FX majors are the most efficient liquid market; with gross ≤ 1.0 the achievable edge is inherently a few % of vol, not equity-like compounding. Practitioners lever FX 5–10× precisely because the un-levered Sharpe is low — and leverage is rail-forbidden here, correctly.
- **In-sample regime windows.** The 10 windows are hand-picked (diverse on purpose, but chosen with hindsight). The walk-forward shows regime-dependence is real (every strategy has good and bad regimes); treat the crisis-alpha as suggestive, not a guarantee — n=2 crises in-sample.
- **Carry rate vintage.** Monthly OECD MEI 3m rates, `vintage='latest'`, monthly-stamp as availability date. Near-market series with tiny revisions, but not strict ALFRED point-in-time — a documented small assumption.
- **No 2008 gap (covered).** Unlike shallow free FX sources, the 2003/2006 data start means the GFC bear is *in* the sample — a genuine robustness win.
- **Costs:** spread-only, no commission/financing modelled (immaterial vs the SPX gap; financing/swap would slightly worsen carry).

---

## 6. Recommendation

- **Do NOT promote any FX strategy as a standalone return engine** — none beats SPX raw without leverage, and that won't change at gross ≤ 1.0.
- **DO keep Trend (and XSMom) on the bench as a candidate diversifier sleeve.** The negative SPX correlation + crisis-positivity is a real, test-validated property that would improve a blended book's drawdown profile. Revisit if/when a multi-strategy capital allocator exists to size an uncorrelated sleeve.
- **Carry:** keep as a *factor/indicator* (clean risk-on/risk-off read from real rates), not a traded strategy over this regime.
- **Next refinements (if pursued):** vol-targeting the FX sleeve to a fixed risk budget; combining trend+carry+XSMom into one blended FX book (their regime-complementarity — carry wins risk-on, trend wins risk-off — suggests the blend Sharpe > any single one); a true ALFRED point-in-time rate vintage for carry.
