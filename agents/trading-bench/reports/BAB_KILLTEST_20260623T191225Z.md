## PIT beta construction + lookahead canary (no leakage)

- **Beta = rolling OLS of daily name-returns on SPY daily-returns**, trailing window (default 252d), **strictly < as-of date** (`bisect_left(spy_dates, asof)` → the as-of bar is excluded). Requires ≥60 paired observations or the name is dropped that month.
- **Frazzini-Pedersen shrinkage:** `beta_shrunk = 0.6·beta_raw + 0.4·1.0` (default). Raw-beta (λ=1.0) also swept.
- **Rank at prior month-end on past-only betas → trade the following month.** Monthly rebalance (quarterly swept).
- **Lookahead canary:** a deliberately-cheating variant uses betas as of ~1 month *forward*. Honest full-Sharpe = **−0.104**, cheat = **+0.001** → **paths differ** (✓). If they had been identical, leakage would be suspected. (Even the *cheating* version is ~0 — there is simply no beta premium here, lookahead or not.)
- **Beta separation is real and correctly signed:** avg low-basket beta **0.704**, avg high-basket beta **1.322** (avg 101 names ranked/month, 246 rebalances). The sort works; the *premium* doesn't.

## Robustness sweep — broad plateau of *failure*, not a knife-edge

24 configs: window ∈ {126, 252, 504}d × shrink ∈ {0.6 shrunk, 1.0 raw} × bucket ∈ {quintile, tercile} × cadence ∈ {M, Q}. **OOS-positive: 10/24, none ≥ 0.5.**

Key patterns:
- **Shrinkage (λ=0.6, the actual FP method) is uniformly negative OOS** (range −0.068 to −0.498). On a low-dispersion large-cap universe, shrinking betas toward 1 compresses an already-thin spread and the L/S degenerates.
- **Raw beta (λ=1.0) is the only thing that climbs toward positive**, and even the *best* config — 126d / tercile / monthly — tops out at **OOS Sharpe 0.226** (IS 0.685). Every other raw config is 0.03–0.23. **Nothing approaches the 0.5 gate.** The single highest OOS number in the entire sweep is **0.226** — still a fail.
- **Longer windows (504d) are the worst** (more stale betas, OOS down to −0.50).
- This is the *good* kind of robustness reading: there is **no knife-edge argmax masquerading as an edge** — the surface is consistently weak/negative. The lane is genuinely empty, not under-tuned.

### What would need to be true to revisit
- **A point-in-time, survivorship-free, broad universe** (the academic test is all-cap CRSP 1926–2012). Our cached universe is 104 today's-survivor large caps — the wrong sample for BAB, which earns most of its premium in **small/mid and junk-vs-quality dispersion** that large-cap survivors lack. A real revisit needs delisting-inclusive PIT membership + a much wider cap spectrum (data we don't have free).
- **Treat it as a defensive/risk-off overlay, not standalone alpha** — the killer battery shows BAB L/S is a clean negative-beta hedge (huge in 2022/2011/tariff bears). *If* the live book ever wants a cheap convex bear hedge, BAB-L/S is interesting **as a sleeve** — but that's the "defensive-sleeve-only, never-alpha" verdict we already filed for cross-asset low-vol, now confirmed on single-names too. Not a new edge.
- It would need to clear the EW-104 control and show a positive OOS L/S spread — neither is close, so this is a confident close.
