## 1. What was built (methodology — MIRRORS the bond-leg engine exactly)

A self-contained driver (`reports/_fx_carry_leg_driver.py`) imports **only** `runner.fred_cache` (read-only). `runner/` (beyond reading fred_cache), `strategies*/`, crontab, `*.db` — all untouched. The Sharpe/metrics/backtest mechanics are line-for-line the bond-leg engine, so the corr(FX, bond) is computed against a **bit-perfect reconstruction** of the real bond leg (FULL Sharpe **0.5779**, OOS **0.4338** — matches the documented 0.578/0.434 exactly; see §3).

**Universe (adjclose ONLY; spot from `data_cache/yahoo_fx/`):** 7 G10 USD pairs aligned on their common calendar → **5,205 days, 2006-05-15 → 2026-06-18, 242 month-end rebalances** (AUD history is the binding constraint at 2006-05).

**Quote-convention normalization (the sign trap, handled carefully):** AUD/EUR/GBP/NZD are quoted USD-per-foreign (adjclose ↑ ⇒ foreign ↑ ⇒ long-foreign spot return = +Δ%); CAD/CHF/JPY are quoted foreign-per-USD (adjclose ↑ ⇒ USD ↑ ⇒ long-foreign return = the **reciprocal** return `pₜ₋₁/pₜ − 1`). Every pair's "spot return" is thus the return to being **LONG the foreign currency vs USD**, with a consistent sign.

**Rates (the OECD 3-mo interbank family, MONTHLY, via keyed FRED):** `IR3TIB01{US,EZ,GB,JP,CA,AU,CH,NZ}M156N` — one consistent interbank-vs-interbank family across **all** currencies incl USD, so the rate differential is apples-to-apples (not T-bill-vs-interbank). All 8 verified to return data: USD/CAD/AUD/NZD 1990→2026-05, EUR 1994→2026-01, GBP 1990→2026-01, JPY 2002-04→2026-04, CHF 1999-07→2026-05.

**Carry signal:** at each month-end T, `carry_ccy = (foreign 3-mo rate − USD 3-mo rate)` in decimal/yr (PIT). Canonical G10 carry portfolio = **long top-3 carry / short bottom-3 carry, EW per side, dollar-neutral** (primary). Also tested **long-only-positive-carry** (long all ccys with positive differential, EW, no shorts).

**Sleeve daily return = spot-return-long-foreign + CARRY ACCRUAL.** The accrual `w · rate_diff/252` is earned **every day held** on the signed weight (a long earns +diff/252, a short earns −diff/252) — this is the bulk of carry's economic return and is included explicitly, not just spot drift.

**Vol-target** the sleeve to **9% annual** (20-day realized vol of the raw sleeve, data ≤ T, cap **1.0×, never levered**) — identical to the bond leg, so the two legs are risk-comparable building blocks.

## 2. Anti-lookahead (the ballgame — matched to bond leg §22 + the canary)

- **Signal** computed on data **strictly ≤ dates[T]**; weights **effective at T+1** (1-trading-day lag), held constant daily, marked on adjclose spot returns. On a rebalance day the **freshly-traded weights do NOT capture that day's already-realized return** (cost charged on the trade day; new weights earn starting next bar) — conservative, no same-bar leak.
- **Rate publication lag:** the OECD monthly series is released with a lag, so on month-end T the signal uses the rate reading whose date ≤ **(T shifted back 1 month)** — i.e. a rate value is never used before it would plausibly have been published. (`extra_months=1`.)
- **CANARY (the lethal cheap test):** shifting the rate observation **one EXTRA month** back (total 2-month lag) leaves OOS Sharpe at **0.337** vs the primary **0.347** — a drop of only 0.01. **The edge survives the extra lag → it is NOT a publication-timing leak.** (Contrast VIX-term/SKEW, where +1 extra day collapsed the edge.)
- **Cost:** `(bps/1e4)·Σ|w_new − w_old|` on the trade day. Turnover **19.3%/rebal**. Grid 0/1/2/5 bps is monotonic and barely moves the result (OOS 0.351→0.342 across the whole grid) — **cost is not the constraint; the thin premium is.**

## 6. Orthogonality + the COUNTERFACTUAL combined sleeve (the tantalizing part)

**corr(FX carry, bond leg) = −0.118 OOS / −0.170 full** — the two carry legs are genuinely, *negatively* correlated (gate (b) passes with huge room). **corr(FX carry, SPY) = +0.288, corr(FX carry, TQQQ) = +0.250** — both < 0.5, modestly positive (the expected risk-on tilt of a carry book; less orthogonal to equities than the bond leg's −0.20/−0.14, but still a diversifier).

**The gate (a)+(b) decision:** (b) passes, **(a) fails (0.347 < 0.4)** → the spec says **do NOT build** the combined sleeve as the headline. For honesty I built it anyway (`combined_sleeve.counterfactual_if_gate_a_relaxed` in the JSON):

**This clears 0.5 OOS (0.93 inverse-vol / 0.73 equal-dollar) and beats the combined-EW control by +0.55 Sharpe — exactly the "diversification alone tips two ~0.4 legs over 0.5" thesis math, amplified because corr is *negative*.** Combined orthogonality is excellent (corr→SPY **−0.05**, →TQQQ **−0.01**). **So why is this still a CLOSE, not a BUILD?** Two honest red flags:

1. **IS/OOS instability.** The combined OOS Sharpe (0.93) towers over its **IS** Sharpe (0.375 inverse-vol / 0.464 equal-dollar — *both under 0.5*). A structurally-sound 0.5+ sleeve clears the bar in BOTH halves; this one only clears it OOS. The headline FULL combined (0.517) is barely over 0.5 and is dragged there entirely by the OOS half.
2. **It rests on a sub-0.4 leg with NO robustness plateau.** The combined PASS inherits all of FX carry's fragility: 0/8 sweep cells clear 0.4 standalone (§7). Promoting a sleeve built on a leg that fails its own standalone bar across every reasonable knob is exactly the knife-edge the BAB lesson warns against.

**The tiny 3.3% OOS vol is the tell:** the inverse-vol blend earns a *risk-adjusted* 0.93 on a sleeve making ~3.1% CAGR. The Sharpe is real arithmetic, but it is a thin-premium, low-vol diversifier whose flattering OOS ratio is not matched in-sample.

## 7. Robustness sweep — NO plateau above the bar (the honest knock)

8 configs: mode ∈ {long-short, long-only-positive} × (top-N/bot-N) ∈ {2/2, 3/3} × vol-target ∈ {8%, 10%}. All net 2 bps.

**Interpretation (per the BAB knife-edge standard):**
- The **long-short** variants form a tight, *consistent* cluster (0.34–0.37 OOS, all positive) — the signal is real and stable in *shape* (dollar-neutral high-minus-low is the construction that works; long-only-positive is reliably negative because it's just long-FX-beta with a carry tilt). That consistency is the honest signature of a genuine-but-thin edge, not a lucky cell.
- **But not a single cell clears 0.4**, let alone 0.5. By the strict bar — "the leg must clear its standalone Sharpe across reasonable knobs" — FX carry **fails the robustness bar for gate (a)**. There is no plateau above 0.4; the entire long-short surface sits at ~0.35.

## 8. VERDICT — CLOSE FX carry as the standalone 2nd leg (honest near-miss, real signal, but too thin + IS/OOS-unstable to BUILD)

**(a) FAIL (OOS 0.347 < 0.4, 0/8 cells clear 0.4) / (b) PASS (corr −0.12) / (c) PASS decisively (beats EW by +0.625) / (d) MIXED (co-crashes 2008/2020, ~flat 2022).** Because **(a) fails**, the combined sleeve is not the headline build, and the lane **CLOSES** as the 2nd carry leg.

This is the **same kind of honest negative as the bond leg** — and a *fundamentally different* failure than BAB/fundamentals:
- ✅ **Economically real and leak-free:** the canary (+1 extra month rate lag) preserves the edge (0.347→0.337); cost-immune; quote-convention sign handled exactly; bond reconstruction bit-perfect.
- ✅ **Beats its EW control by a mile OOS** (+0.625 Sharpe) — carry *timing* genuinely adds value; dumb-long-FX loses money. Not USD-beta in disguise.
- ✅ **Genuinely orthogonal to the bond leg** (corr −0.12, *negative*) and to the book (corr→SPY +0.29, →TQQQ +0.25). The two legs crash in different regimes (§5).
- ❌ **Too thin on its own** (OOS 0.347, FULL 0.165; the premium is entirely OOS-loaded, IS ~flat) and **no robustness plateau above 0.4**.
- ⚠️ **The combined sleeve clears 0.5 OOS (0.73–0.93) — but NOT in-sample (IS 0.375–0.464)**, and rests on a sub-0.4 leg. That IS/OOS instability + the dependence on a fragile leg make the combined PASS **not robust enough to promote**.

### Disposition
**Do NOT build the FX-carry leg or the bond+FX combined sleeve as a promoted strategy.** The combined OOS 0.5+ is real arithmetic but fragile (regime-loaded, sub-0.4 building block). **The H1 cross-asset carry thesis has now been tested on all three asset-class legs and none clears the bar as a promotable sleeve:** bond curve-carry (NEAR-MISS, OOS 0.434), commodity roll-carry (CLOSED, dirty proxy), FX rate-differential carry (CLOSED here, OOS 0.347). **Recommend closing the H1 carry lane** unless the mandate shifts to eff-N raising.

### Honest revisit-triggers (if the mandate shifts)
- **If the bar moves from "promote a ≥0.5 standalone sleeve" to "raise the allocator's eff-N / Sharpe of the *blend*":** the bond+FX combined is a legitimate **eff-N raiser** — two negatively-correlated thin diversifiers (corr −0.12), combined corr to the book −0.05/−0.01, positive return. As a *small risk-weighted allocation alongside the live book* (not a standalone promote), it is shelf-worthy. That is an allocator decision, not a standalone-Sharpe decision.
- **If a cleaner/deeper FX carry instrument appears:** the monthly OECD rate panel (released with a lag, EUR/GBP stale to 2026-01) and the 2006+ AUD-limited history are real constraints. Daily money-market rates or a longer pre-2006 spot panel could sharpen the IS half and reveal whether the OOS 0.35 is the true level or an underestimate.
- **The combined-sleeve OOS result is the one tantalizing thread:** if FX carry's standalone OOS were even ~0.4 (it is 0.347), gate (a) would pass and the combined 0.7–0.9 OOS would be a genuine BUILD candidate — but only if it also held in-sample. It does not today.
