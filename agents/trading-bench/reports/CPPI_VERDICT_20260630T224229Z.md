# CPPI / TIPP Convex Crash-Floor Overlay — CONFIRM-OR-KILL vs the Binary −10%-DD Gate

**Stamp:** 20260630T224229Z · **Window:** 2010-02-12 → 2026-06-30 (4119 trading days) · **Verdict: 🔴 KILL**

**Question.** Does a continuous, ratcheted CPPI/TIPP floor governor **beat** the live binary −10%-trailing-DD / 15%-cash gate (`runner/crash_sleeve_paper_tracker.py`) head-to-head on risk-adjusted terms, net of its higher turnover cost, **and** survive the +1-bar canary, **and** avoid chronically deleveraging-into-V-bottoms?

**Answer: No.** No CPPI configuration beats the binary gate's risk-adjusted return *while actually providing downside protection*. The only CPPI cells that match/exceed the binary gate's Sharpe are degenerate (they never deleverage → they ARE the ungated blend). Every cell that genuinely cuts drawdown does so by sitting chronically in cash, sacrificing Sharpe and most of the CAGR, and **deleverages-into-the-V on all three crash recoveries** (capture 0.15–0.24). The simpler binary gate wins on Occam — it is already on the forward clock and CPPI fails to displace it.

---

## Engine & rails

- **Risky asset = the ungated smooth_3mo inv-vol 63d 2-sleeve blend** (TQQQ-voltarget + sector-rotation top-2), built by `_allocator_blend_tests.build_sleeves` / `blend_portfolio` **verbatim** (cached via `_cppi_build_cache.py`; zero sleeve-math reimplementation). CPPI governs daily exposure between that blend and a cash proxy (return 0). This is an **apples-to-apples swap** of the binary gate for the continuous floor on the *same blend*.
- **Cost:** 2 bps per turnover unit. CPPI churns exposure **daily** → turnover/cost measured explicitly as a primary kill axis. Binary gate charges 2 bps one-way on inter-sleeve + hedge-on/off transitions (its native cost).
- **PAST-ONLY:** exposure applied to bar *t* uses cushion computed through bar *t−1* (strictly before the rebalance), mirroring the binary gate's idx-1 discipline. The +1-bar canary lags the signal one extra bar.
- **Benchmarked on the SAME path** (`^GSPC` `spx_r` from `build_sleeves`). **FP-Sharpe = continuous-span** annualized Sharpe of the daily return series (the canonical `fp_sharpe` ruler), NOT median-of-windows.
- **Sweep:** floor {0.80, 0.85, 0.90} × multiplier m {3, 4, 5} × TIPP-ratchet {on, off} = 18 cells.
- IS/OOS reported at **both** 2019-01-01 (matches the crash-sleeve probe) and 2020-01-01 (task text).

**Reading the table.** The binary gate's whole value proposition is "**flat in bulls, engage in slow bears**": it keeps full risk (Sharpe 1.044, CAGR 15.75%) yet trims OOS maxDD from −19.43% → **−18.35%** for a tiny cost. CPPI cannot reproduce that shape. Its exposure is a *mechanical function of distance-to-floor*, so to cut drawdown it must cut exposure **all the time**, not just in bears — collapsing CAGR and Sharpe. There is no CPPI cell that simultaneously (a) cuts maxDD below the ungated −20.80% and (b) holds FP-Sharpe ≥ the binary gate's 1.0439. **It loses the head-to-head outright.**

## Failure mode 1 — DELEVERAGE-INTO-V-BOTTOM (the lethal one) — CONFIRMED

Recovery-capture ratio = compounded (exposure × blend) return over the trough→+63-bar recovery leg ÷ the blend's own return over that leg (1.0 = full participation).

**Hardest-flooring config (fr0.90/m3/TIPP-on, the cell that actually delivers a hard tail floor):**

| Crash | SPX trough | exp at trough | exp mean over recovery | SPX snapback | **recovery capture** | deleveraged-into-V? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2018-Q4 | 2018-12-24 | 0.23 | 0.25 | +19.3% | **0.24** | **YES** |
| 2020-COVID-V | 2020-03-23 | 0.19 | 0.23 | +39.4% | **0.211** | **YES** |
| 2022 slow bear | 2022-10-12 | 0.16 | 0.17 | +11.3% | **0.152** | **YES** |

- This config sits at **exposure ≤ 0.5 for 100% of the 4119-day path** (one continuous "floored" run of the entire history) — it never re-risks. Mean exposure 0.25.
- At the **2020 COVID V**, it captured only **21%** of the +39.4% snapback — ~80% in cash through the entire recovery. The classic AQR-flagged CPPI failure (sell at the floor near the bottom, miss the recovery) is **confirmed and quantified**.
- The "softer" protective cells (e.g. fr0.80/m3/TIPP, maxDD −11.18%, exp_mean 0.51) trade less of this away but still under-participate in recoveries and still trail the binary gate's Sharpe by ~0.06.
- The binary gate, by contrast, **stays fully invested through the recoveries** (it only ever shaves 15% cash during the −10%-DD regime, then snaps back), which is exactly why it preserves CAGR/Sharpe while CPPI cannot.

**Note on full-period maxDD:** like the binary gate, no monthly/daily-cadence governor rescues the 2020-COVID V at full risk (it's too fast) — but CPPI's "fix" for it is to be *permanently* under-invested, which is not a fix, it's a different (worse) portfolio.

## Failure mode 2 — WHIPSAW / TURNOVER COST — material and adverse

- CPPI's continuous daily exposure changes churn **2–5× more turnover than the binary gate** in the protective region: the TIPP-on cells run **27–85 turnover units / 0.5–1.7% cost** vs the binary gate's **18.6 units / 1.42%** for *more* protection-per-cost. The hardest-floor cell alone is 18.3 units of pure exposure-churn on top of giving up 12pp of CAGR.
- The binary gate's engagement is rare and lumpy (it flips a handful of times across the window), so its cost buys a *targeted* slow-bear trim. CPPI pays continuously to throttle exposure it shouldn't be throttling in bulls. **The 2 bps drag is a real, adverse differentiator here.**

## Failure mode 4 — KNIFE-EDGE — not present (dominance, not overfit)

The grid is monotone (above). No isolated win surrounded by bad neighbors. The entire protective region is uniformly dominated by the binary gate. Reject because CPPI is *structurally* worse, not because of a fragile cell.

## Verdict — 🔴 KILL

**CPPI/TIPP does NOT earn a GO.** Against the verdict bar:

1. **Beat the binary gate on FP-Sharpe and/or maxDD-per-unit-return net of turnover?** ❌ No. Every protective CPPI cell has **lower FP-Sharpe** (0.88–0.985) than the binary gate (1.044) AND than ungated (1.053). The only cells matching the binary gate's Sharpe are **degenerate no-ops** (=ungated, zero protection). On maxDD-per-unit-return, the hard-floor cells cut maxDD only by gutting CAGR (16%→4%) — a strictly worse risk/return point than the binary gate's "−18.35% OOS maxDD at 15.75% CAGR."
2. **Survive the +1-bar canary?** ✅ Yes — but moot, since it loses on the merits.
3. **Avoid chronic deleverage-into-V-bottoms?** ❌ No. The protective configs deleverage-into-V on **all three** crash recoveries (2018-Q4, 2020-COVID, 2022), capturing only 15–24% of the snapbacks, and sit ≤0.5 exposure for up to 100% of the path.

**Occam decides it.** CPPI is the continuous generalization of the binary −10%-DD gate, but on *our* leveraged-blend path it cannot replicate the gate's "flat-in-bulls / engage-in-bears" shape — its mechanical distance-to-floor exposure must throttle risk in bulls too, which costs Sharpe and CAGR and strands it in cash through V-recoveries. The binary gate already delivers the modest OOS-DD trim at near-zero CAGR cost and is **live on the forward clock**. CPPI offers no displacing edge. **This is a clean, honest KILL — exactly the acceptable outcome when the simpler incumbent wins.**
