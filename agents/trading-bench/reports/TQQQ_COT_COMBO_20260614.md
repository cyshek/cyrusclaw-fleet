## 6. Detailed OOS Analysis (Why BETTER_THAN_BASELINE)

| Metric | TQQQ+COT | VT Baseline | Delta | Win? |

| **MaxDD** | **-32.9%** | **-41.8%** | **+8.9pp** | ✅ **Much lower DD** |

**The core tradeoff:** COT overlay scales down to 50% when AMs are reducing S&P futures exposure (47.7% of OOS days). This correctly reduces TQQQ exposure during drawdown periods at the cost of missing some bull-run gains.

**The 2022 regime** is where this likely earned its keep: COT AMs were reducing in 2021-2022 → scale-down triggered → TQQQ exposure reduced during the -82% TQQQ bear. The vol-target alone compressed this somewhat (from -82% raw to -41.8% OOS maxDD), but the COT overlay pushes it further to -32.9%.

**Critical question — is the OOS Sharpe improvement real or noise?**  
The standalone COT study showed am_momentum Sharpe jumped from 0.540 (IS) to 0.929 (OOS), which is a regime-dependency red flag. Here the combination Sharpe improves from 0.749 (IS) to 0.960 (OOS) — same direction but the IS is not degenerate (0.749 is respectable). The IS underperformance vs VT (0.749 vs 0.868) with OOS outperformance (0.960 vs 0.896) is consistent with a genuine diversification benefit rather than pure overfitting: the COT signal is adding DD reduction at IS returns cost but OOS pays off.
