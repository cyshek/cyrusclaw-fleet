# SCOUT — Options / IV-surface / Skew (FREE routes only)

**Scout class:** Options-implied data — implied-vol surfaces, skew, term-structure, put/call ratios, vol-of-vol, dealer-positioning proxies.
**Date:** 2026-06-05 · **Context:** trading-bench (Tessera), paper-only, ZERO spend, scouting only.
**Verification:** All access + history-depth claims below were live-tested via `curl`/`web_fetch` on 2026-06-04/05 (not asserted from memory). Endpoints that responded are quoted with first/last dates actually returned.

---

## TL;DR verdict for this class

Options-implied signal is **genuinely orthogonal** to OHLCV — it encodes the *risk-neutral distribution* (fear, tail-pricing, demand for protection), not realized price. The catch the spec warned about is real: **most free options history is shallow.** But there is one big exception that saves the class — **CBOE's own free volatility-index CSVs go back to 1990** and cover *every* bear regime (2008/2020/2022). Those are processed, single-series indices (not a full surface), but they are exactly the orthogonal fear/skew/vol-of-vol signals we want, at trivial ingestion cost. The *full strike-level surface* (the OptionMetrics-style thing) is only available free from 2019 onward (DoltHub) or 2021 onward (Deribit/crypto). OptionMetrics itself and ORATS are **paid** — noted as the gold standard we can't afford.

**Bottom line:** the high-EV play is the **CBOE free index family** (deep history, trivial ingest, high orthogonality), optionally augmented by the **DoltHub free options DB** for a real 2019+ surface and **Deribit DVOL** for a free crypto vol-of-fear series. Do NOT bother with Yahoo/yfinance options for regime work — no history.

## Notes / honesty on the class's main weakness

- **The spec's warning is correct:** free *full-surface* options history is shallow. The only free per-strike IV-surface sources cap out at **2019** (DoltHub, equities) or **2021** (Deribit, crypto). Neither sees **2008**, and only DoltHub sees **2020**. So if a strategy *requires* a real surface across all three bears, there is **no free source** — that's a genuine ceiling, and the honest answer is "buy ORATS (paid) or accept 2019+ surface coverage."
- **The escape hatch that rescues the class:** CBOE's **processed index series** (VIX/SKEW/VVIX/term-structure) DO go back to 1990/2006 and ARE free. We lose the per-strike granularity but keep the *signal essence* — implied vol level, tail-skew, vol-of-vol, and term-structure slope — across all bears. For breaking a ~0.5 Sharpe **signal** ceiling, these single-series fear/skew features are very likely enough and are the cheapest possible win.
- **Orthogonality caveat:** VIX *level* is inversely correlated with SPX returns (so partly "price in disguise"). The orthogonal alpha is in the **derived** features: SKEW (tail asymmetry), VVIX (vol-of-vol), and especially **term-structure slope** (VIX9D−VIX vs VIX−VIX3M/VIX6M: contango vs backwardation), which flips regime well before price does. Build features from those, not from raw VIX level.
- **Ingestion reality:** #1 (CBOE CSVs) is a 15-minute integration — dated CSV, `pandas.read_csv`, done. #2 (DoltHub) is SQL-API or a one-time `dolt clone`; aggregates time out so paginate by date. #3 (Deribit) is a clean JSON loop. Everything else is either redundant (#4), paywalled (#5/#6-deep/#8), or historyless (#7).
