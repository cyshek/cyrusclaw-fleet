# FX Trend/Carry Lane — Independent Re-Evaluation (S1/S2/S3)

**Generated:** 2026-06-21 · **Status:** RESEARCH / PAPER-ONLY. No leverage anywhere (long/cash only; FX "long" = buy the pair, "cash" = flat at weight 0). No live orders, no live keys, no external spend. All code self-contained under `strategies_candidates/fx_lane/`; protected `runner/` files **not modified** (reused read-only: `fx_bars_cache.py`, `daily_bars_cache.py`).

**One-line verdict:** **Confirmed dead lane as a return engine — no path to a real edge in FX without leverage.** Across three independently-specified strategies, full-period and walk-forward-honest OOS (split 2018): **0 of 5 series beat SPX raw, and 0 of 5 reach Sharpe > 0.8** (best OOS Sharpe = 0.39 on USDJPY trend, vs SPX OOS 0.72; best full-period total = USDJPY trend +32.7% vs SPX +421%). Costs are negligible exactly as hoped — **the signal itself is weak and regime-dependent, not the spread.** This is the *third* independent FX evaluation to reach the same conclusion (see §6), now from the specific angle requested (faster SMA20/50 long-flat, AUD carry-proxy, 12-1 monthly XS-mom, 2018 OOS, Sharpe-focused).

## 1. Setup

- **Data:** Yahoo v8 daily FX majors via `runner/fx_bars_cache.py` (browser-UA, IP-unwalled from this VM, lookahead-safe `asof`/`asof_strict`, on-disk cache). FX has no splits/divs ⇒ `adjclose == close`. Spans: EURUSD/GBPUSD 2003-12, USDJPY 1996-10, AUDUSD 2006-05, USDCHF/USDCAD 2003-09 → 2026-06-09. **Evaluation window 2007-01-01 → 2026-06-09** (covers 2008 GFC + 2020 COVID + 2022 USD surge = real regime diversity).
- **Benchmark:** `^GSPC` adjclose buy-and-hold via `runner/daily_bars_cache.py`, measured on SPX's own trading days over the *exact same calendar window* as each strategy (apples-to-apples). SPX full window: **+421.4% / CAGR +8.9% / Sharpe 0.53 / MaxDD −56.8%**.
- **Cost model:** one-way half-spread on |Δweight| of notional turned over, charged on the realized day. **Baseline 1.0 bp one-way**; sensitivity grid {0.5, 1.0, 2.0} bp (§4).
- **Leak-free construction:** signal computed from closes **through bar t**; position applied to **bar t+1**'s close-to-close return (1-day lag). No-leverage **hard-asserted** every day (gross |w| ≤ 1.0). Annualization √252.
- **OOS split:** train/in-sample = 2007 → 2017; test/OOS = 2018 → 2026 (leak-free: SMA stays warmed across the boundary; only the realized-return *date* assigns train vs test).

## 2. The three strategies (all NO-LEVERAGE, long/cash only)

- **S1 — TREND:** per-pair `SMA(20) > SMA(50)` → long the pair (w=1), else flat (cash). Tested on **EURUSD, GBPUSD, USDJPY**. Daily check; cost only on switches.
- **S2 — CARRY proxy:** **AUDUSD** long (w=1) when its trailing 20-day momentum > 0, else flat. (AUD is historically the positive-carry major vs USD funding; 20d momentum is the requested no-rates carry proxy.)
- **S3 — XS-MOMENTUM (12-1):** monthly, rank the 6 majors by `12-month return minus 1-month return`; long top 2 at 0.5 each (gross ≤ 1.0), flat the bottom 4. Daily-path returns; cost on monthly rebalance.

## 5. Skeptical self-checks (all PASS)

1. **Lookahead leak test (USDJPY trend):** honest next-bar engine → Sharpe 0.238 / +32.7%; deliberately-leaky same-bar variant → Sharpe 0.312 / +47.1%. The leak inflates Sharpe **×1.31 in the higher direction** → confirms our 1-day lag is the conservative side and the reported numbers are **not** contaminated by the classic "signal and trade on the same close" leak. (Modest 1.31× because long/flat with a slow SMA flips weight rarely.)
2. **SMA truncation-invariance:** signal recomputed on the series cut at t (no future bars) is **identical** to the full-series signal at four probe dates. The moving average reads no future data. PASS.
3. **No-leverage invariant:** hard-asserted every simulated day (gross |w| ≤ 1.0 + 1e-9) across all strategies — never tripped.
4. **Param robustness (USDJPY trend, full-period Sharpe):** SMA 10/30 → 0.10 · 20/50 → 0.24 · 20/100 → 0.27 · 50/100 → 0.27 · 50/200 → 0.11 · 100/200 → 0.15. A soft plateau around 0.24–0.27 (no knife-edge), **but it never approaches 0.8** — the ceiling is structural.
5. **SPX benchmark sanity:** direct BH over the FX window reproduces +421.4% / CAGR +8.9% / Sharpe 0.53 / MaxDD −56.8%.

## 7. Answers to the task's two questions

- **Does any strategy beat SPX raw return?** **No.** Best is USDJPY trend +32.7% vs SPX +421.4% (~13× gap). Not close, in any window.
- **Does any get Sharpe > 0.8?** **No.** Best full-period Sharpe 0.24 (USDJPY trend); best OOS Sharpe 0.39 (USDJPY trend). SPX itself is only ~0.53 full / 0.72 OOS, and **nothing here even reaches SPX's Sharpe**, let alone 0.8.

### Why no leverage can't be the rescue
At ~6–8% unlevered vol and Sharpe ≤ ~0.25, reaching even SPX's *volatility* needs ~2.5–3× gross, and Sharpe ~0.25 means the levered return is mediocre with magnified −20%+ drawdowns. Leverage scales vol and Sharpe-implied return, not the Sharpe itself — a sub-0.4 Sharpe levered up is just a bigger sub-0.4-Sharpe book. **Leverage (rail-forbidden here anyway) does not create an edge that isn't there.**

---

## 8. Verdict & recommendation

- **CONFIRMED DEAD LANE for standalone return.** There is **no path to a real edge in FX without leverage** on this bench's mission bar (beat SPX raw). Do **not** build or promote an FX return strategy. This is now the third concordant negative; further single-pair / simple-signal FX return work is not a productive use of cycles.
- **The one residual, non-return value** (consistent with FX_LANE_20260609): FX strategies are **near-zero / negatively correlated to SPX** (here +0.02 to +0.05 for the long/flat trend pairs; −0.14 for the prior long/short basket). That's a *portfolio-construction* property — a low-vol uncorrelated diversifier/crisis sleeve — **not a return engine**, and it only matters once a multi-strategy capital allocator exists to size an uncorrelated sleeve. Note: these **long/flat** variants sit in *cash* during USD-strength panics (so they're uncorrelated but lack the prior long/short basket's actively crisis-*positive* negative beta). If FX is ever revived, it's the long/short trend basket that carries the hedge value, not these long/flat singles.
- **Carry (proxy):** behaves like a risk-on factor that worked pre-2018 and reversed after — keep as an *indicator* at most, not a traded strategy.
