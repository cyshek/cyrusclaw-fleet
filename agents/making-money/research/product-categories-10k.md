# Track B — Product CATEGORIES with a real $10k/mo+ ceiling (audience-less, agent-run)

**For:** Cyrus / `making-money` agent
**Date:** 2026-06-08
**Author:** `track-b-categories` research subagent
**Mandate:** Find PRODUCT categories with a high-enough ceiling ($10k/mo floor; large MRR or Base44-shaped 7–8 fig exit upside) and the right *shape* for an audience-less, ~95% agent-run business with ~$0–50 cash and Cyrus as a thin legal/financial root-of-trust. Distribution is being solved by a companion track; my job is *what to build*. Every category is scored against the 6-gate rubric. My known weak spot is **economic optimism**, so the money + distribution claims are deliberately stress-tested.

> **One-sentence finding:** Of the eight categories, only **three** survive all six gates for *our exact constraints* — (1) **Marketplace-native apps moved up-market** (Shopify/Chrome where buyer-intent IS distribution), (2) **B2B micro-SaaS with a real data moat + legit pSEO** (the slow compounding ScrapingBee/Zapier shape), and (3) a **narrow slice of "picks-and-shovels for AI builders"** where the tool's own output carries a distribution loop. The headline horizontal "AI builds X" category (Base44 shape) has the biggest ceiling but **fails Gate 2 for us** — every winner had a founder audience — and the 2025–26 exit data shows the giant AI payouts are **acqui-hires (talent), which an audience-less, team-less agent business structurally cannot access.** Our only realistic exit is a Base44-style *defensive product buy* or a financial roll-up at 5–8x ARR, not a $2B talent grab.

**The three to actually pursue (and how they fit together as a barbell):**
- **#1 Marketplace app (up-market)** = the *fast, safe base* — built-in distribution solves Gate 2 outright, ships in weeks, 6–8x exit multiple proven. Ceiling ~$10–60k/mo.
- **#2 Data-moat pSEO B2B tool** = the *patient compounding swing* — only audience-less channel that scales to unicorn-tail, best exit optics, but 6–12mo SEO ramp.
- **#3 AI-builder picks-and-shovels** = the *hot-window swing* — fastest-growing demand in 2026, but crowded; only worth it where the tool's output exposes the brand to non-users (a real loop), so distribution compounds.

**Single most promising concrete idea** (detailed in §4): a **data-moat B2B micro-tool that doubles as a free pSEO surface** in a vertical with proprietary, transactional, frequently-changing data — because it's the only one of the three that (a) needs *no* audience by construction, (b) compounds into a defensible moat code can't clone, and (c) has both an MRR path AND a clean strategic-acquirer story. The marketplace app (#1) is the better *first ship* for speed; the data-moat tool is the better *magnitude/exit* bet.

### CATEGORY 1 — Vertical AI SaaS for one industry

**What:** AI does a real, repeated job for ONE niche (law firms, dental/medical, real estate, construction, e-comm ops, recruiting, accounting). High ticket → forgiving math: at $299/mo, ~34 customers = $10k MRR.

**Real comparables ≥$10k/mo (Gate 1):**
- **Harvey (legal AI):** ~$8–11B valuation, $300M Series D, Sequoia/a16z. Proves the *ceiling* is enormous — but it's VC + enterprise-sales + lawyer-founded. The top of the market is **not our shape**. (prior brief, cited)
- **AI receptionist / voice-agent for local trades — the bootstrapped, real-revenue layer:** "I built an AI receptionist for my brother's **mechanic shop**" hit **320 pts on HN** (May 2026); **Sandra AI (YC F24)** = AI receptionist for car dealers; **DentistAI**, plumber (supacalls), vet (LiveTok) clones are multiplying. ([HN](https://hn.algolia.com/api/v1/search?query=AI%20receptionist&tags=story)) These are real businesses billing $100–500/mo per location — vertical AI doing a concrete job.
- **FinChat / Braden Dennis:** mid-7-figure ARR vertical AI for investing (prior brief) — but rode an *existing finance audience*.
- **Sell-price evidence:** microns.io B2B vertical micro-SaaS listings show $10k–67k ARR businesses asking 2–15x; acquire.com: B2B SaaS 5–15x ARR. (prior pass, cited)

**Gate-by-gate:**
| Gate | Score | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Ceiling | **4** | Harvey proves the top; receptionist/vertical-tool layer proves $10–50k/mo is real and repeatable. TAM huge (every profession). |
| 2 Distribution (no aud) | **2–3** | THE problem. Niche SEO + marketplace listings work, but most $300/mo B2B closes on a *demo call / trust* → pulls a human in. Self-serve low-touch slice can hit 3; high-touch slice is a 2. **Borderline kill.** |
| 3 Agent-runnable | **2–3** | Build = agent. But per-customer setup, voice-agent config, accuracy liability, onboarding hand-holding, and demo calls = real human layer. The receptionist shape especially needs per-account configuration + a human-credible sales touch. **Borderline.** |
| 4 Moat | **3** | Vertical data + workflow integration + switching cost once embedded. Cloneable early, sticky late. |
| 5 Acquirer fit | **5** | **Best in the whole list.** Vertical incumbents (clio/legal, dental PMS like Dentrix, ServiceTitan/trades, AppFolio/real-estate) defensively buy AI for their vertical. Salesforce did 12+ deals / $10B in 2025 to own the agent layer. ([agentmarketcap](https://agentmarketcap.ai/blog/2026/04/09/ai-agent-ma-acceleration-q2-2026-google-windsurf-aws-adept)) |
| 6 Legit | **4** | Clean SaaS; watch regulated-vertical liability (medical/legal accuracy). |

**Saturated-or-not:** The *generic* "AI receptionist / AI agent for [trade]" slice is **getting crowded fast** (every YC batch + indie clones). The *less obvious* verticals (construction submittals, dental insurance pre-auth, property-management compliance, e-comm catalog ops) are **less built**. **Verdict: SEND BACK** — survives only if you pick a slice that is *self-serve + low-touch + marketplace-distributed* (so Gates 2 & 3 clear 3+). The high-ticket, demo-closed version fails our autonomy constraint.

**Distribution lane it leans on:** marketplace intent (vertical app stores / Shopify for e-comm-ops) + niche pSEO. NOT paid-ads-to-cold-B2B (CAC too high without a sales close).

**Gate-by-gate:**
| Gate | Score | Justification |
|---|---|---|

| 2 Distribution (no aud) | **1** | **FATAL for us.** Base44 spread via Maor building-in-public on LinkedIn/X + prior rep (Explorium) + Israeli network + AWS demo invite. Every horizontal-builder winner had a founder audience or PR engine. Cold, audience-less, this is a 1. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/06/18/6-month-old-solo-owned-vibe-coder-base44-sells-to-wix-for-80m-cash/); memory 2026-06-07) |

Don't fight the horizontal giants.

### CATEGORY 3 — B2B micro-SaaS / data-moat tool with legit pSEO

**What:** A narrow B2B tool whose value is backed by **unique, structured data** rendered as thousands of SEO pages + a free tool as top-of-funnel → paid plan/API. The Zapier/Wise/ScrapingBee shape. The "compounding background play."

**Real comparables (Gate 1):**
- **ScrapingBee:** ranks **#1 for "web scraping API"** — that ranking IS their distribution forever; bootstrapped past $1M ARR. ([ideaindex 50-bootstrapped](https://www.ideaindex.so/blog/50-bootstrapped-startups-that-hit-1m-arr))
- **Zapier:** ~16.2M organic visits via 70,000+ programmatic "[App]+[App] integration" pages; **Wise:** 8.5M currency-converter pages. Both backed by a real catalog/data — the textbook pSEO engines, unicorn-scale. (prior brief, cited)
- **Nomad List (levelsio):** solo data-moat directory, historically ~$30k+/mo; **OpenAlternative:** 100k uniques in week 1, #1 HN, ranks via a real GitHub-metrics algorithm. (prior `high-ceiling-ideas.md`, cited)
- **Plausible / Baremetrics:** wedge-on-privacy / metrics-as-a-public-benchmark = compounding free distribution. ([ideaindex](https://www.ideaindex.so/blog/50-bootstrapped-startups-that-hit-1m-arr))

**Gate-by-gate:**
| Gate | Score | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Ceiling | **4** | Solo data-moat sites clear $10–40k/mo; unicorn tail (Zapier/Wise) when data is proprietary + transactional. |
| 2 Distribution (no aud) | **4** | **The whole point — distribution IS the product.** Google + LLM citations index thousands of unique-data pages. No audience needed; one-time HN/PH spike then compounding. The rare channel that needs no founder face. |
| 3 Agent-runnable | **4** | Scrape/aggregate → generate pages → schema → internal-link → refresh = a cron loop agents run forever. Build the API too. Human only picks the dataset + handles a scraping-ToS/partnership call. |
| 4 Moat | **4** | The *maintained dataset* is the moat — competitors copy the front-end, not the refreshed data + accumulated rankings + backlinks. Compounds. |
| 5 Acquirer fit | **3–4** | A strategic in the vertical buys the data asset + traffic (e.g., an incumbent who wants the SEO real estate or the dataset). Lower headline than vertical-AI but real (data + traffic + recurring). |
| 6 Legit | **3–4** | Clean money (subs + API + affiliate). Risk = scraping ToS on some sources (pick scrapable/licensable data); pick **transactional/fresh** data to survive AI Overviews. |

**Saturated-or-not:** The *lazy* version (AI writes 5,000 thin pages) is **dead** — Google's Mar-2026 core update hit scaled thin content with ~87% traffic loss, domain-level "weakest link" penalties. (prior brief, cited) The *data-moat* version (unique data per page, transactional/fresh) is **under-built** because it's harder — most people take the lazy path and get penalized. **This is the genuine "moat survives Google" play.**

**Verdict: ADVANCE.** Best combination of audience-less reach + defensibility + exit optics. Pays for it with a slow 6–12mo SEO ramp and a hard requirement that the *data* be transactional/fresh/filterable (not facts an LLM regurgitates).

**Distribution lane:** **pSEO (data-moat variant)** + one-time launch spike. The compounding engine.

**Real comparables (Gate 1):**

- **AppSumo:** lifetime-deal kickstart → cash + reviews + users fast (bad as long-term recurring, good for cold-start social proof).

**Gate-by-gate:**
| Gate | Score | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Ceiling | **3–4** | Up-market apps clear $10–60k/mo; $1M+ ARR apps documented. Capped below "unicorn" but solidly above $10k floor. |
| 2 Distribution (no aud) | **5** | **Best in the list.** Built-in buyer intent + store search + reviews replace the audience entirely. Merchants *search* for solutions. |

| 4 Moat | **3** | App-store ranking + reviews + merchant switching cost + integration depth. Platform-dependent (the risk). |
| 5 Acquirer fit | **3** | Roll-ups + PE + strategics buy at 6–8x TTM profit; not Base44-tier but a *clean, evidenced exit market with active buyers right now.* |
| 6 Legit | **4** | Clean; main risk = platform dependency (rules change, platform competes, app rejected). |

**Saturated-or-not:** The **utility floor is saturated** (10 alt-text/SEO/popup apps per niche — our own `app-wedge-candidates.md` mapped this). **Up-market + complex-workflow apps are less saturated** (fewer builders tackle harder integrations for high-GMV merchants). Pick the harder, higher-ticket workflow.

**Verdict: ADVANCE — best risk-adjusted first ship.** Distribution is *solved by construction*, exit market is *evidenced and active*, ceiling clears the bar. Ceiling is the only knock (sub-unicorn). Treat as the safe base of the barbell.

**Distribution lane:** **marketplace intent** (the cleanest fit for "no audience").

## 3. THE 2025–2026 AI-ACQUISITION LANDSCAPE (grounding the exit case)

The single most important honest correction to the exit thesis: **the giant 2025–26 AI payouts are overwhelmingly ACQUI-HIRES (paying for a star team + a license), which an audience-less, team-less agent business structurally cannot access.** Our exit can only be a **Base44-style defensive *product/user* buy** or a **financial roll-up at 5–8x ARR** — not a $2B talent grab.

**The numbers (well-evidenced):**
- **5,700 AI/ML acquisitions 2020→2025; only 21% disclosed value.** The other ~4,500 = small teams absorbed quietly. "Cheaper to buy a team than to compete for hires or build." 75th-pct deal **tripled $82M→$248M.** **Accenture (21) and Apple (17) lead by count**, not Google/MSFT. ([tomtunguz](https://tomtunguz.com/ai-acqui-hire-wave/), PitchBook data)
- **2025: AI M&A +155% H1; ≥33 major deals, >$157B disclosed; average revenue multiple ~25.8x** (≈3x the SaaS norm). **Infra 12–15x EBITDA; applied-AI software ~10x revenue (+47% YoY).** ([agentmarketcap](https://agentmarketcap.ai/blog/2026/04/09/ai-agent-ma-acceleration-q2-2026-google-windsurf-aws-adept))
- **AI = >50% of all global VC funding in 2025;** focus shifted from building LLMs to **integrating AI into workflows**; "investors prioritize startups with enterprise-adoption traction." ([Morgan Lewis](https://www.morganlewis.com/pubs/2025/09/ai-deals-in-2025-key-trends-in-ma-private-equity-and-venture-capital))

**The deal *structures* (and why they don't help us):**
- **Reverse acqui-hire / license deal:** Google–Windsurf = **$2.4B to hire founders + non-exclusive license, company left intact** to dodge antitrust. Amazon–Adept, MSFT–Inflection ($650M), MSFT–Cove, Google–Character.AI ($2.5B founders+license) all same pattern. **DOJ antitrust head calls acqui-hires a "red flag."** ([agentmarketcap](https://agentmarketcap.ai/blog/2026/04/09/ai-agent-ma-acceleration-q2-2026-google-windsurf-aws-adept); [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/acquihires-often-used-by-big-tech-are-red-flag-doj-antitrust-head-says-2026-03-18/)) → **These pay for PEOPLE. We have no star founders to sell. Irrelevant to our exit.**
- **Independent consolidation:** Cognition bought Windsurf's *remainder* (IP+product+brand+team) in a weekend, doubling ARR. → roll-up logic; reachable if we have real ARR + product.
- **Strategic platform spree:** Salesforce 12+ deals / $10B in 2025 to own Agentforce; Workday→Sana $1.1B; Wix→Base44 $80M. → **THIS is our template: a platform defensively buys a product that threatens its core or fills a hole.**
- **Talent-acqui-hire of tiny teams:** Handshake→Cleanlab (9 employees, talent); typical seed-team acqui-hire **$5–15M.** ([techcrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/01/28/ai-data-labeler-handshake-buys-cleanlab-an-acquisition-target-of-multiple-others/); [allaboutai](https://www.allaboutai.org/articles/analysis/AI-Funding-97-Billion-2025-Analysis/))

**What this means for OUR exit case (brutally):**
1. **The realistic exit is a *product/user-base* buy, not a talent buy.** Base44 ($80M, ~$3.5M ARR, 250k users) is the north-star *because the buyer wanted the product + users + category position*, not a team to hire. To be buyable we need **real users + real ARR + a defensible position a strategic fears**, OR a clean roll-up-able recurring asset.
2. **Two ranges to plan for:** (a) **Roll-up / financial exit: 5–15x ARR** (microns/acquire/Shopify-app 6–8x TTM profit) — *reliably reachable* with a profitable agent-run SaaS. (b) **Strategic defensive buy: Base44-tier** — *rare, needs hot category + threat-to-incumbent*, the lottery tail.
3. **Multiples are inflated by the AI gold rush and will likely settle.** Don't underwrite the 25x; underwrite 5–8x and treat anything above as upside.
4. **The named acquirer must be identifiable up front** (Gate 5). Categories with the clearest named buyers: **vertical AI** (vertical incumbents), **horizontal builders** (Wix/Webflow/Shopify), **AI-infra picks-and-shovels** (Semrush/Ahrefs/infra players), **data-moat tools** (whoever wants the dataset+traffic).

## 4. CONCRETE CANDIDATE IDEAS (survive the gates) — with honest EV

Five specific products that pass the gates, each scored on the shape that matters, with an honest EV range (base / good / tail $ + rough odds) and where I'm probably too optimistic. These are *category-validated seeds*; the companion distribution track + a build spike would de-risk before committing.

### Idea A — Data-moat pSEO tool for a transactional vertical *(TOP PICK for magnitude+exit)*
**Shape:** Pick a vertical with proprietary/aggregatable, **fresh + transactional** data (resists AI Overviews): e.g., live B2B supplier/wholesale pricing & lead-times, regulated-filing/permit datasets, marketplace-listing arbitrage data, ad-library/creative intelligence, or grant/RFP feeds. Render as thousands of unique-data pages (free top-of-funnel) → paid alerts/filters/API.
- **Why it wins:** the *only* idea that needs **zero audience by construction** (Google + LLM citations), compounds a moat code can't clone (the maintained dataset + rankings), and has BOTH an MRR path and a strategic-data-buyer story.
- **Distribution lane:** data-moat pSEO + one-time HN/PH spike.
- **EV:** base $0–2k/mo (40% — SEO never compounds / wrong dataset), good $10–40k/mo (35%), tail $100k/mo+ → strategic data buy (5–10%). Dead-on-arrival risk ~20%.
- **Too optimistic about:** SEO ramp speed (realistically 6–12mo, maybe never), and dataset-acquisition difficulty (the good datasets are ToS-protected or licensed — the "scrapable AND valuable AND legal" intersection is narrow). **This is the gating bet — get the dataset right or it's a zombie.**

### Idea B — Up-market Shopify app for a complex high-GMV workflow *(TOP PICK for speed+safety)*
**Shape:** NOT another alt-text/popup utility. Target high-GMV merchants ($49–299/mo) with a harder workflow few builders tackle: e.g., AI-driven catalog/feed ops, returns/exchange automation with margin logic, B2B-wholesale pricing engines, inventory/demand intelligence, post-purchase upsell with real attribution.
- **Why it wins:** Gate 2 solved by construction (merchants *search* the store); 6–8x TTM-profit exit market is *active now*; ships in weeks.
- **Distribution lane:** marketplace intent.
- **EV:** base $1–5k/mo (40%), good $10–30k/mo (40%), tail $50–60k/mo + 6–8x roll-up exit (15%). DOA ~5% (something always sells in-store).
- **Too optimistic about:** how fast you rank in a crowded store section (reviews take months), and platform dependency (Shopify can ship the feature or change rules). Lower ceiling than A — sub-unicorn.

### Idea C — Vertical AI tool, self-serve/low-touch slice, in an *under-built* vertical
**Shape:** Pick a vertical with money but LESS crowding than receptionist/legal: e.g., construction submittal/RFI automation, dental insurance pre-auth, property-management compliance docs, e-comm catalog enrichment. **Self-serve + low-touch** (price $49–149/mo, no demo) so it stays agent-runnable.
- **Why it wins:** best *named-acquirer* story (vertical incumbents) + real $10–50k/mo comparables.
- **Distribution lane:** niche pSEO + vertical marketplace.
- **EV:** base $0–3k/mo (45% — niche too niche / needs a sales touch after all), good $10–25k/mo (35%), tail strategic vertical buy (10%). DOA ~10%.
- **Too optimistic about:** whether the vertical will actually *self-serve buy* without a human (most B2B at $100+/mo wants a call) — if it won't, this collapses into Cat. 6 (KILL). Pick the slice carefully or not at all.

### Idea D — Data-backed GEO / AI-visibility tool with a proprietary citation dataset
**Shape:** Ride the hot GEO wave, but DON'T ship a me-too prompt-runner. Build a **proprietary dataset of what LLMs actually cite** across a vertical, sold as the moat (monitoring + actionable fixes). Output = shareable "AI visibility report" = a distribution loop.
- **Why it wins:** fastest-growing demand; clear acquirers (Semrush/Ahrefs already buying in); the dataset is the moat the crowd lacks.
- **Distribution lane:** product-led report loop + dev/marketer search.
- **EV:** base $0–5k/mo (50% — bloodbath, you're #14 of 13 tools), good $10–40k/mo (30%), tail strategic acqui (10%). DOA ~10%.
- **Too optimistic about:** how crowded this already is (Profound/Peec/Evertune/AthenaHQ/Semrush/Ahrefs are *funded* and *incumbent*) and whether a no-audience newcomer can get heard over them. **Highest clone/competition risk of the five.**

### Idea E — Vertical "AI builds X" with an embedded share-loop (the safe re-entry to Cat. 2)
**Shape:** Not a horizontal builder. "AI builds your [booking page / digital menu / property listing / portfolio] for [specific niche]" where the *output is shared with the user's own customers* carrying a "made with X" badge → the Calendly/Typeform/Framer loop.
- **Why it wins:** the only way to touch the $80M-exit category without a founder audience — the loop substitutes for one.
- **Distribution lane:** product-led "made-with-X" badge loop + marketplace.
- **EV:** base $0–3k/mo (50% — loop doesn't ignite cold), good $8–25k/mo (35%), tail viral → defensive buy (8%). DOA ~7%.
- **Too optimistic about:** loop ignition from a cold start (loops *amplify* existing distribution; they rarely *ignite* it — Dropbox's referral was bolted onto an already-distributed product). Needs a seed-spike to catch.

**Ranking of the five:** **A (magnitude+exit) and B (speed+safety) are the barbell** — pursue both. C is a strong third if a self-serve vertical slice checks out. D and E are hot-window swings with higher clone/ignition risk — fire only if a build spike shows an edge.

## 5. SATURATED TRAPS TO AVOID + WHERE I MIGHT BE WRONG

### Saturated traps (do NOT build these)
1. **Horizontal AI app/site builders** — bloodbath; every winner had an audience; you'd be the 50th Lovable clone.
2. **Me-too GEO/AI-visibility prompt-runners** — 13+ tools, funded + incumbents (Semrush/Ahrefs). Only a *data-moat* angle survives.
3. **AI SDR / cold-outreach automation** — deliverability treadmill; mailbox providers actively penalize; not a moat.
4. **Thin pSEO / scaled AI content** — Google Mar-2026 update = ~87% traffic loss, domain-level "weakest link" penalties. Dead without unique per-page data.
5. **Generic AI-receptionist-for-[trade]** — getting crowded fast (every YC batch + indie clones); the easy slices are gone.
6. **AI wrappers with no data/loop moat** — die the moment the model adds the feature ("100k users, then the email that ended it" — HN 44796306).
7. **Info-products as a primary play** — audience-dependent harvest, not a cold-start engine.
8. **Utility-floor marketplace apps ($9/mo single-purpose)** — caps below $10k.

### Where I'm probably too optimistic (my known weak spot)
- **pSEO ramp + dataset access (Idea A):** I'm likely too rosy on both speed (6–12mo, often never) and on finding data that is *simultaneously* valuable, scrapable, AND legal. The honest base case for A is a zombie; the tail is real but low-odds. Mitigate by validating the dataset BEFORE building.
- **"Agent-runnable" for B2B sales:** I keep scoring Gate 3 generously, but **B2B at $100+/mo overwhelmingly wants a human touch to close.** Anywhere I say "self-serve, low-touch," reality may force a sales call — which breaks our autonomy constraint and slides the idea toward the KILL'd agency category. Be ruthless: if it needs a demo, it's not our shape.
- **Exit optimism:** the 25x multiples are gold-rush-inflated and will settle; the $80M Base44 outcome is a survivorship outlier, not an expected case. **Underwrite 5–8x ARR roll-up as the realistic exit; treat strategic-premium as a lottery tail.** And remember the acqui-hire billions are *talent* buys we cannot access — our exit MUST be product/user-driven.
- **Distribution "solved by the other track":** I assumed it away per the brief, but Gate 2 is where most ideas actually die. Even marketplace intent needs reviews/ranking (months); even pSEO needs the data moat. No category here is *truly* zero-distribution-effort — the best ones just don't need a *founder audience*.
- **Survivorship bias everywhere:** every comparable (Base44, ScrapingBee, Nomad List, the $1.2M Shopify app) is a *winner*; the median outcome of any of these categories is $0. Cyrus has accepted that — good, because the base rate is harsh (≥50% of indie products never clear $1k/mo).

### The honest bottom line

For an audience-less, agent-run business with ~$0 cash, the physics are unchanged from prior research: **building is free, distribution is the whole game, and the only distribution that needs no founder audience is (a) marketplace buyer-intent, (b) a data-moat pSEO compounding engine, or (c) a product-led output loop.** The categories that survive are exactly the ones that ride one of those three:

- **Marketplace app, up-market (Idea B)** → fast, safe base, sub-unicorn ceiling, *active* 6–8x exit market.
- **Data-moat pSEO tool (Idea A)** → patient, defensible, best magnitude+exit optics, but gated entirely on getting the *dataset* right.
- **AI-builder picks-and-shovels with a moat/loop (Ideas D/E)** → hottest demand, highest clone risk, fire only with an edge.

The exit dream is real but must be underwritten honestly: **5–8x ARR roll-up is the reachable exit; the Base44 $80M defensive-buy is the lottery tail; the $2B acqui-hires are talent buys we cannot access.** Pursue Ideas A + B as a barbell; let the companion distribution track confirm Gate 2 before any build commitment.
