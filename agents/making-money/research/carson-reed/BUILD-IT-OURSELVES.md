## 0. THE MODEL IN ONE PICTURE

We are not inventing anything. We are assembling a **local lead-generation agency** out of off-the-shelf parts, and selling a small local business one outcome: **more booked appointments.** The "AI" is real and load-bearing — it's what lets a tiny team (or an agent + one human) deliver something that used to need a call center and a creative shop.

```
            ┌─────────────────────── THE PRODUCT WE SELL A CLIENT ───────────────────────┐
            │                                                                            │
   Meta ads (FB/IG)  ──►  Landing page / lead form  ──►  Lead hits the CRM (GoHighLevel) │
   (AI-made creative)        (AI-written copy)                     │                     │
            │                                                      ▼                     │
            │                                         AI VOICE CALLER (Retell/Vapi)      │
            │                                    calls the lead in <60 sec, qualifies,   │
            │                                    books them on the client's calendar     │
            │                                                      │                     │
            │                                                      ▼                     │
            │                                          Booked appointment  ──►  CLIENT'S │
            │                                                                  human closes│
            └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

   HOW WE GET PAID: ~$300-500 (3-wk trial) → $1-2k/mo retainer → $3-5k upfront as we get case studies.
   Client pays their own ad spend ($20-100/day) straight to Meta — we never touch it.
```

**The two layers, kept honest:**
- **The agency** (above) is a real, deliverable service. This is what we build.
- **Carson's *own* money** comes mostly from a paid mastermind + a GoHighLevel affiliate link. We are **not** copying that — we're copying the *agency*, which is the part that delivers actual value to a real customer. (If we ever want a content/education layer later, that's a separate decision.)

## 1. WHY THIS IS A GOOD FIT FOR AN AI-AGENT-DRIVEN ATTEMPT

This model is unusually friendly to "let the AI do a lot of it," which is exactly the experiment Cyrus is running:

| Task in the business | Who does it | AI leverage |
|---|---|---|
| Ad **copywriting** | AI | High — LLM writes 20 hook/body/CTA variations in seconds |
| Ad **creative** (image/video) | AI | High — Arcads/Ideogram/Canva generate UGC-style creative for ~$10 |
| The **AI voice caller** itself | AI (it *is* the product) | Total — the caller is an autonomous AI agent |
| **Qualification + booking** | AI caller | Total — runs 24/7, no human |
| **Website chatbot** | AI | Total once built |
| **SOPs / proposals / reporting** | AI | High — generate the whole back-office doc set |
| **Pre-call prospect research + custom deck** | AI | High |
| **Nurture / retargeting sequences** | AI | High — drafts the whole drip |
| **Monitoring ad metrics, flagging cuts** | AI agent | Medium-High — agent can watch dashboards + recommend |
| **The live sales close (Zoom)** | **HUMAN** | Low — AI preps, human closes |
| **Meta account, billing, identity, bank** | **HUMAN (Cyrus)** | None — requires a real person/entity |
| **Final approval to spend money** | **HUMAN (Cyrus)** | None — by our own rules |

**Read:** roughly 70-80% of the *labor* is automatable; the irreducible human parts are (a) the closing call, (b) owning the money/identity, and (c) the go/no-go judgment calls. That's a genuinely interesting autonomy test.

**The two costs that are genuinely irreducible:** (1) **Meta ad spend** — Meta won't run ads free, and you need a **human-owned card** to pay it; (2) the **phone number** for live outbound calls (~$2-3/mo). Everything else can start at $0.

> **The honest $0-50-leash version** (for the autonomy experiment): we can **build a working demo of the whole machine** — a funnel + an AI caller that actually answers a real phone call and books on a real calendar — for **effectively $0** (Retell's $10 free credit + free browser testing + free Cal.com tier), WITHOUT buying any ad traffic. The thing that costs real money is *acquiring clients via paid ads*. So the first milestone that fits the leash is **"a live, demoable AI-agency stack we can show a prospect"** — and the AI-caller pillar confirms this is buildable mostly autonomously by the agent. Detailed in §4.

> **The margin reality that makes this interesting:** raw AI **cost-per-booked-appointment is ~$0.50-$5** (a 3.5-min qualify-and-book call on a budget LLM is ~$0.35). Carson teaches charging clients up to **$200/appointment** on the performance model. The margin on the *mechanism itself* is enormous; the real costs in the business are ad spend + the human closer + time, **not** the AI minutes.

## 4. THE EXECUTION PATH (what we'd actually do, in order)

Sequenced so the **$0 / agent-doable** work happens first, and money/human-gated steps are clearly marked **[NEEDS CYRUS]**.

### Phase 0 — Build a demoable stack for ~$0 (mostly agent-doable)
The goal: a working machine we can show, before spending on traffic.
1. Pick our **starter niche** (2-3 candidates recommended in pillar 1). *(agent can research + recommend; Cyrus picks)*
2. Spin up **GoHighLevel on the free trial**; build a **lead-gen funnel + lead form + a "Lead→Booked" pipeline**. *(agent-doable once we have a login)* **[NEEDS CYRUS: create the account]**
3. Build the **AI voice caller** (Retell, free $10 credit) for that niche: a real qualification+booking script, connected to a real Cal.com calendar, tested live to a phone we control. **This is the centerpiece demo and the single best "AI actually did it" artifact** in the whole research effort — pillar 2 confirms it's buildable *mostly autonomously by the agent* for ~$0. *(largely agent-doable)*
4. Generate, with AI, the **ad creative + copy**, the **landing-page copy**, a **sample sales deck**, and the **SOP set**. *(fully agent-doable)*
5. Result: **a live demo** — "here's an AI front desk that calls a lead in 30 seconds and books them" — that costs ~$0 to have built.

### Phase 1 — Get the first client [NEEDS CYRUS in the loop]
6. Decide the client-acq channel (his ranking: our own paid ads #1; organic #2; AI-driven cold outreach as the $0 option). *(pillar 3 details all three)*
7. If paid: **[NEEDS CYRUS]** approve the ~$1.5-2k gambling budget + own the Meta account → launch our own ads to our funnel → AI caller books *us* discovery calls.
   If $0: AI sends a cold-email/DM cadence to local businesses in the niche (pillar 3 has the exact AI-written cadence).
8. **[NEEDS CYRUS]** Run the live sales call (the one part AI can't do) — AI preps the prospect research + custom deck; Cyrus (or a hired closer at 10%) closes. Use the closing playbook from his two real calls (pillar 3).
9. **[NEEDS CYRUS]** Take payment (Fanbasis/Stripe). Price low on purpose for the first 3-5 (case studies): ~$300-500 3-wk trial → $1-2k/mo.

### Phase 2 — Deliver & make AI run the back office
10. Onboard the client (AI-generated onboarding SOP + intake). Build *their* ads + *their* AI caller (we've done it once already in Phase 0).
11. AI handles **reporting, nurture sequences, creative iteration, qualification**. Human handles the relationship + any escalations.
12. Land-and-expand: start with leads-only or a trial, upsell the AI caller / retainer once results show.

### Phase 3 — Scale (only if Phases 1-2 prove out)
13. Duplicate winning ad campaigns; raise prices with case studies in hand; document everything as SOPs so cheap/AI labor can plug in. (His hiring ladder in `DOSSIER.md` §6 — not relevant until there's real MRR.)

## 6. HONEST REALITY CHECK (kept, but pointed at *us*, not at Carson)

Not hype-policing — just the things that determine whether *our* attempt works, so we go in clear-eyed:
- **Sales is the bottleneck, and it's human.** The whole machine produces *booked calls*; someone still has to close them. That's Cyrus or a paid closer. If neither happens, leads don't become revenue. This is the real make-or-break, same as our lane-1 finding.
- **It's a real service with a real deliverable** — which means a real client expecting results. Underpricing the first few for case studies (his advice) de-risks this.
- **"AI does a lot of it" is true for the *labor*, not the *accountability*.** The agent can build and run most of it; Cyrus owns the money, the account, and the client relationship.
- **Timeline is months, not days** — even Carson says the agency "barely makes money in the first 90 days." The $0 demo is fast; revenue is not.
- **Cheapest honest first win** = a working demoable stack (Phase 0) for ~$0, which doubles as the proof asset for the first sales call. That's the milestone that fits a $0-50 leash.
