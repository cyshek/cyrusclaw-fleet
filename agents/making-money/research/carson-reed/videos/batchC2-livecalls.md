### 1.3 Price mechanics (V1)
- **Headline:** **$15,000 for the full 90 days** (buildout/test/optimize), then **$2,000/mo retainer** thereafter (includes all new ads/creatives).
- **Ad spend is SEPARATE:** **$3,000/mo** (Jacob notes it's *"still less than... this other company"*).
- **Anchoring to ROI:** Built entirely from the prospect's own numbers — clinic doing $12k/mo on $16k overhead (losing money); per-patient profit ~$3,500 on a $5,300 ED treatment; 4–5 patients/week = *"15 to 18,000"* off five to six patients = more than a current month. Goal stated as $60–80k/mo → $2M clinic.
- **Finance-instead-of-discount:** ~**$1,300/mo** financed (because prospect lacks credit/cash up front), *"pay the whole thing off in March."* **No price cut.**

### 1.4 Why V1 was "DIFFICULT" + how Jacob handled it (most instructive)
**What made it hard:** The prospect was **genuinely cash-strapped and emotional about it** — *"I'm making my mortgage payment... I've got now this much money left."* This isn't a stalling objection; it's a real liquidity problem. He had also been burned ("business health AI") and was mid-divorce-from-incumbent ("Pilot Practice"). Plenty of buying intent, zero immediate cash.

**How Jacob handled it (the playbook):**
1. **Validated the constraint** rather than fighting it: *"I respect that move the ones and zeros."*
2. **Isolated desire from money** ("if money were out of the equation, would you do this?") → got a hard YES.
3. **Separated investment from commitment** — made the buyer own the decision emotionally before solving the cash.
4. **Solved cash WITHOUT discounting** — switched to financing (~$1,300/mo) and reframed ad spend as already cheaper than incumbent.
5. **Spoke the niche lingo** (stem cells, 361 compliance) to cement authority — which the prospect explicitly cited as the reason he trusted them.

**Precise outcome:** It got a **DEPOSIT**, not a full close. Carson concedes you could *"call this call a no close."* The "difficult" call's instructive value is the **objection-handling sequence**, not the result.

## CROSS-CALL COMPARISON — what's copyable

### Identical machinery (the repeatable system across BOTH calls)
1. **Short rapport → fast discovery.** Both open in seconds and immediately get the prospect to **indict their current/prior agency** (V1: "business health AI" + "Pilot Practice"; V2: the influencer deal that tanked traffic).
2. **Build the prize from the prospect's OWN numbers** before pitching (V1: $12k/$16k = losing money; V2: $75–80k/mo, wants $1M/mo).
3. **Pen-and-paper + screen-share ritual:** "write down 1) lead generation, 2) appointment setting, 3) sales." Forces engagement + memory anchoring.
4. **PRICE DROPPED EARLY, before any feature** — same stated psychology both times: *"it clears their brain... they actually listen to justify that price."* (V1: "$15,000"; V2: "$10,000.")
5. **The 3-pillar pitch** (verbatim consistent): Pillar 1 lead-gen (40–50 / 40–80 ads/mo, 3–4 words swing conversion, human-scripted + AI visuals, compliance 351/361, dynamic/variant education video); Pillar 2 appointment-setting (speed-to-lead 60–120s / 2–5 min US callers, builds the "house on fire" gap, deposit/consultation-fee mechanic, after-hours AI content bot); Pillar 3 sales (option 1 placed closer @10% commission vs option 2 deposit route — recommends deposit route for early clinics).
6. **"If money were out of the equation, would you do this?"** — used in BOTH to isolate the real objection and get a YES on the system.
7. **Finance, never discount.** V1 ≈ $1,300/mo; V2 ≈ $833/mo. The headline price is never cut — it's restructured. Ad spend reframed as already-cheaper-than-incumbent (V1) or as the cost of doing marketing at all (V2).
8. **30-day money-back / guarantee** as risk-reversal; **2–3x / "3x results"** as the ROI north star.
