## META PIXEL / FUNNEL SETUP (specifics)
- Match ad copy ↔ landing-page copy exactly ("kills conversion rate" if mismatched — big beginner mistake).
- Install Meta Pixel code at top of GHL booking page; create data set in Business Suite → Data sources → Datasets and pixels → install code manually.
- Booking page optimizes for **PageView**; **Thank-you page** gets same pixel code + the **"Schedule"** standard event (from "Facebook Events Pixel" spec page) — because only people who booked reach the thank-you page. Turn on Advanced Matching. Assign pixel to ad account + self.
- "You HAVE to have the pixel to run ads… all the money you spend will go down the drain because Facebook isn't tracking anything."

## ADS MANAGER CAMPAIGN SETUP (step-by-step Carson gives)
- Objective: **Leads** campaign (auction), **Manual setup** (refuse Advantage+ "rabbit hole").
- Budget at **ad-set level (ABO)** for testing, NOT CBO — turns off budget-sharing/optimizations.
- Conversion location: website only; performance goal = maximize conversions; data set = your pixel; conversion event = **Schedule**.
- Targeting: **broad** — "I don't do many targeting." Inclusion = United States only. Dynamic creative OFF. Turn off all Facebook text "improvements."
- **CBO vs ABO teaching:** ABO with one ad per ad-set = force equal spend per ad for clean testing (e.g., 3 ad-sets × $10/day = $30/day, equal). Multiple ads under one ad-set = Facebook decides distribution.
- Captions/CTA = "Learn More."

### Metrics Carson tracks (custom column set)
Amount spent, results, **cost per result**, **cost per unique click** (lower = bigger winner / proxy for CTR), CPM (cost per 1,000 reach), frequency, daily budget, link clicks, cost per landing-page view. "Only really look at amount spent, results, cost per result, cost per unique click. Don't obsess."
- Optimization rule: **let ads spend** — don't cut at $10. "I've had ads spend $20, $30, $50 looking terrible then pick up like crazy." Cut after ~$100 spend with 0 calls.

### Pre-qualifying calendar form (GHL)
- Add friction once unqualified people show up: questions like "How many deals have you closed this year?" (option "0 → don't book" / can redirect-DQ), "Target GCI (gross commission income)," "How are you finding clients now?" Qualified bookings feed better data back to pixel.

## CLIENT ACQUISITION — THREE METHODS

### 1. Paid Ads (Carson's #1 recommendation)
- Offer > targeting. "Much less about targeting… make the creative super good + matching copy + matching funnel."
- Image vs video: image = cheaper cost/result, higher volume, **lower intent**; video = pricier (e.g., $20 image call vs $30–40 video call), lower volume, **higher intent**.
- Image-ad recipe (Canva 600×600, League Spartan): niche callout top → one-sentence offer middle → guarantee/pay-per-result. Make 3–4 variants (emoji vs none, black vs white bg) and test.

### 2. Organic Content (recommends EVERYONE do alongside ads/outreach)
- "It's a rocket/snowball" — slow then exponential. Carson: 3 yrs on YouTube, had ~2,000 subs a year ago.
- **YouTube = superior** platform. Titles must call out the industry. Funnel link at TOP of description ("Work with me / have me implement AI into your [niche] business").
- **"Quality of viewer, not amount of views."** Proof story (ASSERTED): interviewee **"Justice" (solar)** closed **~$15,000 in deals** from a YouTube channel getting ~100 views/video / ~100 subs.
- Use **VidIQ** outlier scores → recreate/improve 3x+ outlier videos in your niche. Credits strategy attribution: learned from mentor **Jordan Welch**.
- Make **Lucid Chart "give value" videos**; do a mid-video CTA (~half length of end CTA) + full end-of-video pitch every time.
- **Instagram = top-of-funnel** feeder to YouTube; 3–4 reels/week; "comment [keyword]" CTA → DM funnel link; double-combo if same IG account also runs your ads.
- Consistency critical: Carson has posted every month, never missed.

### 3. Outreach (deliberately downplayed)
- "Outreach isn't something I do today… I actually recommend most people get a job." Only do it if you've exhausted resources, can't afford ads, can't get a job, too young.
- How HE started: outreach got first **5–10 clients** up to **~$3K/month** (in 2023). Not scalable past ~$10K/mo without big cold-call/setter teams ("massive headache for very little return").
- **Cold calling:** done inside GoHighLevel (green phone icon → enable → dial pad; conversations auto-tracked).
- **Email:** Instantly.ai ($30/mo); buy "aged emails" online, warm up a few days, then send.
- **DMs:** IG/Facebook/LinkedIn; respect platform daily-send limits; can buy/make extra accounts.
- **Scripts:** provides **2 docs in the GHL "resources" tab/snapshot** — "Cold Email Outreach Script" + "Cold Calling Outreach Script" (DM script = shorten the email one via Manus/ChatGPT). Sourced from mastermind members "closing clients up to **$5–10K/month**," then templatized by niche. (Scripts themselves are blurred/not read aloud — NOT quoted in this slice.)
- **KPI discipline:** set minimum daily inputs (e.g., 100 emails / 1,000 emails / 100 DMs / 100 cold calls). Commit **3–6 months / 90 days minimum**.

## SALES (Module 12 intro)
- **Structure: Discovery → Demo → Close.**
- Carson's mistake early: "Hey how's it going? Great" → jumped straight to pitch = "completely wrong."
- **Discovery opener (QUOTED):** *"All right, John. Well, look, man, I've got about 45 minutes here. I'm really excited to speak with you… But before we go into anything of my end and what we do… I wanted to just quickly spend some time to ask you some questions to see if we could really help you or not in your real estate business… because the last thing I want to do is spend 45 minutes showing you everything I do and then we get to the end and realize it's not going to even be a fit. So… do you want to give me like the quick 1-8-0 of your business — how things are going, what's going well, what's going wrong…"*
- **Rule:** discovery time ≥ demo time (e.g., 20 min discovery if 20 min demo). Never 2 min discovery / 40 min demo.
- Use Manus/ChatGPT to generate niche-specific discovery questions & pain points.
- Discovery = "knife to cut open their problems & hammer the pain"; Demo = "band-aid," tailor every solution to their stated problems; qualify out broke/$0 prospects.
- **Double tie-down (QUOTED):** *"Do you think this can help you, John?… 'Yeah, Carson, I do.' … Okay, cool, John. Why? Why do you say that?"* → makes prospect sell themselves before the close.
- **Close:** show a pricing deck. "A lot of the big guys in my mastermind use **Manus** to make their sales deck." Deck formats: Lucid Chart (with pricing at bottom), PowerPoint, Google Slides, Google Docs.
- Promises (later in module, not in this slice): live sales-call recordings + a sales-guy masterclass from the mastermind.
- **Appearance/trust:** invest in setup (mic, lighting, fake olive tree, ~$100–150). Carson's self-deprecating proof: started at 16, overweight, stuttering, glasses, long hair, "didn't look trustworthy."
