## The Business Model ("AI Agency")

**What it is:** A service-based agency that combines lead-gen ads + an AI phone caller (and optionally fixing the client's sales process). Positioned as the evolution of SMMA.

**The 3-step value chain Carson teaches:**
1. **Get leads** — run Facebook/Instagram (Meta) lead-gen ads for the client. (Carson is Meta-only; says he's not experienced in TikTok/Google/YouTube ads.)
2. **Get appointments** — an **AI caller** you build calls each lead ~3 min after opt-in, sounds human, qualifies + books onto client's calendar.
3. **Close the deal (optional add-on)** — rebuild the client's sales scripts/process using AI (ChatGPT/Manus). Most local businesses have terrible sales processes.

**Why it beats SMMA:** Regular SMMA only does step 1 (~33% of the job), leaving the client to chase leads (which they never do) → useless service → high churn. AI agency "absorbs more of the client's problems" (cites Amazon/Bezos philosophy) → lower churn, charge more, easier to sell.

**Churn data Carson cites (ASSERTED, from his own agencies):**
- SMMA model: ~15–20% churn
- AI agency model: ~8–10% churn (goal: sub-5%)

**ICP / Niche (who you sell to):** Any service-based local business that needs leads → appointments → closed deals. Named examples: roofers, plumbers, HVAC, painters, home services, real estate agents, medical clinics/med spas, dental practices, online businesses. (Manus research in video ranked: #1 med spas, then home service HVAC/plumbing/roofing, then dental.)

**AI-caller advantages over human phone setters (his sales angle):** cheaper, no sick days/vacation/PTO, always-on, programmable, no mistakes. Cites stat: calling a lead within 5 min of opt-in = "500% more likely" to convert vs hours later.

## Pricing & Offer Construction

**Three pricing models taught (Module 6 V2):**

| Model | What it is | Example numbers | Carson's rank |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| **PIF (Pay In Full)** | Upfront lump sum | $5,000 upfront | **#1 best** (gives capital to reinvest in client acquisition) |
| **Retainer** | Monthly recurring fee | $1,000–$1,500/mo | #2 |
| **Performance** | Pay-on-result | Pay-per-appointment (PPA) $100–$200/appt; or per deal/customer ~$1,000/deal; or % of revenue (~10%) | #3 for beginners, BUT #1 at scale ($100k+/mo) |

**Carson's recommended "standard" beginner offer:**
- **3-month service (120 days), $5,000 upfront** = paid ads + AI caller build-out (+ optional sales-script help).
- **Always put a term** on the service (never open-ended/unlimited).
- **Client always pays ad spend SEPARATELY.** Recommended ad spend: **$20/day bare minimum** (small local biz like real estate agents) up to **hundreds/day** (doctors/medical clinics who can afford it). Note: $100/day = ~$3k/mo, too much for many local agents.

**Downsell / option-stacking:** If they won't pay $5k upfront, offer "$5k now (all-in) OR $2,500/mo" to incentivize the PIF.

**Post-term retainer / maintenance:** After the initial 3 months, add a maintenance retainer (~$1,500/mo) to continue. Bundle discount example: pay 3 months upfront = $4,000 instead of $4,500 (to pull cash forward).

**Price range claims:** AI-agency builds charge "as low as $3k up to $10k+"; says he knows people charging **$30k–$50k+**. ASSERTED proof point: an agency owner with personal-injury law firms — **ONE client paying $120,000/mo**, $4M/mo ad spend, 6-yr contract, NDA/exclusive (performance model). Also referenced "Peter" interview on his channel: **$200k/mo at 19yo** with medical clinics, 60–70% margins.

**Upfront target for students:** "Close clients that pay you $500 to $10,000 upfront" (explicitly NOT $200–$300/mo low retainers).

## Other notable claims/positioning
- Bashes competing models: day trading (2–3 yr to profit, 99% lose), AI automation (saves time not money, easily replaced, Fiverr undercut at $30), SMMA (works but "outdated"), freelance brand scaling ("SMMA in wrapping paper"), affiliate marketing (high barrier), drop-shipping/ecom (>99.5% fail, expensive ads).
- Realistic timeline framing: **90 days to first client** (took him 6 months); underpromise/overdeliver.
- Mindset homework: write a multi-horizon **vision doc** (90 days / 1 / 3 / 5 / 10 yrs) — goals, possessions, daily routine — to train subconscious "north star."
- 6 beginner traps: (1) website before clients, (2) client fulfillment before clients, (3) outsourcing before you can do the skill, (4) "LLC warriors" (be sole proprietor first), (5) obsessing over testimonials, (6) quitting too early.
