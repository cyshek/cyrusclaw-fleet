## BUSINESS MODEL (the core offer Carson teaches)

**"AI Agency"** — positioned explicitly as NOT an SMMA/marketing agency (he says being an "AI agency" makes you stand out in saturated/red-ocean markets).

The **AI agency offer = two big things:**
- **Digital ads** (specifically **Meta = Facebook + Instagram**) → for lead generation. "It all starts with leads."
- **AI caller** (the main emphasis) → handles **inbound calls** (acts as front desk) AND **outbound calls** (calls/follows up the new opt-in leads, books them on a calendar).
- **AI chatbot** → minor; framed as a possible **upsell** only.

Key framing:
- "Every business needs leads. Not every business wants the AI caller yet." → Leads can be sold alone first, AI caller added later as an upsell.
- AI caller can **fully replace** a front desk, or run **alongside** a human (after-hours / when human doesn't pick up).
- Sales pitch hook for the upsell: clients often **never call their leads** — Carson says he's had clients not call a single lead. Then in a follow-up meeting: *"Look, man, the leads were not contacted within 5 minutes. Let's install this AI caller."*

## PICKING YOUR NICHE (Module 8)

### Blue Ocean vs Red Ocean (two buckets)
| | Blue Ocean | Red Ocean |
|---|---|---|
| Competition | Low | High / "saturated" |
| Proof of concept | LESS (key point — "less proof of concept" in all caps) | MORE |
| Risk/Reward | High risk, high reward | Lower risk, still good reward |
| Examples | carpet cleaners, dog breeders (esp. high-ticket French bulldogs that sell for $10k+), pool building, plumbing | real estate agents, med spas, dentists, roofers |

- Carson's own agency works primarily with **medical clinics** (specifically **high-ticket treatment med spa clinics** — he's "very niched," not all med spas). Also works with **Inc. 5000 companies** today.

### Niche rules (write these down — emphasized)
1. **Stick with your niche for at least 3–6 months** (6 months is the real target; 3 is "premature"). Do NOT niche-hop monthly — he says this is the **#1 reason people quit / fail / waste thousands–tens of thousands of dollars and 1+ years**. Frames "shiny object syndrome" as the killer.
2. **Start with what you know** (your competitive edge): e.g., getting a real estate license → target realtors; dad was a roofer → target roofers; law degree → target law firms.
3. **Decide fast** — pick within 24 hours; use Manus / ChatGPT to brainstorm. "Acting is almost always better than inaction."
4. **TAM (Total Addressable Market): need ≥ 20,000 businesses** in the country you target (else ad costs / CPA / cost-per-call get too high and you cap out). TAM worry mainly applies to small blue-ocean niches.
5. **Pick only 1–2 countries**, ideally cash-rich: **US, Canada, UK** (so you can charge higher ticket).
6. **Check seasonality** (e.g., roofing prime season ~March/April–end of Sept; landscaping/solar/pool seasonal). Work around it or build during down months.
7. **Consider the client's sales cycle** (rarely discussed online, per Carson) — see below.

### Sales-cycle insight (called "agency sauce")
- **Real estate agents = one of the LONGEST sales cycles** (6–12+ months to close a home) → hard to show tangible $ results in 90 days. This determines your **term limits**.
- **Med spa / dentist / roofer = short cycle** (days from opt-in to paid service) → fast tangible monetary results.

- Stuck with **real estate agents for 6 months**; **$0 made and zero sales calls booked for the first 6 months** ("you can look at this on my channel").

## MARKETING ANGLE / OFFER POSITIONING (Module 8 continued)

Core concept: **sell the END RESULT (the "bridge" destination), not the mechanism/"bridge"** (the boring how). Analogy: don't sell "eat celery & chicken," sell "lose 10 pounds in 90 days."

### "One-sentence offer explanation" formula
> **"We will get you [X result] within [X days], or you do not pay."**

Three key parts: (1) the result, (2) the timeframe, (3) the safety net ("or you do not pay").

**Worked example (real estate agents):** *"We will get you **10 listing appointments within 90 days** or you do not pay."*

**What "or you do not pay" actually means** (Carson reveals the marketer's reality): it usually does **NOT mean a refund** — it means *"if we don't get you the results in [X] days, we'll work for free until we do."* That's the standard marketing-agency convention.

### "Only pay per [result]" angle
- Variants: "only pay per closed deal," "only pay per job" (roofers/job-based), "only pay per patient" (med spas/dentists), "only pay per appointment."
- This attracts the **brokest of the broke** AND some great clients → it's a **volume play** (lots of sales calls because the offer is stand-out).
- Mechanism on the call: pitch the pay-per-result offer (high volume / lower quality leads) but **downsell to a retainer/PIF** if they can't afford the high ad spend it requires.

### Volume vs quality
- "We will get you X or you don't pay" = **lower volume, higher quality** leads.
- "Only pay per result" = **higher volume, lower quality**.

## PRICING (Module 9) — the most detailed section

Carson's big differentiator vs other YouTubers: **don't give everyone a cookie-cutter "charge $5k."** Pricing depends on the person's situation. Four models, picked by a self-assessment.

### The qualification checklist (check the boxes)
1. Sales experience? (Y/N)
2. Experience **within the niche** (real industry experience, not "talked to a few realtors")?
3. Are you **confident**?
4. **Age/look above 20 years old**? (appearance matters in sales — "if you can't take care of yourself, how will you take care of me?" / being in shape helps)
5. Good English (for US markets)?
6. Talk mature?

**Rule of thumb:** check **3–4+ boxes → go straight to Retainer/PIF.** Check fewer → start with **Trial-with-upfront** (and fall back to **Pay-on-results** only if you fail many calls).

### The 4 pricing models

| Model | When to use | Structure / $ |
|---|---|---|
| **Trial w/ upfront fee** | Beginners, <3–4 boxes checked | 3–4 week service, **ads only**, **$300+** (he started at **$250**, worked up $300→$400→$500). Client always pays ad spend separately. Get a **testimonial** in exchange. |
| **Retainer** | 3–4+ boxes | **Ads + AI caller**, **$3k–$5k** to start (no case studies). 3-month initial term, then monthly maintenance (**$750–$1.5k/mo** depending on initial charge). |
| **PIF (Pay In Full)** | 3–4+ boxes | Same as retainer but all upfront. **$3k–$5k**; build up to **$10k**. Monthly fee after (~**$1k–$2k/mo**). |
| **Pay on results** | ONLY if (a) you failed ~10–15 shown calls on the trial offer, OR (b) you advertised pay-per-result and the client opts in | No upfront fee; client only pays (high) ad spend; you take per-appointment or per-deal cut. |

### Carson's own pricing ladder / journey (ASSERTED)
- Started: **$250 for 3 weeks** (ads only) → up to **$500** → then **3-month services at $1,500/month**.
- Signed many real estate agents at **$1.5k–$2k/month for 6 months**; some clients stayed **15+ months**.
- Realtor client commission/value: **$5,000–$20,000 per home sale** → one client pays for 1.5–2 years of his marketing.
- **Today his agency charges ~$20k–$30k upfront** (because of the high-end clientele / Inc. 5000 level).

### Free trials — why he's against $0 trials
- No financial buy-in → client doesn't care → "trash clients." Weight-loss-coach analogy. People in his **mastermind** who did $0 trials got lots of clients but bad ones.
- His version: charge **something** ($250+) for the trial = skin in the game.

### Ad spend is ALWAYS separate (he stresses this — got tons of questions on last year's course)
- Example: client pays **$5k upfront** + you tell them to spend **$30/day** on their own ads = +$900/mo ≈ **$2.7k over 3 months**, so total in their mind ≈ **$7.7k** (the $2.7k is NOT in the $5k).
- Ad spend varies wildly by niche: high-ticket med spa clinics spend **$200+/day**; starting real estate agents **$20–30/day**.

### PIF vs Retainer — the on-call comparison tactic (script-ish)
Present **two offers side by side** to funnel them to PIF (more cash upfront):
> *"PIF is, hey, we'll do everything for you — three months, **5K upfront** — ads + AI caller. The AI caller you keep even beyond working with us because we build the agent within your own account; it's your asset. The ads we manage every week/month... after the 3-month service we charge **$1,000/month**. If that doesn't sound interesting, we also do a split monthly payment option at **$2,000/month** — over 3 months that's about **$6k vs the $5k**, just to incentivize paying upfront to save you money. Same service, same monthly fee after. How would you like to move forward?"*

### Advanced: Pay-per-result vs PIF pricing math (sales-call downsell mechanic)
Set the pay-per-result ad spend **so high** that the PIF becomes the cheaper "downsell":
- **PIF:** $3k upfront + $30/day ads (~$1k/mo × 3) = **~$6k total / 3 months**.
- **Pay-per-result:** $0 upfront, but **$200/day** ad spend = $6k/mo × 3 = **$18,000** (3× more) **plus** your per-deal/per-appointment cut.
- Result: client picks the cheaper PIF as the lower barrier of entry; only does pay-per-result if they explicitly want it and can fund the big spend.

### Legality caveat (pay-per-result)
- **Pay per appointment** (e.g., $100–$200/appointment) = fine in most industries.
- **Pay per closed deal / % of commission** = legally sticky. In US **real estate** you **can't take a % of commission without a real estate license** → use a **finder's fee / flat fee** instead.

### Ad-data / spend lesson (ties to fulfillment skill-building)
- More daily spend = faster data. Same ad at **$10/day takes 10 days** to gather the same data as **$100/day in 1 day**. "The math will math."
- High-spend pay-per-result work **refines your ad skills fastest**; winning ads can be **duplicated across markets** (e.g., a Seattle winner re-run in Austin will likely work again).

### Key mindset (emphasized, face "made big")
> The **most valuable thing from your first few clients is the EXPERIENCE, not the money.** You need **5–10+ clients** of feedback/case studies before you truly know your fulfillment is good. "One client isn't enough. Three clients even isn't enough." People who obsess over fulfillment before having clients are wrong.
