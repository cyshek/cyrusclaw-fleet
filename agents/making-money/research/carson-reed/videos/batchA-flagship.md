## 2. The Business Model (service, ICP, price points)

### What the student sells
A **done-for-you local lead-generation + AI automation service**, bundled as three pillars:
1. **Meta ads** (Facebook + Instagram) to generate inbound leads.
2. **AI caller** — an AI voice agent that calls every new lead in seconds, qualifies, and books appointments (the front-desk replacement / after-hours catcher). **This is the headline differentiator.**
3. **AI chatbot** — website/SMS chatbot (GHL Conversation AI) to capture and nurture web traffic.

### ICP / niche
- **Local service businesses** that (a) need a constant flow of new customers and (b) lose money on missed/after-hours calls.
- Repeatedly named examples: **dentists/dental, med spas, healthcare/medical, insurance, home services, chiropractors** — appointment-based businesses where a booked call = real revenue.
- His rule: "**Every business needs leads; not every business wants the AI caller yet**" → you can sell the **leads (ads) alone first**, then upsell the AI caller later.
- A mastermind member he interviews runs a **med spa agency** (see §7).

| Typical upfront (setup) | **~$2,000–$5,000 upfront** (he uses **$5K upfront for a 3-month engagement** as the recurring example; mentions 2–4K as common for newer agencies) |
| Monthly retainer | **~$1,500–$3,000/mo** range implied; client also pays their **own ad spend** on top |

## 4. Offer Construction (packaging, pricing, guarantees, retainer vs one-time, upsells)

### Packaging
- Sell the **3-pillar bundle** (ads + AI caller + chatbot) — but **land-and-expand**: many clients buy **ads/leads first**, AI caller as the **upsell** once trust exists.
- Position the AI caller two ways depending on client comfort: **full front-desk replacement** OR **after-hours / missed-call coverage** (acknowledges clients stay attached to good human staff).

### Pricing structure
- **Hybrid: upfront setup fee + monthly retainer + client's ad spend.** Core recurring example = **$5K upfront for 3 months** of service.
- **Pricing models taught:**
  - **Retainer** (monthly recurring) — the goal for stable MRR.
  - **Performance-based** (pay per result/booked appt) — higher risk, use selectively.
  - **PIF (pay-in-full)** — collect the whole engagement upfront; preferred for cash flow.
- **First 3–5 clients:** charge **less / underprice on purpose** — the asset is the **case study and experience**, not the cash. Jab at YouTubers who obsess over fulfillment perfection before having any clients.

### Guarantees
- Carson leans on **performance framing and results/case studies** rather than a hard money-back guarantee; the "guarantee" is implicitly your demonstrated lead/booking results. (No blanket refund guarantee scripted.)

### Upsells / backend
- **Leads → AI caller → chatbot** as a natural upsell ladder.
- Raise prices on later clients ("more trust, bigger deals") once you have results.
- Add more ad spend / more campaigns over time.

## 5. Client Acquisition (channels, scripts, sales-call structure, objections)

### The 3-channel priority order
1. **Have a job / save capital first** — fund the agency (GHL, ads) before quitting anything.
2. **Paid ads (his preferred fast channel)** — run **your own** Meta ads to a landing page → lead form → booked call. Same machine you'll sell to clients.
3. **Organic content + outreach** — post content; cold outreach (incl. **cold email via Instantly**).

### Landing page / booking flow (for himself and clients)
- Ad → **landing page** → **opt-in / lead form** → **booked call** on GHL calendar → AI caller / setter follows up fast.
- **Retargeting:** create Pixel audiences of people who **viewed the landing page but didn't book**, exclude people who booked, then **hammer them with retargeting ads daily** ("hey, I've seen you before…" hooks). Only worth it at higher spend.

### Paid ads mechanics (Module 12 — very detailed)
- **Adset mix:** ~90% budget across **3 adsets** — **lookalike, stacked-interest, broad** (recommends **broad** for beginners). ~10% to **retargeting**.
- **Creatives:** every video ad = **Hook + Body + CTA**; film **multiple variations of each** (e.g., 5–10 hooks, 3–4 bodies, 3–5 CTAs) and combine for many variations.
- **Image vs video ads:** image ads = more leads, lower intent, easiest for beginners ($30–$50/day). Video ads = fewer but higher-intent leads; used at scale.
- **"Andromeda" Meta update:** algorithm now wants **lots of creatives**; he runs **~150 ads** in the agency at once. Launch ~30, find ~3 winners, **duplicate winners into a "winners campaign" with higher budget**, cut losers, repeat the cycle to scale.
- **Cut rule:** kill an ad once its cost-per-lead hits **~2.5–3× your target CPL** (e.g., target $20 CPL → cut at ~$60).
- **Don't over-tinker** at low spend — let winners spend; check small accounts ~2–3×/week.

### Sales call structure (Module 13) — **Discovery → Demo → Close**
- **Discovery:** dig into the prospect's current lead flow, missed calls, what a customer is worth, pain. Build the gap.
- **Demo:** show the **unique mechanism** — the AI caller booking leads instantly + ads filling the pipeline. The 3-pillar system as the solution.
- **Close:** present pricing (anchor high value vs the cost), handle objections, collect payment (Fanbasis).
- **1-call vs 2-call close:** can close on the first call; or run a **2-call** (discovery then demo/close) for bigger/colder deals.
- **Pricing presentation:** anchor against the **value of booked appointments / lost missed-call revenue**, then the price feels small.

### Objection handling (pre-call + on-call)
- Pre-call **nurture/retargeting** so prospects show up warm ("hammer sequences," up to ~20 nurture ads in 48h before the call).
- On-call: tie every objection back to **cost of inaction** (missed leads/after-hours calls) and the **case studies / demonstrated metrics**.

## 6. Fulfillment — How the AI Service Gets Built Per Client (Module 14)

### Onboarding
- Onboarding call to hype the client, gather access (ad account, GHL sub-account, calendar, brand info, business details for the AI caller script).

### Build the ads
- Spin up the client's Meta campaign using the **same structure he runs for himself** (adsets, creatives, lead form). He says fulfillment ads ≈ self-promo ads "except maybe adding a lead form."

### Build the AI caller (the crown jewel)
- Use **Retell AI** to create the voice agent + his **GHL connector app** so the caller can pull new leads and book on the GHL calendar.
- Configure the **call script/prompt** (greeting as the business's front desk, qualification questions), **call transfers** (e.g., hand off hot leads to a human), and **calendar booking**.
- The caller calls **new leads within seconds** of opt-in, qualifies, and books appointments — 24/7, including after-hours and missed calls.

### Build the chatbot
- **GHL Conversation AI** + a **website widget** to capture web visitors and answer/qualify, feeding the same pipeline.
- **Instantly** used where cold-email outreach is part of the play.

### Ongoing delivery / retention
- **Weekly reporting** to clients; check-in/onboarding calls handled by you (or later a CSM).
- Retention via results + backend upsells (more spend, AI caller add-on, more campaigns).
