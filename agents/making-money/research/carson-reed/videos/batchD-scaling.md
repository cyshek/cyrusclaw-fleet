## 1. SCALING via paid ads

> Brief expected this in `-n8iMwIxGa0`. That video is actually an offer-philosophy talk. The real paid-ads scaling mechanics live in the **mastermind call (`Xv5MGNvanIA`)** and the **sales-call teardown (`YwF7femtbYg`)**. Here's what's actually evidenced.

### Platforms & funnel (the AGENCY's funnel for clients)
- **Ad platform:** Meta only — Facebook + Instagram ads. No mention of Google/YouTube/TikTok ads as his channel. (One *prospect* mentions Google Ads; Carson's stack is Meta.)
- **Creative:** **AI-generated UGC video ads** + AI image ads. He name-drops **Arc Ads** (~$10/video, you feed it a 30–45s ChatGPT/Claude script, pick an AI actor). Claims AI actor ads "have been performing better than a lot of the human UGC I've hired."

- **The client funnel:** Ad (pain-based, e.g. "shoulder pain") → landing page / **Tally form or GHL calendar** → **AI caller (Retell AI) calls within 2–5 min** ("speed to lead") → **appointment-setting call builds pain** → **education video that *dynamically* swaps 30% of its content** to match the ad they came from → **sales call (Zoom or in-person), client closes**.

The two-call structure = phone discovery call (low commitment, high show rate) → Zoom close call ("they treat the Zoom with more respect").

### Budgets / ROAS actually stated (from the mastermind, real numbers)
These are **mastermind members' numbers**, which is the most concrete evidence in the whole batch:

(This is a notable, fairly honest admission.)

### Is the REAL business an agency or selling-the-dream?
**Both, but the dream is what's aimed at you.** Evidence the agency is real: named team (closer Jacob, "Ethan," "Wyatt" setting appointments), a genuine 30-min messy sales call with real objections, niche fluency (FDA 351/361 stem-cell ad regs, CareCredit/Cherry/Clarity Pay financing, "GCI" real-estate jargon), real tool accounts. - A domain/email
- ChatGPT/Claude — to write ad scripts + caller prompts

**First actions / milestones:** pick niche → build the productized system → run ads at low intent first, add friction as data comes in → optimize cost-per-call → close at $5k–$25k. Milestone markers he references: first client, "$50k months," scaling spend, hiring a closer, hiring media buyer + CSM + closer.

## 6. Realistic outcome read (SHOWN vs ASSERTED)

**What's genuinely SHOWN (and useful):**
- The **mechanics are real and coherent**: Meta ads → AI caller (Retell) → GHL booking → pain-based discovery → close. The tools exist at the stated prices. The sales-psychology tactics (state price early to clear the mental block; make them write it down; build pain = "house on fire, sell the fire extinguisher"; two-call vs one-call close tradeoffs; authority frame vs bending to market) are **legitimately good, battle-tested sales advice** — the $10k call teardown and the mastermind call are real operator-level content.
- Member metrics ($44–$88 cost/call, 4–7 calls/day at $350/day spend, $10k deals signing) look **plausible and unembellished** — they're discussed as ordinary problems, not flexed.

**What's ASSERTED / harder than it looks:**
- **The headline income** ($25k/mo at 17, "$1M at 19," any "$2.5M/year") is **never documented** in these videos. Treat as marketing until independently verified.
- **Survivorship is huge.** Even the $10k sales call **does not visibly close on camera** — the prospect runs the numbers wrong, gets confused, and leaves to "talk to his partner / other companies"; Carson *asserts* it closed later ("I'll throw up a payment screenshot," not in transcript). And Carson **openly admits "10–20% of clients" don't succeed** and the agency "barely makes money for 120–150 days." For a beginner with no team, no track record, no niche fluency, and no Carson-built ads, the realistic close rate and survival rate are **well below** what the highlight reel implies.
- **The "easy" framing fights the evidence.** He says the system is "pretty boring" and "not much work" — yet his own best member is "working to midnight," members are getting "burned," "window-shopped," and struggling so badly with sales they're "thinking of paying Cole Gordon" (a $$$ sales coach). The bottleneck for nearly everyone in the mastermind is **sales skill**, which takes months of reps to build. That's the opposite of "lazy way."
- **Capital + access barrier:** the model needs clients who'll pay $5–25k *and* fund $3k+/mo ad spend. Selling that as a true beginner with zero proof is the hard part — and it's exactly the part the funnel solves by **selling you the mentorship** rather than teaching you to do it free.

**Bottom line for a normal beginner:** The *blueprint is real and sound*; the *outcomes shown are top-decile and survivorship-filtered*. A disciplined beginner could plausibly land a mid-four-figure local-business retainer following this — but the "teenager to millionaire, lazy and boring" packaging is **aspirational marketing**, and the most reliable beneficiary of the funnel is **Carson** (mentorship seats + GHL affiliate), not the median viewer.
