## 1) OUTREACH SYSTEM (channels, scripts, volume, cadence, booking)

### 1a. His ranked client-acquisition methods (from `9TRPg0Rj1i8`)
Carson explicitly names **3 "web methods" to close clients** and ranks them — and he positions himself as the contrarian:

1. **Paid ads (PRIMARY, what he actually does).**
   > "most people on YouTube are going to tell you to go to outreach… is that a joke? Are we done with this? … no one does that. That is not predictable any way, shape, or form. **What is predictable is running paid ads.**"
   - Run **your own** FB/IG ads targeting *business owners in your niche*.
   - Drive them to a simple GoHighLevel funnel page → calendar → Zoom/phone sales call.
   - Starter budget: **"$30 a day to start," "$30–50/day is good"** to book some calls.
   - His own spend today: **"1,500 to 2,000 a day… like 45–60K a month on my own ads."**
   - He calls the upfront ad money a **"gambling budget"**: *"have $1,500 to $2,000 set aside to do this."* If you don't have it → **"You need to get a job."**
   - **The ad creative itself states the offer.** He literally prompts an AI ad tool: *"make me an ad. I want to work with realtors. My service is ads plus an AI caller receptionist, and my goal is to get them 10 listing appointments in the next 90 days."* → that AI-generated ad is what he runs.

2. **Organic content (SECONDARY, long-term nurture).**
   - Post YouTube/Instagram with the **niche keyword in the title** (e.g. "roofers," "med spa owners") so the algorithm targets those owners. *"Views are a vanity metric… 50 HVAC business owners that watch your 12-minute video"* is the win.
   - Doubles as a **nurture asset**: he cites a (uncited) stat — *"people need an average of 9 hours [of content consumption] to buy from someone"* — and sends booked leads to his channel before calls.
   - Timeline: **"3 to 6 months to gain traction."**

3. **Cold OUTREACH (LAST RESORT, explicitly discouraged).**
   - Channels he lists: **"Instagram DMs, WhatsApp, cold email, cold calls."** "It's free, it just costs time."
   - When to use it: **"only… if you have no money… if you have $0, you're 13 years old, and you're in a third-world country."**
   - Otherwise: *"if you are of age and you live in a country where you can go make minimum wage… [paid ads] is the move… if that scares you, bite the bullet."*

> **Key takeaway for the "outreach" question:** Carson does **not** teach a cold-outreach machine (no Instantly/Smartlead stacks, no list-building cadence, no DM scripts, no volume targets like "X emails/day"). **He actively steers students away from outreach** toward paid ads + a funnel. The "scripts" he gives are **sales-call scripts**, not outreach scripts. This is a meaningful divergence from the task's framing.

## 2) SALES METHOD (the framework, from the LIVE call `9GJpRIqYz_4`)

This is a real $15k pitch to a **stem cell clinic owner**, with Carson narrating over it. Closer = **"Jacob."** The clinic's pain: a prior agency got them ~100 leads/mo but only ~6 showed and ~3 became patients; overhead $16k/mo vs. ~$12k/mo revenue = **bleeding money** ("code red," in Carson's words). This is the playbook he's demonstrating:

### 2a. Discovery first, differentiate hard
- Open with ~20 sec of small talk, then straight into discovery. Carson: *"I went from like 20 seconds of small talk to straight into pretty much establishing… [Jacob] is completely differentiating what we do and what this client currently works with."*
- Tactic: **make the prospect verbalize the cost of their current setup.** Jacob's exchange:
  > "What happens if they're not educated before they come in? — You're burning a lot of time… — Not just time. What else are you burning?… — Well, money. — There you go."

### 2b. **Drop the price EARLY** (his signature move)
He says this twice and explains the psychology:
- *"we always normally drop price before we even go into the presentation… boom, price, boom, right before we go into things."*
- *"if we wait till the end of the presentation, $15,000 is a big shock number… when you say price upfront, it clears their brain… we've noticed if we delay price toward the end, these people generally don't listen to one word we say. They just are listening for the price."*
- Delivery line: *"I'm not going to hide the price from you. Price is for the full 90 days, it's 15,000. But what you're going to get for that is…"*

### 2c. The **3-pillar presentation** (writes it on a Lucidchart)
He has the prospect write down 3 things, then justifies the price against each:
1. **Lead generation** — *"we launch 40 to 50 new advertisements every single month… even two or three words can change your conversion… by 10, 15, 20%."* Humans script, AI does supplementary visuals; medical ads must be **"compliant with 361"** (compliance is a deliberate trust/expertise signal).
2. **Appointment setting** — **US-based appointment setters call within 60–120 sec** of form fill; 15–20 min structured call that **qualifies on financial capability, medical background, age**; collects a **non-refundable "consultation fee" deposit** (framed as rolled into treatment) to kill tire-kickers. Plus AI drip / educational content auto-sent based on the lead's form answers (VSL-style 20–25 min webinar with per-treatment variations, split-tested).
3. **Sales** — optional closer-for-hire; here they leave closing with the clinic since its in-person close rate is ~50%.

### 2d. Anchor the price to the prospect's OWN numbers
Jacob walks the math live:
- Gets prospect to admit avg ED patient spends **~$5,300** (profit ~$3,500); goal of 4–5 patients/week ⇒ **"$15–18k off of 5–6 patients"** — i.e., one good week > the entire monthly fee. Carson: *"that's already more than what they're doing a month now."*

### 2e. Objection handling on money (see §5)
- Reframe money as a *symptom*: *"most of our successful clients… don't view money as a problem, they view it as a symptom… if you had the right marketing… what else would you have more of? — More money."*
- The **commitment close:** *"My question is if money were out of the equation, would you do this? — Yeah, absolutely."* Then: *"I can help with the investment… but I can't help with the commitment itself. That has to come from you… Are you willing to do that?"*
- When the prospect genuinely can't pay $15k upfront → **finance, don't discount:** restructure to **"$1,300/month + $3,000 ad spend,"** "pay the whole thing off in March." (Note a transcript comedy beat where prospect mis-hears "$13,000/mo.")

### 2f. What he tells students to do differently (the mastermind lessons embedded)
- **Pick a niche you already know** so you can "speak their lingo." He hammers this — the prospect literally closes emotionally on *"no one else is talking to me like this [about stem cells and 361]."* Carson: *"You guys wonder why I tell you pick the niche that you already have the personal relationship with… You can just speak their lingo."*
- **Compliance is a moat** in medical: *"if you work in the medical space… you need to be up to date on regulations and compliance."*
- **Premium price justifies premium service** (and vice versa): *"the reason our price point is so high is because we don't go that [templated] route… most agencies charge five, ten, not even five… because their service normally sucks."*

> **Outcome of this "case study" call (honest):** It did **NOT close on the call.** Carson admits: *"we ended up getting a deposit and still to this day, we are waiting to hear back from him… even if you call this call a no close…"* So the headline "$15,000 sales call" produced **a deposit + an open, unconverted maybe** — SHOWN as a deposit, ASSERTED that "he'll convert down the road." Good teaching honesty, but the title oversells.

  3. **Trials** — *"three-week ads-only trials for just $300–$500"* — the "last resort if you suck at sales / no experience" / foot-in-the-door for case studies.

- **Premium positioning:** charge more *because* the service is genuinely more labor-intensive (40–50 fresh ads/mo, human scripting, compliance) than $3k/mo "templated" competitors.

## 5) OBJECTION HANDLING + QUALIFICATION + RED FLAGS

### Objection handling (verbatim from the live call):

- **"Money is tight" (general):**
  → Reframe as symptom not problem (§2e). → Commitment isolation: *"if money were out of the equation, would you do this?"* → *"I can help with the investment… can't help with the commitment."*
- **"You can't promise results":**
  → Agree, pivot to track record + system: *"I know I can't promise you results, but you guys have been in the space for a while and you're working with others."* (prospect's words — they sell themselves).
- **"How's communication / you have other clients":**
  → Concrete deliverable: *"Weekly statistic updates… Skyler sends a 20–25 min video… weekly one-on-one calls."*
- **Differentiation vs. incumbent agency** is used as a standing objection-preempt: every incumbent weakness (no deposits, slow launch, bad follow-up, templated AI creatives) is hammered.

### Qualification (how he filters):
- **On the sales call:** discovery probes current results, overhead, goals, close rate, *"if money were out of the equation…"* (intent check).
- **In the funnel (built into the offer):** appointment setters qualify on **financial capability, medical background, age**; a **non-refundable consultation-fee deposit** is the hard filter — *"nobody who actually doesn't care about solving this problem… is going to pay that consultation fee."* The whole funnel is designed to *"get rid of as many tire kickers as possible."*

### Red flags / honesty signals Carson himself names:
- Prospect language he treats as bad leads: **"tire kickers," "Medicare moms,"** people who *"come in thinking it's going to be like 50 bucks."*
- He flags that this prospect was **financially shaky** (mortgage payment cited, no credit history) — i.e., a borderline-qualified buyer he still pursued via financing. (Read critically: financing a struggling clinic owner into a $15k deal is itself an ethical gray area he doesn't dwell on.)
