=== PRODUCT RESEARCH ===
**Tool: Kalodata (Cal Data).** Scrapes TikTok Shop best-sellers + UGC video ads.
- TikTok Shop = treasure trove of organic UGC creator content. Rip videos, port to Meta (FB/IG).
- DO NOT pick top-of-page hot products. They're saturated/burnt — Facebook auction will crush you.
- Go DEEP into pages: products doing ~200-300k/month in last 7-30 days (validated but not exploited).
- ALWAYS cross-reference Facebook Ad Library — if many advertisers running it = cooked.

**Product criteria:**
1. **Deep desire** (insecurity, convenience, day-to-day pain) → impulse-buy trigger.
2. **Visual** — sells itself with no explanation needed.
3. **Lightweight + small** (China shipping uses volumetric weight, cutoff ~60cm / under 4-5kg).
4. **Content exists** (TikTok/IG videos already exist about it) — proves demand.
5. **NEW** to market (month-old, not year-old). If old, you can't break in without huge ad team.
6. **Seasonal** allowed — check Google Trends 5-year for Q4 gifts/winter/summer.

**Good example: fleece tights** (visual, deep desire = cold-girls-wanna-wear-cute-outfits, light, seasonal).
**Bad example: titanium cutting board** (needs convincing about microplastics — requires huge ad team).

=== WEBSITE BUILDING ===
- Don't waste time on homepage. **You don't need a homepage.**
- Heatmaps show **60-80% of buyers never scroll past the "hero" section.** Buying decision happens above the fold.
- What matters above fold:
  - Strong product photos (steal from competitors initially, no copyright worry — swap later)
  - 3 USP bullets
  - Visual offer block (use Shopify app **Kaching** — shows BOGO / free gift etc. visually)
  - Guarantee row (free shipping / returns)
  - FAQ accordion
- "Site should take 20-30 minutes." All winners started with this template.
- He cites 8-figure brand with "kinda ass website" — what carried: product photos + offer + product.

=== PRICING ===
- Get true cost from **1688.com** (Chinese factory site), not Alibaba (drop-ship-priced).
- Use Google Translate to get Chinese name, then 1688 search.
- Service fee ~$1-2/order. Get shipping estimate via Uni Express calculator or ChatGPT.
- **3x markup minimum** off (product + shipping + service fee). **4-5x ideal.**
- Math: $10 cost → $30 sell = $20 acquisition buffer. $50 sell = $40 buffer (better).
- **Higher-AOV products preferred:** $30 cost × 3x = $90 sell with $60 buffer even at only 3x markup.

=== META ADS STRUCTURE ===
**Warm up new ad account:** engagement campaign with picture of his dog, $3-4 spend, just to prove you're not a bot.

**Campaign setup:**
- Sales objective, Advantage+ AI, US ONLY (where real brands get built and exit value lives).
- CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization) NOT ABO. $100-150/day budget. 3-day test.
- 6-10 ads, **ONE ad per ad set** (duplicate ad sets, swap creative). All settings stay identical.
- Pixel = AI. **Trust the AI.** Meta spent millions building it — don't try to be smarter with manual interest targeting.
- Ad copy: Steal primary text from competitor in Ad Library → ChatGPT rewrites. Headline = offer ("BOGO Free / Money-back / Results in 30 days").
- If ads keep getting rejected → strip primary text, keep simple headline. Avoid scammy words.
- Start campaign at 12-5am (fresh day for the AI).
- **Never touch anything during days 1-3.** Don't add/remove ads. Don't change budget.

=== METRICS BY DAY ===
**Day 1** (just need proof of concept):
- CPM <$30 ideal, <$50-70 acceptable.
- CPC <$2.
- CTR 2-3% (1% ok).
- **Add-to-carts 5-10%** ← most important on day 1, NOT sales.
- (Read ATC from Shopify analytics, not Meta.)

**Day 2:**
- CPM should drop.
- ROAS 0.8-2 (sales coming in, not necessarily profitable).
- ATC trending up.

**Day 3 (verdict day):**
- CPM $30-40, CPC $1-2, ATC 5-10%.
- Should be break-even or profitable.
- **Cut if not improving across the board.** Don't get emotionally attached.
- Show: real example, day 1 CPM $70/CPC $3 → day 3 CPM $12/CPC $0.70. That's pixel learning.

=== ITERATION (after Day 3) ===
Dig into ad-set spend distribution. Three outcomes:
1. **High spend, no sales** → ad concept is OK (FB chose to spend) but execution bad. Rebuild script/copy/footage under same concept.
2. **High spend, lots of sales** → reiterate variations of that winning concept.
3. **Low spend, sales** → leave it alone (low-hanging-fruit ad — force-spend will kill it).

After consistent profit (day 4-5+):
- STOP repeating same concept (you already did that).
- Test NEW angles (dog-owners → parents → relationships) + NEW formats (UGC testimonial → voice-over → comparison).
- Creative fatigue is real — every winning ad has a peak then crashes; pipeline of new concepts required.
- ONE testing campaign per store. Each new ad = new ad set inside same campaign. NEVER 20 campaigns.
