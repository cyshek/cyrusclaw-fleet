# SELECTION RUBRIC — the bar an idea must clear to be worth our time
_Created 2026-06-08. This is the anti-self-deception gate. Every candidate (product × distribution engine) gets scored here before it earns more research or any build. My known weak spot is ECONOMIC OPTIMISM, so this rubric is deliberately harsh on the money + distribution claims._

## The mandate it enforces (Cyrus, 2026-06-08)
- **Floor of interest = $10k/mo.** Below that, don't build it. Sub-$2k/mo utilities (App Guardian) are parked floors, not the mission.
- **Optimize for OUTCOME MAGNITUDE** — large recurring MRR OR a Base44-shaped 7–8 figure exit. Research BOTH shapes.
- **Time is NOT a constraint.** Depth of research traded for higher odds is explicitly wanted.
- **Constraints:** no founder audience; agent-run ops; ~$0–50 cash; founder face-time AVAILABLE-BUT-MINIMIZED (agents script, Cyrus performs occasionally only where structurally unavoidable); Cyrus = one-time legal/financial root of trust (Stripe KYC, entity, bank, dev accounts).

## The 6 hard gates (a candidate must pass ALL to advance)
A candidate is scored 0–5 on each. **Any gate scoring ≤2 = killed or sent back**, no matter how good the others look. The whole point is that one fatal flaw kills it — averages lie.

### Gate 1 — CEILING (is $10k/mo+ even physically possible?)
- Must show, with REAL comparables, that businesses of this shape reach ≥$10k/mo. Not "could in theory" — name 2+ real ones near/above the line.
- TAM must be large or fast-growing enough that $10k/mo is a small slice, not the whole market.
- 5 = clear path to $50k/mo+ with comparables; 3 = $10k/mo plausible with strong execution; ≤2 = caps out below $10k/mo. **KILL if ≤2.**

### Gate 2 — DISTRIBUTION LOOP WITHOUT A FOUNDER AUDIENCE (the killer gate)
- There must be a SPECIFIC, evidenced way to reach customers that does NOT require Cyrus to already have an audience. One of: (a) paid ads with viable CAC<LTV math, (b) a structural product-led loop (the product carries its own distribution), (c) marketplace built-in buyer intent, (d) an organic channel proven to survive AI/automated content + the minimal scriptable founder face.
- "We'll do content marketing / go viral / post on social" with no structural loop = automatic ≤2.
- 5 = loop is built into the core product action (mandatory virality) OR proven CAC<LTV; 3 = a real but effortful channel exists; ≤2 = secretly audience-dependent. **KILL if ≤2.** This is where most ideas die.

### Gate 3 — AGENT-RUNNABLE OPERATIONS
- Build, marketing creation, customer support, and iteration must be doable by agents at ~95%, with Cyrus as a thin scriptable human layer only where unavoidable.
- Heavy ongoing human labor (manual fulfillment, real-time human sales, hand-holding onboarding) = low score.
- 5 = fully autonomous after setup; 3 = mostly autonomous, thin human layer; ≤2 = needs a real human operator. **KILL if ≤2.**

### Gate 4 — DEFENSIBILITY / MOAT (does winning stick?)
- AI makes code free → the product itself is never the moat. The moat must be: the distribution loop, proprietary data, network effects, brand, or switching costs.
- "Anyone can clone this in a weekend and there's nothing stopping them" = low score.
- 5 = strong compounding moat (data/network/loop); 3 = some switching cost or head-start; ≤2 = trivially cloned, no defense. **KILL if ≤2 ONLY IF the loop (Gate 2) is also weak; a strong enough loop can BE the moat.**

### Gate 5 — STRATEGIC-ACQUIRER FIT (only required for the EXIT case)
- For an exit-shaped bet: name a CLASS of acquirer (or specific companies) who would pay a premium to own this — usually because it threatens their core or fills an obvious hole (Base44 → Wix defensive buy).
- For the MRR-machine case, this gate is N/A (score "MRR-track").
- 5 = obvious named strategic buyers with a defensive reason; 3 = plausible acquirer exists; ≤2 = nobody strategic would buy it. (Only kills an EXIT-labeled candidate.)

### Gate 6 — DOWNSIDE / LEGITIMACY
- Transparent money mechanism I can explain dollar-for-dollar. Bounded, reversible/capped downside within the $0–50 leash + Cyrus's one-time setup. No drainers, no free-money traps, no unfair advantage, nothing that depends on deceiving users or violating platform ToS in a way that nukes the whole business.
- Flag any irreducible Cyrus touchpoints up front.
- 5 = clean, legit, bounded; 3 = some ToS/gray risk that's survivable; ≤2 = relies on a trap, fraud, or business-ending ToS violation. **KILL if ≤2.**
