## 1. The Actual Offer(s)

### 1.1 The headline offer (most common across videos)
A **three-pillar done-for-you AI service** sold to local service businesses, fulfilled almost entirely via outsourcing.

The three pillars:
1. **Lead generation** — AI UGC video ads built on `arcads.ai`, run on Meta (Facebook + Instagram). [`m1SAOzAi28A` @ ~0–1m; `ofESUSaRgag` @ ~12–13m]
2. **Appointment setting** — AI voice caller built on `retell.ai` (sometimes called "Retail AI" in transcripts) or Synthflow, calls inbound leads within seconds and books them onto the client's calendar. [`m1SAOzAi28A` @ ~1–2m; `Z91gE18IhgM` summary]
3. **Closing assist** — Either (a) Claude AI rewrites the client's existing sales script weekly from their call recordings [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary; `rw3Dga3VXuo` @ ~27m], or (b) Wyatt's team places an "American closer" onto Zoom calls for the client (medical niche only) [`ofESUSaRgag` @ ~2m, ~15–16m].

### 1.4 Stem-cell-clinic niche specifics (`AugsZMdqb8Q`)
- **Niche reveal:** Florida stem-cell law changed July 1, 2025 → regulatory tailwind. Wyatt's partner Peter was already at $200k/mo in this space and joined as fulfillment lead.
- **LTV economics:** $3k–$96k per treatment, $8k–$12k average package. Doctors treat marketing as a tax write-off, which Wyatt says makes $15k closes easier.
- **Compliance constraints become a moat:** can't say "stem cell" in ads; must use "protocol," "treatment," "support," "options" — high barrier to other agencies entering.
- **Org structure used:** Wyatt = Head of Sales; Carson (roommate) = Head of Marketing; Peter = Head of Fulfillment + niche expertise.
- **Two-call close** (versus his usual one-call), based on Peter's data from the niche. Call 1 = appointment setter qualification; Call 2 = closer pitches/closes.
- **First-month results (claimed):** ~$60k cash collected / ~$80k revenue; later cited as $57.5k cash w/ 67% margin [`ofESUSaRgag` @ ~0–1m]; later still cited as $80k cash / $100k revenue at 67% margin [`9NLC7kzJHqQ` @ ~3m]. *Numbers move depending on the video.*
- **Florida lawful niche** confirmed by prospect saying "You know the law changed there, right? July 1st. We wrote the law." [`AugsZMdqb8Q` summary]
- **Stated culture quotes from sales-call recordings:** "we don't want to work with the lower class" re: people who can't afford treatments; "let's just take your average shady doctor." [`AugsZMdqb8Q` summary]

## 3. Acquisition Channels

### 3.1 What he says works
**Paid Meta ads** — primary channel for ~90% of his clients. [`znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~0–1m]
- Format: AI UGC video ads from `arcads.ai` (script via Claude/ChatGPT, AI actor talking head) [`m1SAOzAi28A` @ ~0–1m; `rw3Dga3VXuo` @ ~29–31m] OR static text-on-white-canvas image ads via Canva (magic-expand) [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary; `ofESUSaRgag` @ ~13m].
- Imitation strategy: open Facebook Ads Library → search "roofing leads" / "HVAC leads" / niche term → download competitor ads with a "Facebook ad library downloader" → drop transcription straight into Arcads → swap actor. "We don't innovate, we imitate." [`znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~1–4m]
- Setup: Meta Pixel on GHL funnel; Advantage+ placements; objective = leads or traffic; lifetime budget; 1–5 captions per ad; short videos (15–30s); copy = curiosity + problem + benefit + proof + CTA. [`ofESUSaRgag` @ ~13–15m]
- Budget pattern told to clients: start at $50/day proof-of-concept → scale to $100/day when CPL ≈ $75–100 per qualified call → guarantee kicks in at $100/day. [`8jlrLrKn_7M` @ ~22–23m]
- His own budget on the $57k month: **$14,000 spend → $57,500 revenue** in 30 days. [`ofESUSaRgag` @ ~11–12m]

**YouTube content (his preferred long-term)** [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary; `znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~0–1m]
- VidIQ extension (free) → find videos performing 10×–20× channel average → copy title, thumbnail, structure → put your face on it.
- High-intent watchers: "HVAC owner googling 'how to get jobs' = high-intent buyer." Even 10-view videos convert.

**Inbound from his own funnel** (mentorship sales) — gameplan call → mentorship close. He runs this same funnel on himself. [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary]

### 3.2 What he says doesn't work
**Cold outreach** (cold DMs, cold email, cold calling). Direct quotes / paraphrase:
- "Cold outreach is a complete waste of time… you'll never get a cold DM from Microsoft." [`znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~0–1m]
- "You're better off working at McDonald's and putting $100/day into ads." [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary; `rw3Dga3VXuo` @ ~28–29m]
- Argument: cold outreach puts you in submissive position; inbound (ads + YouTube) puts you in authority position. [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary]
- Concedes: if you don't have ad money, work a McDonald's job and reinvest — don't bother with DMs. [`m1SAOzAi28A` @ ~7m]

**AI callers calling AI-agency leads (specifically)** — he says don't use AI to call leads who opted into AI-agency ads, because they'll pressure-test the AI ("what color are your jeans," inappropriate questions) and bots can't handle it. AI callers DO work when your client's customers receive them (e.g., 67-year-old Nancy who wants a new roof doesn't expect AI). [`HEbNqoaexkc` @ ~1–2m; `vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~4m]

**Organic short-form content alone** — can work but requires being good on camera + 2–3 posts/week minimum. Not his bet. [`znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~1m]

### 3.3 Cold-outreach exception (mentorship-friendly tier)
- Professional services (lawyers, accountants) are the *one* place he says LinkedIn Sales Navigator outreach works ($100/mo subscription). Cites a mastermind member doing $100k/mo from a single law-firm client landed via LinkedIn + Facebook groups + McKinsey data deck. [`9NLC7kzJHqQ` @ ~6–7m]
- Outreach volume rule (when you do it): **"500 a day. I'm not kidding."** [`FRT8zyUEKW8` summary]

## 4. Funnel Mechanics (Step by Step)

Built on **GoHighLevel** (snapshot offered in video descriptions) [`rw3Dga3VXuo` @ ~10–11m]. Two-call close is the newer/preferred medical model [`znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~9–10m]; older one-call close still appears in earlier scripts.

### Step 1 — Meta ad
Headline pattern: "Attention [NICHE]" + end-result CTA. Examples:
- "ROOFERS will bring you verified leads on autopilot. Just pay per job." [`ofESUSaRgag` @ ~12–13m]
- "Get 90 patients in 90 days on a pay-per-result basis." [`m1SAOzAi28A` @ ~4m]
- "If you're a peptide clinic looking to get 30–90 patients every month on a pay-per-result basis…" [`m1SAOzAi28A` @ ~9m]
- AI actor reads the script (15–30s). [`rw3Dga3VXuo` @ ~30–31m]

### Step 2 — Opt-in / VSL page
- Logo + headline calling out niche + branded mechanism name (e.g., `Soul.ai`, `dental.ai`, `shingle.ai`, `judge.ai`, `brush.ai`, `sun.ai`). [`rw3Dga3VXuo` @ ~12m; `Z91gE18IhgM` summary; `ofESUSaRgag` @ ~6m]
- Short VSL video at top. Below: a **survey** with mostly "filler" questions (state, capacity, etc.) so the financial-qualification questions feel less invasive — "How much do you have to invest?" / "How much are you making?" [`znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~6–7m]
- "Talk only end results, never mechanism." [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary]
- Always capture name/email/phone *before* showing the calendar — so leads who don't self-book still enter re-engagement. [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary; `rw3Dga3VXuo` @ ~13m]

### Step 3 — Calendar page
Same copy reiterated + embedded Calendly/GHL calendar. Settings: 4-day forward range, 12-hour scheduling notice. [`rw3Dga3VXuo` @ ~20m]

### Step 4 — Pre-call page (post-booking)
Goal = push toward Google's 7-hour content-consumption rule even though you'll never hit 7 hours. [`eDbydkAY5XU` @ ~0–1m]
- 3-minute (or 30–60 min) pre-call video from the closer who'll take the call. [`eDbydkAY5XU` @ ~1–2m; `ofESUSaRgag` @ ~7–8m; `znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~8m]
- Below: 4 short FAQ videos answering top objections [`m1SAOzAi28A` @ ~8–9m].
- Below: testimonial videos, screenshots, case studies [`m1SAOzAi28A` @ ~9m].
- Optional: Google Doc, Facebook group, "AI caller use cases" doc [`eDbydkAY5XU` @ ~2–3m].
- $100 anchor for no-shows/distracted attendees referenced in some versions [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary].

### Step 5 — Pre-call re-engagement (for leads who don't self-book within ~2–3 min)
Three parallel systems, all in GoHighLevel:

**A. SMS appointment-setter bot (GHL + ChatGPT integration):**
- Initial: "Hey {first_name}, this is Jessica with the {company} team. Saw you wanted to learn more about [offer]. Were you able to find a time to speak…? I can drop my calendar link here if it's easier." [`HEbNqoaexkc` @ ~2–3m; `vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~1–2m; `rw3Dga3VXuo` @ ~18–19m]
- Cadence: wait 1h → `{first_name}?` → wait 1 day → `you alive lol?` → wait 1 day → `well this is awkward` [`HEbNqoaexkc` @ ~3m; `vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~2m]
- Then daily meme drops from `imgflip.com` (skeleton on bench, Uno-draw-25, Pablo Escobar waiting). [`HEbNqoaexkc` @ ~3–4m; `vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~9–10m]

**B. AI voice call (Retell / Synthflow / Sendinflow AI):**
- Cadence: **immediately → 1h → 3h → 24h → then 1×/week forever.** [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary]
- Philosophy: **"Buy, die, or say goodbye."** [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary]
- *Caveat:* For *his own* AI-agency leads he uses humans, not AI callers, because the leads pressure-test the bot (§3.2).

**C. Email sequence (7 days):**
- Cadence: email + wait 1 day × 7. Tried 14 and 30 days, open rates dropped; settled on 7. [`vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~3–4m]
- Written by Claude AI with prompt: *"Can you generate 7 emails written like Dan Henry for my AI agency? I attached a PDF with all my agency's info in it. The CTA is to book a free consult call. Here's the link to do so."* [`vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~2–3m; `rw3Dga3VXuo` @ ~21–23m]
- The PDF is a **one-pager** Google Doc covering: ICP, offer, messaging, price, guarantee, refund policy. Treated as agency source-of-truth. [`vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~2–3m]

**D. Human phone setting (Slack-routed):**
- Slack channel auto-populates new lead w/ name, email, phone, revenue. 3-min grace period for self-book, then setter dials. [`vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~4–5m]
- Auto-dialer via `dialer.io` for speed-to-lead under 2 min. [`HEbNqoaexkc` @ ~5–6m]
- Pipeline stages: Fresh Lead → Call Attempt 2 → Call Attempt 3 → Dead Lead. Stops at 3 tries (tested up to 7, ROI dropped). [`vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~6–7m]

### Step 6 — Post-booking automation (after lead self-books or setter books them)
Exact sequence (from `eDbydkAY5XU` @ ~3–5m and `ofESUSaRgag` @ ~10–11m):
1. **T+0:** Robotic-looking confirmation email (deliberately robotic).
2. **T+1 minute:** Human-sounding email driving them to the pre-call page/video.
3. **T+1m + 6 sec:** SMS message 1 (split into 3 short messages with 6s + 12s gaps so it looks human-typed in real time).
4. **T-24 hours:** Email + SMS reminder.
5. **T-3 hours:** Email + SMS (two-call model only). [`znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~11m]
6. **T-1 hour:** AI voice call reminder (Sendinflow / Retell) + email + SMS.
7. **T-10 minutes:** Pre-recorded voicemail drop via GHL ringless voicemail + email + SMS.

Parallel **"hammer them" sequence** (varied content formats: YouTube video, text, PDF, voice note) running alongside reminders to nurture different consumption styles. [`znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~11–13m]

### Step 7 — Discovery call (two-call model only)
- Done on phone, not Zoom. [`znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~10m]
- Setter qualifies (money, fit, location), books demo call, pre-frames the demo closer ("you'll be talking to our executive consultant Jacob…"). [`znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~10–11m; `HEbNqoaexkc` @ ~10m]
- Closer is framed as "executive consultant" not "sales rep." [`HEbNqoaexkc` @ ~10m]

### Step 8 — Sales / demo call (Zoom)
See §5 for full script. Pitched on a Lucid Chart with 3 pillars. No software demos opened ("confused buyers don't buy"). [`eDbydkAY5XU` @ ~8–9m; `HEbNqoaexkc` @ ~17–19m]

### Step 9 — Post-call follow-up (`vM2WATRYRo0`)
**For no-shows:**
1. Wait 10 min. **Triple-dial from personal cell** ("no one calls 3 times unless it's an emergency"). [`vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~8–9m]
2. SMS sequence: initial "we had a call at 3pm EST, assuming this was a mistake??" → 1h → 1d → then daily memes (trade offers, "respond to me or draw 25"). [`vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~9–10m]
3. Short email subject: `our appointment` (lowercase, no period). Signed *"sent from my iPhone"* (stolen from Grant Cardone). [`vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~10m]

**For no-close (showed up, didn't buy):**
1. Book a follow-up *on the call*, <7 days out (ideally next day). [`vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~10–11m]
2. Reminders rewritten in follow-up context ("I know Wednesday didn't work, but our call's coming up"). [`vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~11m]
3. **Hail Mary** 1–2×/month: "Hey, are you still interested in [outcome]? I could send you a short doc breaking down how it works." Or: "Just got off a call with my head of partner success — I think I have a strategy for your situation." [`vM2WATRYRo0` @ ~11–13m]

### Step 10 — Onboarding
- "Same-day or next-day onboarding call" with Peter (operations/founder). [`8jlrLrKn_7M` @ ~20–21m]
- Contract sent during onboarding, adjusted if client objects ("we are an open book"). Wyatt corrects Jacob mid-call: **never call it a "contract" — call it an "agreement."** [`8jlrLrKn_7M` @ ~25–26m]
- Acknowledged failure mode (stem-cell agency): "doctors won't even fill out our onboarding form" was the live bottleneck at month 1. [`AugsZMdqb8Q` summary]

## 5. Sales Scripts (Direct Quotes)

### 5.1 Setter call intro [`HEbNqoaexkc` @ ~6–7m]
> "What's up, {name}? Hey {name}, this is your name with owner's team at {company name}. I saw in our system that you applied to become a partner but didn't end up booking a call. Just checking in to see if this was a glitch or an issue and maybe learn a little bit more about your business and send some free value your way. Awesome. And by the way, {name}, this call is recorded for quality assurance purposes and I'm actually a manager over here at {company}'s partner success team. I've been assigned to your account. So the appointment you would have booked is just like a phone call with me. So, I'd love to just go ahead and treat this like a partnership application call so we can get you booked in with just a few quick questions."

**Why:** "partnership" framing softens — "if people feel like they're about to buy from you, they're just going to hang up the phone." [`HEbNqoaexkc` @ ~7m]

### 5.2 Discovery questions [`HEbNqoaexkc` @ ~7–9m; `eDbydkAY5XU` @ ~6m; `m1SAOzAi28A` @ ~11–14m]
- "So just so I get a baseline — where is your business currently in terms of monthly revenue?"
- *(follow-up)* "Gotcha — is that all profit?" → Wyatt: this is how you back into the real profit number.
- "Where do you want to be revenue-wise 3–6 months from now?"
- "How long have you been trying to get to {goal}?"
- "What systems and processes do you have to get customers at will?"
- *(if none)* "You have no systems or processes in place?" *(super-concerned tone)* "How long have you been operating this way?" → Wyatt: "build pain, make them realize they should have been doing something."
- "What's your current offer and price point?" (or niche-specific: "How much are you charging for a roof?")

## 9. What's Repeatable for Someone with NO Sales Experience but Strong Agent/Dev Capability

Brutally honest read of the surface area:

### 9.1 What a dev/agent-capable person CAN cleanly replicate
- **The funnel build.** GHL snapshot + the exact 3-page flow (opt-in → calendar → pre-call) is straightforwardly templated. A dev can stand it up in a day. [`rw3Dga3VXuo`]
- **The automation SOPs.** All 3 re-engagement systems (SMS bot, AI voice, 7-day email) are mechanical. An agent can wire them to GHL + Retell + Claude API with zero novel insight. [`vM2WATRYRo0`]
- **The ad creative pipeline.** Imitate-not-innovate from FB Ads Library → Claude rewrites the script → Arcads renders it. Pure tool chaining; no creative skill required. [`znu-DSlZMDY` @ ~1–4m]
- **The fulfillment substack.** Hiring a $1k Upwork dev to build a Retell agent is below the skill of someone reading this. You can probably *do* the fulfillment yourself at higher quality than the $1k subcontractor.
- **The pre-call nurture stack.** Pre-call video + utility-belt PDFs + ROI calculator are pure asset production. A capable agent operator can build the ROI calculator as a TypeScript page in an afternoon.
- **The one-pager + email generation.** Already designed to be done by Claude. Trivial.

### 9.2 What's load-bearing but learnable
- **Sales-call script execution.** The scripts are explicit and well-structured. Discovery → "three things I hear" → 3-pillar pitch → feeler → drop price → utility belt. Memorizable in a weekend. Hardest part is *tonality* and the willingness to "twist the knife" on pain — Wyatt himself says this on tape. [`FRT8zyUEKW8` summary]
- **Pre-call qualification.** Just a survey + a 2-min phone screen. Code-able as a form, but the human screen is where the cash-on-hand gate happens.
- **The actual "first ever sale" hurdle.** Jacob did it cold and closed $10k in his first few weeks. That data point cuts both ways — it argues the script works for an inexperienced person, but Jacob still had door-to-door background. [`AugsZMdqb8Q` summary]

### 9.3 What's structurally hard for a solo dev/agent
- **Money on the table.** The pitch is at $10–15k upfront from real human business owners over Zoom. You can't agentify this part. Either you sit on the call yourself, or you hire (and pay) a Jacob-equivalent. There is no automation shortcut here.
- **Trust assets.** Wyatt's close rate leans heavily on 140+ Trust Pilot reviews, named-mastermind clients, screenshotted Stripe payments, and a YouTube channel. A new entrant has none of these on day 1. The first 5–10 deals are uniquely hard because the utility belt is empty.
- **Niche access.** The stem-cell windfall is a *regulatory-tailwind moment* in one state. Most of Wyatt's stated edge ($15k closes, easy compliance moat) comes from being in the right niche at the right time with the right partner (Peter, who was already at $200k/mo). A solo agent operator can copy the niche but not the partner.
- **Capital exposure.** You burn $1–14k+ in Meta ads before close #1. If you have no W-2 income, this is real money at risk. The "you don't need experience" framing skips this.
- **Ad-account / compliance heat.** Medical-adjacent niches get accounts shut down. You'll be cycling Meta accounts, possibly losing pixel data, and need a legal/compliance review of every script. Compliance is the moat *if you can pay your way through it*, otherwise it's the wall you crash into.
- **Onboarding pull-through.** Stem-cell agency's actual month-1 bottleneck was clients not filling out the onboarding form. *Closing the deal is not the bottleneck — getting paid out from fulfillment is.* You need a real human (Peter-type) who can call doctors and chase paperwork. This is the *least* automatable part of the business.
- **Refund-scam exposure.** Clients game the ROI guarantee. Wyatt's team had to add proof-of-show clauses *after* getting burned. A solo operator gets burned first and learns second.
- **Sales recovery from no-shows / no-closes.** Triple-dialing from your personal cell and managing a Hail Mary follow-up cycle is real, human, repeated work. The follow-up SOP is automatable; the *triple-dial* and "did the lead actually pick up" are not.

### 9.4 Honest verdict for the brief
- If "agent/dev capability" means *you can build automations and write code well*, you can replicate **everything in §4 (funnel) and §3.1 (ads) and ~80% of §1 (offer) end-to-end with no sales experience.** That puts you at the level where you have a funnel that *generates qualified opt-ins and books calls automatically.*
- The chasm is **the Zoom call where someone asks you for $10,000**. Wyatt's entire business above that line; everything below it is the marketing apparatus.
- **Two viable paths for the no-sales-experience operator:**
  1. **Use the funnel as a lead-gen-for-yourself.** Run the system, generate booked calls, then *sell those booked calls* to an existing close-capable agency or solo closer at $1–2k per booked call. You skip the close entirely. (Wyatt does not describe this path, but the architecture supports it.)
  2. **Use the funnel + buy a closer.** Find a Jacob via Wyatt's "personal network" rule, give them 30–50% commission, and you are the operator behind the curtain. Cost: you fund ads + a closer's commission before profit. Risk: dependency on one person until you can hire a second.
- **What you CANNOT skip:**
  - You will spend real money on ads before close 1.
  - You will need someone (you or hired) on Zoom willing to ask for $10–15k.
  - You will need to handle compliance + onboarding chasing + refund scams.
  - You will need to keep generating new ad creative as the existing ones fatigue (Wyatt's mid-month dry spell).
- **The arbitrage thesis is real but the labor is misrepresented.** Wyatt frames this as "client pays 3 stacks, you outsource 1 stack, keep 2 stacks, virtually no work." [`Z91gE18IhgM` summary] What he leaves out: the work is *acquisition + closing + chasing onboarding + creative refresh + dealing with refund scams + managing setters/closers/devs*. That's a job — sometimes a 3-partner job, per the stem-cell agency.
