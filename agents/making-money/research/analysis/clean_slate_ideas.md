# Clean-Slate Money-Making Ideas — Cyrus + 24/7 AI Agent

*Brutally honest. Action over caution. Selection criterion: Expected Money × Fit. Inputs: Wyatt (AI agency) + Subi (dropshipping) playbooks, cross-cut against the operator profile (busy founder, $1–3k burn, can sign/pay/2-decisions-week, can't take calls; agent is 24/7, codes, browses, messages, spawns subagents).*

---

## Hard Constraints That Shape Everything Below

1. **No phone calls from agent, no on-camera person.** Anything that requires a Zoom closer asking for $10k is either Cyrus on calls (he said he can't reliably) or a hired closer (rev share). That's not a non-starter, but it kills "agent does it all" agency dreams.
2. **$1–3k initial burn.** Wyatt's own "$1–2k ads to first client" matches. Subi's "$1.5k cumulative loss before a winner" is realistic. Anything requiring $10k+ upfront is gated until something works.
3. **Cyrus has OpenClaw.** That's an actual asset — multi-agent orchestration, browser/exec/cron/messaging — not just a dev. Several ideas below are *only* viable because of that platform.
4. **30–90 day revenue target.** Excludes anything where the path to first dollar is 6+ months (e.g. SEO sites, app store apps from scratch, B2B SaaS with 6-month sales cycle).
5. **No trading.** Skipping all market/arb/HFT/onchain MEV plays.

## The 12 Ideas

### 1. Wyatt-Style AI Agency (lead-gen for local high-ticket services), Cyrus-on-calls-2hr/day variant

**What:** Sell roofing / HVAC / med-spa / accountant owners a $5–15k upfront "AI lead gen + AI caller + script rewrite" package. 90-day term, ROI-or-refund guarantee, then $1k/mo retainer. The whole back-end (ad creative pipeline, GHL funnel, Retell voice agents, follow-up SMS/email, AI script rewrites from call recordings) is automated by the agent. Cyrus only does Zoom discovery + close calls.

**Why fits:** Wyatt's stack is 80% commodity tooling chained together — the *exact* shape of work an agent excels at (Cal Library scrapes, Arcads renders, Claude rewrites, GHL automations, Retell builds). The unautomatable 20% is the Zoom close. Cyrus is a founder — he can do 2hr/day of sales calls if the economics are real ($7k+ margin per deal). Capital fit: $1–2k ads to first client matches budget exactly.

**Capital:** $2k (GHL $97, Arcads ~$200, Meta ads $1.5k to first close, Retell credits $50, domain $5).
**Time-to-first-revenue:** 30–60 days realistic. Wyatt's own "first sale day 7" requires luck; plan for 45.
**12-mo ceiling:** $300k–$1M revenue, $150k–$500k net, IF Cyrus does calls consistently and they hire a closer by month 3. Wyatt at $57k/mo first month is the upper anchor.
**What kills it:** Cyrus's calendar — if he can't reliably do 2hr/day of calls, the funnel fills up with booked leads and rots. Also: medical compliance ad bans, refund-scam clients, paid-traffic dependency.
**Split:** Agent 75% (all build/fulfillment/follow-up/onboarding chase), Operator 25% (calls + occasional creative greenlight + payment signing).

### 2. Subi-Style Dropshipping with VEO3 AI-UGC Creative Pipeline

**What:** Pick a TikTok-Shop-validated product from Kalodata (page 20–40, not page 1), source 1688/Alibaba via agent, slap up a Shrine theme Shopify store in 2 hours, generate 8–12 ad variants using VEO3 + ripped TikTok B-roll, launch Meta CBO $150/day USA-only, run Subi's 3-day kill protocol. Repeat ~5–8 times until a winner hits.

**Why fits:** VEO3 + Prompt Cowboy + Claude scripts collapses the *one* thing dropshipping previously needed humans for (UGC creative). Agent can run product research, store build, ad gen, daily metric reads, kill/scale decisions, customer service VA-script responses, and 1688 sourcing-agent DMs end-to-end. Cyrus signs the LLC + funds the Stripe.

**Capital:** $3k tight ($150/day × 3 days × 5 tests = $2,250 ad spend + Cal Data $69 + Shopify $39 + apps $80 + sample stock $200–400). $5k more comfortable.
**Time-to-first-revenue:** 7–21 days (day-2 sales on a hit per Subi). Winner discovery: 30–60 days.
**12-mo ceiling:** Hit rate is the variance. Realistic median outcome: $0 (no winner found) or $20–80k/mo on a moderate winner. Tail: $400k/mo (Subi's vibration plate). Plan for either lose-it-all or 6-figure month by month 4.
**What kills it:** Meta ad account ban cascade (Q4 wave is real), creative fatigue if you only have AI hooks, Shopify Payments freeze if you scale too fast, chargebacks if product quality bad, ad-account sabotage at scale, tariff math (post-May-7 de minimis kill).
**Split:** Agent 90% (research, build, creatives, ads management, CS, sourcing), Operator 10% (LLC/processor signing, Meta BM identity verification, occasional gut-check kill decisions, fund replenishment).

### 3. Productized AI Voice Receptionist for Local Service Businesses ($297–$497/mo SaaS-flavored)

**What:** Sell HVAC/plumber/dentist/law-office owners a fully-managed Retell/Vapi voice agent that answers their main line 24/7, books appointments into their existing calendar (Jobber/ServiceTitan/Calendly/GHL), sends an SMS follow-up, and emails a daily call log. Flat $297/mo per location, $497/mo if multi-location. One-time $500 setup. No upfront $10k, no big close required.

**Why fits:** Same fulfillment stack as #1 (Retell + GHL + Twilio + Zapier) but priced for self-serve / soft-close. Cyrus can sell via email + 15-min Zoom demos (less stamina than 1-hour high-ticket pitches). Agent does literally all setup: scrapes business info, builds the prompt, configures call routing, sends onboarding video. Recurring revenue compounds — way more durable than agency model. Wyatt himself proves the underlying AI-caller pitch works on Nancy-the-67yo-roofing-lead; this just sells *that* to her roofer directly.

**Capital:** $500 (Retell $100, Twilio $50 for numbers, domain, GHL or n8n $30). Cold outreach via email/LinkedIn = no paid ads needed.
**Time-to-first-revenue:** 14–45 days. Faster than #1 because no $10k ask.
**12-mo ceiling:** 100 customers × $400 avg = $40k/mo MRR = $480k ARR. At 80% gross margin (Retell minutes are the main COGS), ~$320k/yr profit. Realistic year-1: 30–50 paying = $12–20k MRR.
**What kills it:** Voice AI is a crowded category (Synthflow, Bland, Goodcall, dozens). Differentiation comes from niche-fit + done-for-you setup. Also: if 1 noisy customer churns and trash-talks, reputation hit.
**Split:** Agent 85% (lead research, outreach drafting, setup automation, ongoing prompt tuning, monthly reports), Operator 15% (the 15-min Zoom demo, contract signing, fund Stripe).

### 4. Productized "Done-For-You Inbound Lead Engine" for B2B SaaS Founders ($2k/mo retainer)

**What:** Target solo / small B2B SaaS founders ($5k–$50k MRR) who hate marketing. Sell them: agent-driven ICP scraping + personalized LinkedIn/email outreach (Smartlead/Instantly + Clay) + landing page + content distribution. Flat $2k/mo, no ad spend included, 3-month minimum. You produce 10 booked demos / month or partial refund.

**Why fits:** B2B SaaS founders ARE the operator — they get the value, won't haggle on $2k. Outreach is the perfect agent task (research, personalization, sending, reply triage, booking). Cyrus knows this customer (he IS one). No phone calls needed — entirely async via DM/email. Wyatt explicitly says don't sell to SaaS founders for $10k AI-agency offers; but $2k retainers for *outbound execution* is a different pitch — they're buying labor, not insight.

**Capital:** $500 (Smartlead $97, Clay $149, Apollo $99, domain pool $150).

**12-mo ceiling:** 25 clients × $2k = $50k MRR = $600k ARR. Realistic year-1: 8–15 clients = $16–30k MRR.
**What kills it:** Email deliverability (cold inbox warmup is real), reply quality (if agent's personalization is shallow, prospects sniff it), retention (founders churn fast if pipeline doesn't materialize in 60 days).
**Split:** Agent 90% (everything operational), Operator 10% (initial 30-min strategy call per new client + Stripe).

### 5. Vertical Newsletter + Affiliate/Sponsorship Stack (1 vertical, daily AI-written, niche-deep)

Agent writes a daily newsletter — news roundup + 1 deep analysis + 1 tool spotlight. Distribute via Beehiiv. Monetize: (a) Beehiiv Boosts (paid to add referrals to other newsletters), (b) sponsorships at $50/k subs starting at ~3k subs, (c) affiliate to software in the niche, (d) eventually a $97/mo paid premium tier.

**What kills it:** Agent slop = unsubscribes. Quality bar must be HIGH. Picking a niche too narrow (no advertisers) or too broad (no resonance).
**Split:** Agent 95% (everything), Operator 5% (niche pick + weekly QA spot check + sponsorship signing).

### 6. SaaS Micro-Tool the Agent Researches & Ships in a Vertical (the "build for an audience you observe" play)

**What:** Spend 2 weeks lurking in 5 niche Reddits / Discords / Facebook groups (agent scrapes + summarizes daily). Identify the most-repeated workflow pain. Ship a $19–49/mo single-purpose web tool that solves it. Examples that fit this shape: "AI estimate generator for landscapers from a photo," "auto-generate Etsy listing photos from one product shot," "scrape & monitor competitor pricing for Amazon FBA sellers," "WhatsApp catalog sync for small wholesalers."

**Why fits:** Cyrus is a strong dev with OpenClaw — agent can write/deploy a Next.js + Stripe + Postgres app in days. Agent can run the Discord/Reddit listening forever. Self-serve pricing means no calls. SaaS multiples (3–5x ARR) make even a $5k MRR product a $200k+ asset.

**Capital:** $300 (Vercel/Supabase free tiers, Stripe, domain, some LLM tokens for the product itself).
**Time-to-first-revenue:** 45–90 days (2 weeks listening, 2 weeks building, 4 weeks distribution).
**12-mo ceiling:** Median outcome $500–$5k MRR. Top decile $20k+ MRR. Most micro-SaaS plateaus low; distribution is the moat.
**What kills it:** Distribution. "If you build it they will come" is a lie. Need a content/community plan from day 1 (which an agent can also run, but it's slower than performance ads).
**Split:** Agent 85% (research, build, support, content), Operator 15% (vertical pick, occasional product calls, legal/Stripe).

## The ONE I'd Start Tomorrow If Forced

**#3 — AI Voice Receptionist for local service businesses.**

Why over the others: it's the only idea that gives you recurring revenue from week 4, doesn't require Cyrus to take an hour-long sales call, has clear unit economics (Retell minutes ~$0.07/min × ~200 mins/mo = $14 COGS on $297 MRR = 95% gross), and uses *existing proven demand* (Wyatt's data shows businesses pay handsomely for AI calling — we just sell it directly instead of bundled).

### Week-1 Plan (7 days, all agent-executed unless noted)

**Day 1 — Positioning & infrastructure (agent solo, ~6h)**
- Pick ONE vertical to start: **HVAC contractors in Texas, Florida, Arizona** (high call volume, missed-call pain, sunbelt has fewer competitors, decent owner-operator culture). Lock it.
- Buy domain (`aftercallai.com`-style), spin up Framer/Carrd one-pager: "Stop losing $50k/yr to missed calls. AI receptionist answers + books in 8 seconds. $297/mo. Setup in 1 day."
- Set up Stripe (Cyrus signs), Calendly for demo, basic CRM in n8n/Airtable.

**Day 2 — Build the receptionist template (agent solo, ~4h)**
- Set up Retell account, build a polished HVAC-optimized voice agent: warm greeting, intake (name/phone/address/issue/urgency), Calendly booking, SMS confirmation, daily call summary email.
- Record a Loom demo of it answering 3 sample calls (you call it from your own phone — agent handles the trigger, Cyrus optional).
- Build the "white-label" version: bring-your-own-Twilio-number, forward client's main line, plug in their Calendly/JobNimbus/Housecall Pro.

**Day 3 — Lead list (agent solo, ~3h)**
- Scrape Google Maps for HVAC contractors in 20 metros across TX/FL/AZ filtered: 4+ stars, 20+ reviews, has website, NOT a national brand. Enrich with owner name + email + LinkedIn via Apollo/Clay. Target: 1,500 leads.

**Day 4 — Outreach sequence launch (agent solo, ~3h)**
- Warm up sending domains (3 inbox addresses, Smartlead, gradual ramp).
- Cold email sequence (3 touches over 9 days): "Subject: missed 22 calls last week" / Body: hyper-personalized line about THEIR business + 30-sec Loom of the demo bot + 1 sentence offer + Calendly link.
- Parallel: LinkedIn DM same prospects (lighter, 1 touch).
- Send to 200/day, ramp to 500/day by day 7.

**Day 5 — Heartbeat: triage replies (agent every 30 min, Cyrus 10 min review at EOD)**
- Agent classifies replies: interested / objection / unsubscribe / hostile. Drafts reply to interested + objection bucket. Cyrus approves with one click.
- Book demos. Goal end of week 1: 3–6 demos on the books for week 2.

**Day 6 — Build pilot offer (agent solo, ~2h)**
- Offer for week-1 closers: **first month free, $497 setup waived, 30-day kill switch**. Goal: get 3 logos on the wall ASAP to use as social proof in week-3 outreach.

**Day 7 — Review & decision point (Cyrus + agent, 30 min)**
- Metrics review: opens, replies, demos booked, ad-account/inbox health.
- If: ≥3 demos booked → run plan as-is. <3 demos booked → switch vertical to dental (higher pain) or expand metros. >10 demos booked → fund 2nd vertical in parallel.

**Cyrus's actual week-1 time commitment: ~3 hours total** (Stripe + LLC signing, 1 review meeting, approve outreach copy, sign off on offer). Demos themselves start in week 2.

**Spend in week 1:** ~$400 (Smartlead, Apollo, Retell, Twilio numbers, domain, Calendly Pro). Plenty of room in the $1–3k budget for week 2–4.

## Considered but Rejected

- **Trading bots / market making / DeFi / arbitrage.** Explicitly excluded by Cyrus.
- **Personal brand / influencer / "build in public" content monetization.** Requires Cyrus on camera consistently — operator-time-bound and slow. Maybe a side benefit, not a strategy.
- **Mobile / iOS apps.** App store gatekeeping + 6+ month payoff curve violates 30–90 day target.
- **Productized accounting/bookkeeping/legal/tax services.** Requires licensed humans, agent can't legally sign returns or be the responsible party.
- **High-ticket coaching / mastermind ("we'll teach you AI agencies").** Works mechanically (Wyatt's whole back-end is this) but requires a real personal brand built first. 6-month flywheel minimum.
- **NFTs / generative art / token launches.** Regulatory, reputational, and category is dead.
- **Selling AI-generated faceless YouTube videos / TikTok channels at scale.** Crowded, monetization-rate collapsing, platform de-prioritizing AI-only content actively.
- **Print-on-demand / Merch by Amazon / KDP low-content books.** Once-good agent plays. Saturation + Amazon policy changes (KDP now bans/flags AI books) make ROI poor in 2025/26.
- **Generic "AI agency" mentorship/info-product to consumers.** Requires personal brand + you compete with Wyatt's exact playbook. Skip.
- **Real estate wholesaling automation.** Possible but legal risk per state + needs phone closer + cash buyer relationships — operator-bound bottlenecks.
- **B2B cold-call boiler-room style outbound for enterprise software.** Enterprise sales cycle (6–18 months) violates revenue target.
- **Buying & flipping micro-SaaS on Acquire/MicroAcquire.** Capital-bound ($30k+ entries) and slow.
- **AI girlfriend / companion apps.** Reputational risk, app-store hostile, payment processor hostile (Stripe blocks these). Hard no.
