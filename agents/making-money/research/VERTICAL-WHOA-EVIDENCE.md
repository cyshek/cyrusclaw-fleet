## The question this answers
The selection doc scored G1 by desk judgment: it *assumed* a real ICP site, when audited, surfaces a visceral, specific, fixable problem the freelancer can lead with. If real ICP sites are mostly already clean, the audit lands as a shrug → G1 low → K leaks. So: **do real sites in each vertical's ICP actually have audit-exposable, "whoa"-grade problems?** Measured, not asserted.

## Method (honest by construction)
- 3 cohorts of 12 **real, public** ICP sites each, curated to be **representative across quality tiers** (polished big brands + small/DIY operators) — NOT cherry-picked broken sites (that would defeat falsifiability). Cohorts are hard-coded in the harness.
- Each site → `auditUrl(url, <profile>)` with the matching profile. Polite: concurrency ≤3, 25s/site guard, per-site try/catch, small delay. **Network failures are recorded as `error` rows and EXCLUDED from all rate denominators.**
- **Whoa bar (defensible):** a site clears it if it has a *specific, lead-worthy* problem the audit can lead with — **grade ≤ C** (a low headline number is itself visceral) **OR ≥1 `fail` on a high-visibility, vertical-relevant lead check** (V1: meta_desc/og/share/title/h1/https/broken/speed; V2: the local_* checks; V3: product/price/review checks).

### Per-vertical reads
- **V1 SiteLens — 50%, but bimodal.** Polished agencies score A (webfx 98, thrive 95, directive 95) — for those the audit is a near-shrug (the optimism risk is real). But the long-tail/mid sites crater hard: lyfemarketing **F/30**, mainstreetroi & coalitiontechnologies **D/54**, straightnorth **C/63**. The lead hook is the **share card** (50% have a broken/blank link preview) — visceral and screenshot-able ("here's what your link looks like when shared: blank"). G1 holds for the *small* half of the ICP, not the polished half.
- **V2 LocalLens — 63%, broadest weakness.** Nobody scored an A; everyone is a B/C with **5.5 warns/site** — local sites are *consistently* mediocre, and the single most common hard fail is **no LocalBusiness schema** (50%), the exact "you're invisible to Google's local data" line that is visceral to a brick-and-mortar owner. Even big chains (aspendental, rotorooter, anytimefitness) sit at B with multiple local-signal gaps. The whoa here is *breadth* (many fixable warns) more than depth.
- **V3 ShopLens — 100%, but READ THE LIMITATION.** Every store fails `product_schema`. That is **partly a keyless-fetch artifact, not pure site brokenness**: modern Shopify/DTC stores inject Product JSON-LD **client-side via JS**, and the engine fetches **raw HTML only** — so it can't see JS-rendered schema. Confirmed on real *product* pages (allbirds/bombas/deathwishcoffee product URLs) — `product_schema` still fails there too. So V3's 100% overstates true brokenness: some of those "failures" would resolve in a JS-rendered crawl. That said, the headline scores are genuinely the lowest (median 65, avg 2.64 fails / 8.27 warns) and several real on-page gaps (img_alt, h1, meta_desc, price-not-in-text) are real — so V3 is still high-whoa, just not a clean 100%.
  - **UPDATE 2026-06-09 (artifact now partially corrected — false-FAIL fix shipped):** the engine now also reads the OTHER raw-HTML product-structured-data formats it was ignoring — **Open Graph product tags** (`og:type=product`, `product:price:amount`), **Microdata** (`itemtype=schema.org/Product`), and **RDFa** (`typeof=Product`). On a re-run of the same real product pages: **`allbirds.com/products/mens-wool-runners`** ships `og:type=product` + `product:price:amount=110.00` server-side → was a **false FAIL**, now an honest **WARN** ("product data present via Open Graph, but no Product JSON-LD — add it"), and its `price` flips text-WARN → structured **PASS**. **`deathwishcoffee.com`** product page genuinely ships only `og:type=website` with no product markup → correctly **still FAIL** (no over-correction). Net: the engine no longer tells an Allbirds-class store "your products are invisible to Google Shopping" (a credibility-killing factual error on the conversion-half of K); the V3 distribution will now contain honest WARNs where it previously had artifact FAILs. Fix + 28 new tests in `src/profiles.js` / `test/profiles.mjs`.

## Verdict vs the doc's G1/G2 ranking

**The data SUPPORTS the V1→V2→V3 queue order — but for a DIFFERENT reason than the doc gives, and it CHALLENGES the doc's implicit "V1 has the most exposable problems" optimism.**

- **G1 (exposable-problem density) does NOT favor V1 — it favors V3 > V2 > V1.** The selection doc scored G1 equally (4) for V1/V2/V3 and treated V1 as the strongest overall. The evidence flips the G1 sub-ranking: **e-com and local sites are measurably more broken than agency sites.** Agency/marketer sites are the *cleanest* of the three (they sell web work, so half of them have polished sites → the audit shrugs on the polished half). This is the optimism-correction this tick exists for: **V1's "whoa" is real only on the small/DIY half of its ICP, not the polished half.**
- **But V1 still rightly leads the QUEUE — because the queue is ranked by G2 (loop overlap), not G1.** The doc's own decisive gate is G2 (the Calendly property), where V1=5 is uniquely high (audit recipients ARE marketers/ICP). This evidence doesn't touch G2 (that's a loop-conversion fact EXP-2 measures, not a site-content fact), so the G2-driven V1→V2→V3 order stands. The honest synthesis: **V1 wins on loop quality (G2), NOT on whoa density (G1); V3 has the most visceral audit but the weakest peer-overlap loop.** That is exactly the doc's §5 self-flagged tension, now with numbers.
- **Actionable nuance for V1 outreach:** since V1's whoa is bimodal, the freelancer should **target the small/DIY half** of the SMB-services ICP (where the audit lands hard), and **lead with the share-card** finding (the most common, most screenshot-able fail) rather than a generic SEO score — on a polished agency site the score-hook is a dud.

## Limitations (stated, not buried)
1. **Small n** (8–12 ok per vertical). These are directional base-rates, not population estimates. Wilson-style bands would be wide.
2. **Public, well-known sites skew CLEANER than the true long-tail ICP.** The real freelancer prospect is smaller/worse than aspendental or allbirds — so the *true* whoa-rates are likely **higher** than measured here (this biases AGAINST whoa, i.e. the measured rates are conservative floors). The one exception is V3 where the keyless artifact biases the rate UP.
3. **Keyless raw-HTML fetch ≠ full signal.** It cannot see JS-rendered content (the V3 `product_schema` caveat above — **now partially mitigated**: as of 2026-06-09 the engine reads OG-product / Microdata / RDFa product markup from raw HTML, so a store with server-side product signals is an honest WARN, not a false FAIL; only a true JS-only-JSON-LD store with zero raw-HTML product markup is still under-credited), runs no live-perf/PSI ring (Cyrus-gated key absent), and samples (not exhaustively crawls) broken links. The HTML/SEO/structured-data checks it *does* run are 100% real and keyless.
4. **Homepage-not-product for V3:** the cohort audited store homepages (what a freelancer pastes first); ShopLens scores a product page best. Re-running on product URLs (done as a spot-check) gave similar grades, so the direction holds, but the absolute V3 numbers would shift on a product-page cohort.
5. **5 cohort sites failed to fetch** (golds.gym, kjt-law, thesmilestylist.ca, olivegarden, mvmt — DNS/abort/timeout from this datacenter IP); excluded from rates. Not a measurement bias, just reach loss.
