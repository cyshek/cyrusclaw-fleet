                  Spokane, WA Divorce FAQs

                  • What is the non-emergency contact number for the local police station in Spokane, Washington?

                    The non-emergency contact number for the local police station in Spokane, Washington is 509-456-2233.

                  • Where can I get a copy of police reports for incidents related to my case in Spokane, Washington?

                    You can obtain a copy of your police report online by clicking the Public Records Request Center link https://publicrecords-spokanewa.govqa.us/WEBAPP/_rs/supporthome.aspx, or you can visit the Police Records Unit at 1100 W. Mallon Ave. Spokane, WA 99260.

                  • What happens to our family car after a divorce has been granted in Spokane?

                    The court will determine if the family car was given as a gift or inheritance from a family member before the marriage. If it was acquired during the marriage, the judge must know who uses it for work and other trips necessary for the family's upkeep. It will also depend on the agreement between parties during property division in Spokane, especially if the car has sentimental value to the husband or wife.

                  • What factors will a court in Spokane consider when deciding child custody during divorce proceedings?

                    Generally, a court in Spokane will determine a child's overall relationship with each parent and other siblings. Another deciding factor would be the capability of a parent to take care of the child, whether work schedules would affect the time they can spend caring for the kid. Finally, the court would give weight to the child's wish if the child is mature enough to make decisions.

                  • What happens if your spouse physically threatens you and your children before the divorce is finalized in Spokane?

                    If you and your children are victims of domestic violence in Spokane, you can ask for a Domestic Violence Protection Order from the authorities. The court can stop your spouse from visiting you and your children at home, school, or the workplace. Your spouse will be required to undergo evaluation or treatment if there is evidence of drug usage.

                  • Is it possible to get a divorce in Spokane even if neither party committed any wrongdoing?

                    Yes, you can file for divorce in Spokane even if infidelity is not present. Since Washington is a "no-fault" state in divorce cases, you do not have to find fault other than you both have irreconcilable differences. However, when a party disagrees that there are irreconcilable differences in the marriage, the case will be transferred to a family court, letting the couple go through counseling.

                  • How soon can a divorce decree be issued in Spokane?

                    A divorce will require at least 90 days to be granted in Spokane. The state of Washington observes a mandatory 90-day waiting period, also known as the cooling-off period. The court would want couples to have more time to try and work things out before officially ending their marriage.
