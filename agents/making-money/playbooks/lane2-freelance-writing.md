## The positioning that beats the AI-slop problem

The freelance-writing market is flooded with $0.01/word AI content-mill garbage. We do **not** compete there — that's a trap and a race to zero. We win on the axis solo humans can't: **research-heavy, niche-expert, fact-dense deliverables that read like a knowledgeable human wrote them.**

**Target rate band:** $0.10–$0.50/word, or $75–$250 per piece, or $400–$1,500/mo retainers. Below $0.08/word = decline, it's not worth the cycle.

**Niche tilt (pick ONE to lead, Day 1):** the more technical/specialized, the less AI-commodity pressure and the higher the rate.
- **B2B SaaS / dev-tools content** (Cyrus runs OpenClaw + multi-agent infra — genuine domain credibility here; this is the strongest pick).
- **Fintech / crypto-explainer / trading content** (trading-bench gives real subject-matter grounding; note Cyrus's crypto-adjacency caution — keep to education, not shilling).
- **Technical docs / API docs / developer onboarding guides** (highest bar, lowest competition, premium rates).

Lead recommendation: **B2B SaaS / dev-tools.** It's where Cyrus has the most authentic credibility, the rates are good, and "AI wrote it" is least disqualifying because the value is in accuracy + structure, not voice.

## Where to sell (in priority order)

1. **Upwork** — biggest pool, escrow protects against scams, reputation compounds. Downside: race-to-bottom at the low end (we ignore that segment), takes a few proposals to land #1. **Lead here.**
2. **Contra / contently / Paved (newsletter sponsorships)** — higher quality, slower.
3. **Direct outreach to niche blogs / SaaS marketing leads** — highest rate, no platform cut, but cold-outreach-gated (overlaps lane #1 mechanics).
4. **Content agencies as a subcontractor** (e.g. Verblio, Scripted, ClearVoice) — steady volume, lower rate, but they bring the clients. Good as a baseload while direct pipeline builds.

Realistic mix: Upwork for first reputation + cash, agencies for baseload volume, direct for the premium ceiling.

## 7-Day execution plan ($0–$25)

- **Day 1 — Niche + offer + sample (agent-led).** Agent locks ONE niche (default: B2B SaaS/dev-tools), writes the positioning one-liner, and produces **2 spec-sample pieces** (e.g. one 1,200-word SEO article + one technical how-to) as portfolio proof. These are the single most important asset — they ARE the credibility. *Human: 5 min to approve niche.*
- **Day 2 — Portfolio + profile (agent-drafts, human-owns).** Agent writes the Upwork/Contra profile copy, headline, and 3 portfolio entries built around the Day-1 samples. *Human MUST: create/verify the Upwork account (KYC) + connect Stripe/bank for payout. This is the unavoidable identity step.*
- **Day 3 — Proposal engine (agent-led).** Agent builds a proposal template system: a tight, personalized, no-fluff proposal that references the client's actual product/post (not a copy-paste). Plus a screening checklist to filter scam/overpayment/off-platform-payment jobs.
- **Day 4 — First proposal batch (agent-drafts, human-sends).** Agent finds 15–20 well-fit job posts in the niche, drafts a custom proposal for each. *Human approves + submits* (or approves agent to submit from his account). Volume matters early — reply rate is low until reputation exists.
- **Day 5 — Land + scope first gig (agent + human).** Triage replies, agent drafts clarifying questions, human takes any intro call. Lock scope in writing + keep ALL money in Upwork escrow.
- **Day 6 — Deliver (agent does the work).** Agent researches + drafts + edits the deliverable to a genuinely-human, fact-checked standard. Human does final read-through before it goes out under his name.
- **Day 7 — Revise + ask for the 5-star + pitch the retainer.** Agent handles revisions. Human relays + asks: "Glad it worked — want me on a monthly basis for X pieces?" Converting one-off → retainer is what turns this from hourly into a baseload.

**Week-1 realistic outcome:** $0–$200 in escrow, a live profile with 2 strong samples, 15–20 proposals out, ideally 1 gig landed/in-progress. **First paid dollar: week 1–2** (faster than lane #1). Spend: $0–$25.

## Scam-avoidance hardline (non-negotiable)

The category is infested with *fake-client* scams. Auto-decline any job that:
- Asks to move payment **off-platform** (gift cards, Zelle, crypto, wire, PayPal F&F).
- Sends an **overpayment** then asks for a partial refund.
- Wants you to "buy software/equipment" to start.
- Asks for a free "test" of 1,000+ words (a paid micro-test is fine; a huge free one is IP theft).
- Has a brand-new client account with no payment-verified badge for a high-value job.

Rule: **all money stays in Upwork escrow until reputation is built.** Never go off-platform for a stranger.
