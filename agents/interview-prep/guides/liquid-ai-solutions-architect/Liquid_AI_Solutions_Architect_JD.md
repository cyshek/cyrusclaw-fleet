## ABOUT LIQUID AI

Spun out of MIT CSAIL, Liquid AI builds general-purpose AI systems (Liquid Foundation Models / LFMs)
that run efficiently across deployment targets — from data center accelerators to on-device hardware —
ensuring low latency, minimal memory usage, privacy, and reliability. They partner with enterprises
across consumer electronics, automotive, life sciences, and financial services. Unicorn-stage,
scaling rapidly.

## THE OPPORTUNITY

Liquid AI is building a solutions architecture function from scratch. You'd be one of the first SAs,
working directly with the Head of Solutions Architecture and across GTM to own customer engagements
end-to-end. Their models are purpose-built for environments where memory, latency, and power are
binding constraints — edge devices, mobile, embedded systems, on-prem infra where frontier models
can't run. Customers range from AI-native companies to enterprises exploring AI for the first time.

## WHAT WE'RE LOOKING FOR

- **Technical builder:** download a model, build a demo, present it to a customer. As comfortable in a
  Jupyter notebook as in a boardroom.
- **Creative problem solver:** see opportunities where customers see limitations; show an enterprise
  why a small efficient model changes their cost structure or enables something new.
- **End-to-end owner:** no line between pre-sales and post-sales — own the outcome from first call to
  go-live and beyond.
- **Org builder:** build a function, not inherit one — create playbooks, demo libraries, engagement
  processes that scale.
- **Imagination-gap closer:** reframe what's possible on hardware customers already own.

## THE WORK

- Own customer engagements end-to-end: qualified opportunity → technical validation → go-live →
  ongoing delivery across all segments.
- Build customer-specific demos and POCs using Liquid models (including LEAP for fine-tuning, domain
  adaptation, and evaluation) to drive technical wins.
- Lead technical discovery: map customer architectures to Liquid solutions, drive competitive
  positioning vs. open-source and incumbent models, quantify ROI.
- Co-own the product-field feedback loop: document friction, eval failures, capability gaps; partner
  with product and research to influence roadmap.
- Turn engagement learnings into reusable assets: reference architectures, solution primitives, demo
  building blocks, engagement playbooks, vertical solution patterns.

## DESIRED EXPERIENCE

**Must-have:**
- Applied ML skills: hands-on with ML models in customer-facing contexts (demos, prototypes, or
  production integrations).
- Pre-sales AND post-sales experience: owned technical customer engagements end-to-end, not just the
  pitch.
- Strong customer-facing communication: run discovery, build relationships with technical and business
  buyers, present to executives.
- Understanding of AI architectures and deployment tradeoffs: token efficiency, on-device vs. cloud,
  model size vs. latency, open-weight vs. proprietary.

**Nice-to-have:**
- Familiarity with small / efficient model deployment (edge, on-device, latency-constrained).
- Thought leadership / technical blogs / industry talks.
- Efficient-model deployment: quantization (INT4/INT8, GGUF, AWQ), serving frameworks (vLLM,
  TensorRT-LLM, llama.cpp), hardware-aware optimization.
- Designing and debugging model evaluations — why benchmark results diverge from production.

## WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE (YEAR ONE)

2. A library of scalable demos, playbooks, and customer-facing collateral exists and is used.
3. A structured feedback loop from customer conversations to product/model teams influences roadmap.
